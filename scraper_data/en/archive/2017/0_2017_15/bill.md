EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Commercial Vehicle Licensing Board Act 1987
(“Act 334”).

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and the provision on the commencement_
of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to introduce new definitions of “e-hailing vehicle”,_
“intermediation business” and “intermediation business licence” and to amend
certain existing definitions.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 10 of Act 334 in consequence of the_
introduction of a new Part IVa to regulate the licensing of intermediation
business.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to insert a new subsection (1b) into section 14 of Act 334_
to provide that an e-hailing vehicle shall be a public service vehicle subject
to an intermediation business licence issued under Part IVa.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to amend section 26 of Act 334 in consequence of the_
introduction of a new Part IVa to regulate the licensing of intermediation
business.

7. _Clause 6 seeks to introduce a new Part IVa into Act 334 to regulate the_
licensing of intermediation business.

The proposed new section 26a provides for the licensing of intermediation

business, including the application, conditions that may be attached to an
intermediation business licence issued by the Board and the offence of operating
an intermediation business without a licence.

The proposed new section 26b provides for the renewal of an intermediation

business licence including the application and conditions for the renewal of
such licence within the period and in the manner as determined by the Board.


-----

_Commercial Vehicles Licensing Board (Amendment)_ 17

The proposed new section 26c empowers the Board to grant or refuse the

application by the holder of an intermediation business licence to vary the
licence or the conditions of the licence.

The proposed new section 26d provides for the circumstances in which the

Board may revoke or suspend an intermediation business licence.

The proposed new section 26e provides that the intermediation business

licence shall not be transferred or assigned to any other person. The proposed
section 26e also provides that the Board may authorize the transfer or assignment
of the intermediation business licence to any other person.

The proposed new section 26f requires the holder of an intermediation

business licence to keep accounts and records in accordance with the rules
made by Board.

The proposed new section 26g provides for the duty of the holder of

an intermediation business licence to inform the Board of any proceedings
or claims against the holder of an intermediation business licence which
might have an adverse effect on the financial condition of the holder of an
intermediation business licence and affecting the holder of an intermediation
business licence’s ability to perform any conditions under the licence.

8. _Clauses 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12_ and _13 seek to amend sections 27, 30, 31,_
33, 36, 37 and 42 of Act 334 respectively in consequence of the introduction
of a new Part IVa into Act 334 to regulate the licensing of intermediation
business.

9. _Clause 14 seeks to introduce a new section 43a to stipulate the liability_
of officers of a company where a body corporate commits an offence under
Act 334.

10. _Clauses 15, 16, 17, 18 and_ _19 seek to amend sections 44b, 46, 47, 52_

and 56 of Act 334 respectively in consequence of the introduction of a new
Part IVa to regulate the licensing of intermediation business.

11. _Clause 20 seeks to provide for savings and transitional provisions._

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

The Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
