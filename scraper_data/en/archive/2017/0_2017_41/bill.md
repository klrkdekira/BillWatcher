**ENACTED** by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:

**Short title**

**1. This Act may be cited as the Malaysian Maritime Enforcement**
Agency (Amendment) Act 2017.

**New sections** **5a, 5b, 5c and** **5d**

**2.** The Malaysian Maritime Enforcement Agency Act 2004

[Act 633], which is referred to as the “principal Act” in this Act,
is amended by inserting after section 5 the following sections:

“Establishment of Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps

**5a. There is established a body to be known as the Malaysian**
Maritime Volunteer Corps.

**Members of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps**

**5b. (1) Any person may apply to be a member of the**
Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps if—

_(a) he is eighteen years of age or above;_

_(d) he fulfils other requirements as may be determined_

(2) An application to be a member of the Malaysian

(5) A member of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps

(6) A member of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps—

_(a) may be paid such allowances as may be determined_

by the Minister after consultation with the Minister
of Finance; and

_(b) shall not be entitled to any remuneration._

(7) A member of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps
while performing any duty under this Act shall be deemed
to be a public servant within the meaning of the Penal Code

[Act 574].

**Establishment of Malaysian Maritime Cadet Corps**

**5c. The Minister may establish a body to be known as the**
Malaysian Maritime Cadet Corps for any area in Malaysia.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Malaysian Maritime Enforcement Agency
Act 2004 (“Act 633”).

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title of the proposed Act._

3. Clause 2 seeks to introduce new sections 5a, 5b, 5c and 5d into
Act 633.

The proposed new section 5a seeks to provide for the establishment of the

Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps.

The proposed new section 5b seeks to provide for person who may become

a member of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps. The new section 5b
also seeks to provide that a member of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer
Corps may be called to perform any duty and such member shall be under the
direction, command and control of the Director General. The new section 5b
further provides that a member of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps
while performing any duty under Act 633 shall be deemed as a public servant.

The proposed new section 5c seeks to provide for the establishment of the

Malaysian Maritime Cadet Corps and the proposed new section 5d provides
for person who may be enrolled into the Malaysian Maritime Cadet Corps.

4. _Clause 3_ seeks to introduce new section 7a into Act 633 to provide that
an officer of the Agency shall have all the powers of a police officer of the
rank of Corporal and below and the powers of a prison officer of the rank
of Sergeant and below under the Prison Act 1995 [Act 537] when escorting
and guarding any person in custody of the Agency. Paragraph 7a(2)(b) seeks
to deem the office of the Agency to be a police station for the purpose of
Act 633.

5. Clause 4 seeks to amend section 10 of Act 633 to provide for the protection
of any member of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps from being taken
of any action that is done or omitted to be done by him in good faith in the
execution of his duties under Act 633.

6. _Clause 5_ seeks to amend section 11 of Act 633 to provide that members
of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer Corps shall on demand, declare his office
and produce authority card issued to him in the execution of his duties under
Act 633.

7. Clause 6 seeks to amend section 18 of Act 633 to empower the Director
General to issue Standing Orders relating to the general control, training,
duties and responsibilities of the members of the Malaysian Maritime Volunteer
Corps.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will involve the Government in extra financial expenditure the amount
of which cannot at present be ascertained.
