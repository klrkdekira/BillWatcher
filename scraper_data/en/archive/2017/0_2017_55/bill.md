EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Tourism Industry Act 1992 (“Act 482”).

2. Clause 1 contains the short title and the provision on the commencement
of the proposed Act.


-----

6 _Bill_

3. _Clause 2_ seeks to amend the definition of “accommodation premises” to
expand the definition to cover permanent or temporary structure.

4. Clause 3 seeks to amend subsection 5(3) of Act 482 by increasing the
penalty from the existing fine of not exceeding fifty thousand ringgit or to
imprisonment for a term not exceeding five years to a fine of not exceeding
five hundred thousand ringgit or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding
ten years.

5. Clause 4 seeks to amend section 8 of Act 482 to provide the procedure
for suspension of any licence granted under Act 482. Under the proposed
amendment, the Commissioner has a discretion either to suspend or to revoke
any licence granted to a licensed tourism enterprise under Act 482. The proposed
amendment further seeks to simplify the mode of advertising of a suspension
or revocation of any licence granted under Act 482. With the proposed
amendment, the Commissioner has an option to advertise the suspension or
revocation of licence either in at least one national daily Bahasa Malaysia and
English newspaper or any electronic media that the Commissioner deems fit.

6. Clause 5 seeks to amend subsection 9(1) of Act 482 to allow any licensed
tourism enterprise to appeal to the Minister against the suspension of their
licence by the Commissioner.

7. Clause 6 seeks to amend section 10 of Act 482 by deleting subsection 10(3)
to waive a licensed tourism enterprise’s obligation to advertise the surrender
of its licence.

8. Clause 7 seeks to amend section 11 of Act 482 by inserting the effect
of a suspension of licence under section 8 of Act 482. Under the proposed
amendment, a licensed tourism enterprise whose license has been suspended
is not allowed to transact any new business during the suspension period. The
amendment further seeks to increase the penalty from the existing fine of not
exceeding fifty thousand ringgit or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding
five years to a fine of not exceeding five hundred thousand ringgit or to
imprisonment for a term not exceeding ten years.

9. Clause 8 seeks to amend subsection 15(3) of Act 482 by increasing the
penalty from the existing fine of not exceeding twenty thousand ringgit to
a fine of not exceeding fifty thousand ringgit.

10. _Clause 9 seeks to amend section 32 of Act 482 by deleting subsection 32(3)_

to waive a licensed tourism enterprise’s obligation to advertise its intention
not to renew its license. The proposed amendment further seeks to make any
contravention of subsection 32(1) an offence.

11. Clause 10 seeks to insert new subsection 34(3) in Act 482 to empower the

Minister to prescribe the penalty for the contravention of or failure to comply
with any provision of any regulations made under Act 482. The penalty that
may be prescribed is a fine not exceeding one hundred thousand ringgit or
imprisonment for a term not exceeding five years or to both.

12. _Clause 11 seeks to amend section 37 of Act 482 by increasing the penalty_

from the existing fine of not exceeding five thousand ringgit to a fine of not
exceeding fifty thousand ringgit.

13. _Clause 12 seeks to amend subsection 39(1) of Act 482 to provide that_

the written consent of the Public Prosecutor must be obtained before any
offence can be compounded by the Commissioner.

14. _Clause 13 seeks to amend section 49 of Act 482 to provide that no_

prosecution in respect of any offence under Act 482 shall be instituted except
by, or with the written consent of, the Public Prosecutor.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
