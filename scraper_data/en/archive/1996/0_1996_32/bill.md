EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Employees' Social Security Act 1969
("the Act").

2. Clause 3 seeks to introduce a new proviso to section 31 of the
Act. The new proviso allows insured persons who sustain employment
injury due to motor vehicle accidents or their dependants to claim
compensation or damages from the insured persons' employer or a
servant of the insured persons' employer who is required to be
insured against Third Party risks under Part IV of the Road Transport
Act 1987.

3. _Clause 4 seeks to delete section 42 of the Act. With this deletion_
any person who is entitled to receive benefits under this Act will
be able to receive any similar benefits admissible under the provisions
of any other written law.

4. Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 57A. The new section
57A enables the Organisation to provide for education benefit in the
form of loans or scholarships to a dependant child of an insured
person.

5. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 59 of the Act by adding a new_
subsection (2A). The new subsection (2A) empowers the Minister to
appoint one of the Deputy Directors General to perform the duties
of the Director General in the absence of the Director General.

6. _Clause 7 seeks to amend section 59B of the Act to specifically_
name the Board as the Social Security Organisation Board and to
enlarge the composition of the Board by introducing as members one
officer from the Ministry of Human Resources, one officer from the
Ministry of Health and three persons with experience in social security
matters to be appointed by the Minister. The Director General will
no longer be the Deputy Chairman. Clause 7 also seeks to introduce
new subsections (8), (9) and (10). Subsection (8) provides for the
appointment of alternate members for members who represent
employers and insured persons. Subsection (9) provides that when
attending meetings of the Board, an alternate member shall be deemed
to be a member of the Board. Subsection (10) provides that an
alternate member, unless he sooner resigns membership or his
appointment is sooner revoked, ceases to be an alternate member
when the member in respect of whom he is an alternate member
ceases to be a member of the Board.

7. _Clause 8 seeks to amend section 59c of the Act. With the_
proposed amendment the Minister is required to appoint any person
to carry out the duties of the Chairman in the absence of the Chairman.

8. _Clause 10 seeks to amend section 71 of the Act by adding a new_
paragraph (viiia). The new paragraph (viiia) enables the Fund to be
used for the purpose of granting loans or scholarships to a dependant
child of an insured person as specified under the new section 57A.

9. _Clause 12 seeks to delete section 75B of the Act as it is no longer_
applicable in view of the proposed amendments to sections 59B and
59c.

10. _Clauses 13 and 14 seek to amend sections 93 and 94 of the_
Act to increase the penalty provided in those sections.

11. _Clause 15 seeks to amend section 105 of the Act by adding_
a new paragraph (xxxia). The new paragraph (xxxia) enables regulations
to be made for the imposition of a fee or a charge for providing any
information or document which is in the custody of the Social Security
Organisation.

12. Other amendments which are not specifically mentioned are
consequential or minor in nature.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 1852.]
