24 _Bill_

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to provide for offences against the precepts of the
religion of Islam by persons professing the religion in the Federal
Territories, Several of the offences included in this Bill are presently

provided for in Part IX of the Administration of Muslim Law
Enactment 1952 of the State of Selangor as modified and extended
to the Federal Territories (hereinafter referred to as the "1952
Enactment"). However, such provisions have been revised and
improved, including imposing heavier penalties to reflect the gravity
of the offences.

2. Part I deals with preliminary matters. Subclause 1(2) clearly

provides that the Act proposed by this Bill applies only to persons
professing the religion of Islam.

3. Part II contains offences relating to 'aqidah. Clause 3 makes
it an offence for any person to worship nature or does any act
which shows worship or reverence of any person, animal, place or
thing in any manner contrary to Islamic Law. Clause 4 is an offence
on deviationist teachings and practices. Other offences provided
under this Part are the offences of propagating religious doctrines
other than that of the religion of Islam among persons professing
the Islamic faith (clause 5) and the offence of declaring onself or

another person a prophet, Imam Mahadi or wall (clause 6).

4. Part III contains offences relating to the sanctity of the religion

of Islam. Among the offences provided for in this Part which are
not provided for in the 1952 Enactment are the offence of insulting
or bringing into contempt the religion of Islam (clause 7), the offence
of defying a Court order (clause 10) and the offence of gambling
_(clause 18). Offences provided for in the 1952 Enactment and which_
provisions have been improved upon are the offence of deriding
Quranic verses or Hadith (clause 8), the offence of acting in contempt
or defiance of religious authorities (clause 9), the offence of teaching
religion without tauliah (clause 11), the offence of giving, propagating
or disseminating opinion contrary to fatwa (clause 12), the offence

of printing, producing, etc. religious publication contrary to Islamic
Law (clause 13), the offence of failing to perform Friday prayers
_(clause 14), the offence of being disrespectful of Ramadhan (clause_
_15), the offence of non-payment of zakat or fitrah (clause 16), the_
offence of instigating neglect of religious duty (clause 17) and the
offence of consuming intoxicating drinks (clause 19).

5. Part IV contains offences relating to decency. Apart from the

offences of sexual intercourse out of wedlock (clause 23) and khalwat
_(clause 27), all other offences under this Part are not provided for_
under the 1952 Enactment. Such offences are the offence of incest
_(clause 20), the offence of doing an act preparatory to sexual_
intercourse out of wedlock (clause 24), the offence of liwat (clause

6. Part V contains miscellaneous offences. The offences provided

for use in judicial proceedings (clause 30), the offence of regarding

or fitrah (clause 34) are existing provisions in the 1952 Enactment.

7. Part VI contains provisions on abetment and attempt which are
more detailed than those provided for in the 1952 Enactment.

8. Part VII contains general exceptions. Among the general

Majlis to appoint rehabilitation centres or approved homes. Clauses
_55 and 56 empower the Court to commit persons convicted of certain_
offences to an approved rehabilitation centre or approved home.
_Clause 57 provides for the cessation of application of Part IX,_
except sections 164 and 165, of the 1952 Enactment to the Federal
Territories. Clause 58 seeks to modify the provision of section 164
