_i n t i t u l e d_

An Act to amend the Labuan Offshore Business Activity Tax Act
1990.

**[** **]**

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:**

**Short title and commencement**

**1.** (1) This Act may be cited as the Labuan Offshore Business
Activity Tax (Amendment) Act 2009.

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed by the
Minister by notification in the Gazette.

**Amendment of long title**

2. The Labuan Offshore Business Activity Tax Act 1990 [Act 445],
which is referred to as the "principal Act" in this Act, is amended in
the long title by substituting for the words "offshore business activity
carried on by an offshore company" the words "a Labuan business
activity carried on by a Labuan entity".

**Amendment of section 1**

3. (1) Subsection 1(1) of the principal Act is amended by deleting the
word "Offshore".

Act, a reference is made in any written law to the Labuan Offshore
Business Activity Tax Act 1990, such reference shall be construed as
a reference to the Labuan Business Activity Tax Act 1990.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Labuan Offshore Business Activity Tax
1990 ("Act 445"). The amendment is proposed arising out of the
rebranding of Labuan as a new Labuan International Financial Centre and
to ensure consistency with other legislations relating to Labuan's
financial services.


-----

_2._ _Clause 1_ contains the short title and the power of the Minister to
appoint the commencement date of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2_ seeks to amend the long title of Act 445. The proposed
amendment seeks to provide a more comprehensive scope of Labuan's
business activities.

4. Consequential to the rebranding of Labuan as a new Labuan
International Financial Centre, a change of name of Act 445 is proposed
in clause 3 with the deletion of the word "Offshore".

5. _Clause 4_ seeks to amend section 2 of Act 445. The proposed
amendment substitutes for the word "offshore" appearing in all
definitions the word "Labuan". The amendment also introduces several
new definitions, among which are "Labuan business activity" and
"Labuan entity".

6. _Clause 5 seeks to introduce new sections 2A and 2B into Act 445._

Under the new section 2A, the Minister is able to designate any
activity, other than those falling under the category of Labuan trading
and Labuan non-trading activity, carried on by any Labuan entity to
be a Labuan business activity.

Labuan entities are listed in the Schedule as provided for in the new
section 2B. The Minister is given the power to amend the Schedule by
order published in the _Gazette_ upon the recommendation of the
Director General including able to declare other Labuan entities. The
Schedule is introduced in clause 29.

These provisions will facilitate the development of Labuan as an
international financial centre and ensure that it remains current.

7. The amendments made in _clauses 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14,_
_15, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20 and 27 to sections 3, 3A, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 8A, 9, 10,_
11, 12, 13, 15, 16 and 26 of Act 445 respectively are consequential to the
rebranding and renaming of Labuan's financial services and Labuan's
business activities.

8. _Clause 21 seeks to introduce new sections 17A and 17B into Act 445._

The new section 17A empowers the Director General of Inland
Revenue to issue guidelines in order to clarify the provisions of Act
445 and to facilitate compliance with the law.

With the proposed new section 17B, any person may request for an
advance ruling from the Director General of Inland Revenue on the
application of any provision of Act 445 to a particular type of
arrangement. Subject to certain qualifications, a ruling issued under
this proposed section is binding on the person who requested for such
ruling and on the Director General of Inland Revenue.


-----

9. _Clauses 22, 23, 25 and 26 seek to amend sections 20, 22, 23 and 25_
of Act 445 respectively.

For the offence of disclosing any information relating to any return of
profits or statutory declaration by any officials under section 20 of Act
445, the penalty is increased from five thousand ringgit to one million
ringgit.

For the offence of failure to comply with a notice to furnish
information under section 22 of Act 445, the penalty is increased from
three thousand ringgit to one million ringgit.

_Clause 23_ also provides power to the Director General to call for
information from any person as he may require and for the purpose of
compliance with any double taxation arrangements entered into by the
Government of Malaysia.

For the offence of failure to file a statutory declaration and return of
profits under section 23 of Act 445, the penalty is increased from five
thousand ringgit to one milliion ringgit.

For the offence of making any incorrect or false statutory declaration
under section 25 of Act 445, the penalty is increased from ten thousand
ringgit to one million ringgit.

10. _Clause 24_ seeks to introduce a new section 22A into Act 445. The
proposed new section 22A allows the disclosure of any information on a
double taxation arrangement to any authorised servant or agent of the
Government with whom the Government of Malaysia has made such
arrangement and upon a request from a tax authority of any government
of any territory outside Malaysia.

11. The new section 27 introduced into Act 445 in clause 28 deals with
the method of service of documents required to be served by the
Authority to any Labuan entity other than a notice of demand which is
dealt with in section 13 of Act 445.

12. The new section 28 introduced into Act 445 in clause 28 seeks to
provide for the power of the Director General to give directions if there is
no form prescribed or procedure laid down for any act or procedure under
Act 445.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure.
