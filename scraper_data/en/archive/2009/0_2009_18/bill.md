EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Energy Commission Act 2001 (“Act 610”)
primarily to separate the functions of the Chairman of the Commission
and the Chief Executive Officer. Currently, the functions of the Chairman
of the Commission and the Chief Executive Officer are carried out by the
same person. The amendments in this Bill will limit the role of the Chief
Executive Officer to the administration and management of the functions of
the Commission and its daily affairs.

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and provision on the commencement of_
the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend the definitions of “Chief Executive Officer” and_
“Minister” respectively in section 2 of Act 610.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 5 of Act 610 to increase the number_
of members of the Commission to include the Chief Executive Officer,
three members representing the Government and not more than six other
members.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 7 of Act 610 to set out the tenure of office_
of the members of the Commission, other than the Chief Executive Officer,
and to limit the term of reappointment of the members of the Commission.

6. _Clause 5 is a consequential amendment._

7. _Clause 6 is a consequential amendment._

8. _Clause 7 seeks to introduce a new section 17a into Act 610 to give the_
Commission the exclusive right to the use of the symbol or representation
selected or devised by the Commission. It also seeks to prohibit the misuse
of such symbol or representation.

9. _Clause 8_ seeks to amend section 18 of Act 610 to provide for the
appointment of the Chief Executive Officer by the Minister on such terms
and conditions as he considers desirable.

10. _Clause 9 seeks to introduce a new section 18a into Act 610 to empower_

the Minister to appoint temporarily any officer of the Commission to exercise
the functions of the Chief Executive Officer when the office of the Chief
Executive Officer is vacant.

11. _Clause 10 seeks to amend section 21 of Act 610 to enable the Commission_

to grant scholarships to its officers.

12. _Clause 11 seeks to amend section 25 of Act 610 to enable the Commission_

to charge the loans, scholarships and advances on the Fund.

13. _Clause 12 seeks to introduce a new section 30a into Act 610 to impose_

a duty on the Commission to seek the approval of the Minister and the
concurrence of the Minister of Finance before it enters into a contract under
which the Commission is to pay or receive an amount exceeding ten million
ringgit.

14. _Clause 13 seeks to amend section 33 of Act 610 to impose a duty on_

the Commission to keep proper accounts of the Fund and proper reports of its
activities. The Commission is required to have its statement of accounts and
a statement of its activities for the financial year to be sent to the Minister
who will then cause them to be laid before both Houses of Parliament. This
_clause also provides that the Statutory Bodies (Accounts and Annual Reports)_
Act 1980 shall apply to the Commission.

15. _Clause 14 seeks to delete section 34 of Act 610 in consequence of the_

application of the Statutory Bodies (Accounts and Annual Reports) Act 1980
to the Commission.

16. _Clause 15 provides for a saving provision in respect of any action taken_

or direction given by the Chairman acting as the Chief Executive Officer prior
to the commencement of this Act.
