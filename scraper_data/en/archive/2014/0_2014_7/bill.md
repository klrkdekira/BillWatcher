_i n t i t u l e d_

An Act to amend the Companies Commission of Malaysia
Act 2001.

[ ]

**ENACTED** by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:

**Short title and commencement**

**1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Companies Commission of**
Malaysia (Amendment) Act 2014.

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed by
the Minister by notification in the _Gazette_ and the Minister may
appoint different dates for the coming into operation of different
parts or different provisions of this Act.

**Amendment of section 2**

**2. The Companies Commission of Malaysia Act 2001 [Act 614],**
which is referred to as the “principal Act” in this Act, is amended
in section 2—

_(a) by inserting after the definition of “member” the following_

definitions:

‘ “business entity” means—

_(a) in the case of the Registration of Businesses_

Act 1956 [Act 197], any sole proprietorship or
partnership;

LICENSING

**20g. (1) An application for grant or renewal of licence shall**

**Revocation of licence**

**20i. (1) The Commission shall have the power to revoke**

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Companies Commission of Malaysia Act 2001
(“Act 614”).

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and seeks to allow the Minister to appoint_
the date of commencement of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2_ seeks to introduce certain new definitions and to amend certain
existing definitions of terms used in Act 614.

4. _Clause 3_ seeks to substitute section 6 of Act 614. The new section 6
contains provisions on the membership of the Commission. With this proposed
amendment, the members will consist of the persons from various fields and
who have wide experience or knowledge in company and commercial matters.
This clause also seeks to introduce the temporary exercise of functions of the
Chairman when the office of the Chairman is vacant or in his absence.

5. _Clause 5 seeks to amend section 11 of Act 614 to provide for a better_
provision on the appointments of the Chief Executive Officer and the Deputies
Chief Executive Officer by the Minister and to introduce the temporary
exercise of functions of the Chief Executive Officer when the office of the
Chief Executive Officer is vacant or in his absence.

6. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 17 of Act 614 to extend the functions_
of the Commission in relation to corporate responsibility and business
sustainability.

7. _Clause 7 seeks to amend section 18 of Act 614 to provide for the power_
of the Commission to formulate training programmes, to grant and renew
licenses for the purposes of giving effect to the laws under the jurisdiction
of the Commission.

8. _Clause 8 seeks to amend section 20 of Act 614 to allow the Commission_
to delegate any of its functions and powers to a committee established under
section 19 of Act 614, any member of the Commission, the Chief Executive
Officer, any employee of the Commission or to the Registrar.

9. _Clause 9_ seeks to introduce new Parts IIIa, Part IIIb and Part IIIc into
Act 614. The new Part IIIa contains sections 20a and 20b and deals with
the functions and powers of the Registrar, Deputy Registrars and Assistant
Registrars. The proposed new section 20a provides that the Chief Executive

Officer of the Commission shall be the Registrar for the purposes of the laws
specified in the First Schedule of Act 614. The new section 20b provides for
the Court to take judicial notice of the seal and signature of the Registrar,
Deputy Registrars and Assistant Registrars.

The proposed new Part IIIb contains sections 20c, 20d and 20e. The proposed

new section 20c empowers the Commission to issue guidelines, practice notes,
notices or circulars or in respect of any provision under this Act or the laws
specified in the First Schedule.

The new section 20d seeks to empower the Commission or the Registrar

to publish information. The new section 20e empowers the Commission or
Registrar to take action, other than prosecution, against a person who fails
to comply with Act 614 or the laws specified in the First Schedule of Act
614, guidelines, practice notes, notices, circulars, conditions or restrictions
imposed.

The proposed new Part IIIc contains the provisions on licensing.

10. _Clause 10 seeks to amend section 25 of Act 614 to extend the protection_
against personal liability to the Registrar and a public officer.

11. _Clause 11 seeks to introduce new Part IVa into Act 614 which contains_
the provisions on investigation and enforcement. The Registrar shall have
all the powers of a police officer of whatever rank as provided for in the
Criminal Procedure Code [Act 593], except the power to arrest without warrant,
in relation to enforcement, inspection and investigation. Such powers shall
be in addition to the powers provided under this Act and not in derogation
thereof.

12. _Clause 12 seeks to amend section 35 of Act 614 to reduce the Commission’s_
contribution into the Federal Consolidated Fund.

13. _Clause 13_ seeks to substitute section 37 of Act 614. The new section
37 seeks to provide for the categories of persons who can represent the
Commission or its employees in a civil proceeding.

14. _Clause 14_ seeks to introduce new sections 37a and 37b into Act 614.
The new section 37a provides for the modes of services of any documents
or notices that would be issued by the Registrar in carrying out its functions
and powers under this Act and the laws specified in the First Schedule. The
new section 37b provides for the evidentiary value of certificates, notices
or documents issued and authenticated in the name of the Chief Executive
Officer, the Registrar or a duly authorized officer.

15. Clause 15 seeks to introduce new sections 38a, 38b, 38c, 38d and 38e
into Act 614. The new section 38a empowers the Registrar to compound any
prescribed offence with the written consent of the Public Prosecutor. The new
section 38b provides for the liability of any director, partner, chief executive
officer, manager, secretary or other similar officer of a body corporate for
offences committed by the body corporate. The new section 38c makes it
an offence to destroy, conceal, mutilate, or alter any book, record, account,

16. _Clause 16 seeks to amend section 40 of Act 614 to empower the Minister_
