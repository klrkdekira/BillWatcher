_Veterans_ 3

A BILL

_i n t i t u l e d_

An Act to establish the National Veterans Council, to provide
for the registration of veterans and veterans associations, and to
provide for matters connected therewith.

[ ]

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:**

PART I

PRELIMINARY

**Short title and commencement**

**1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Veterans Act 2011.**

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed
by the Minister by notification in the _Gazette._

**Interpretation**

**2. In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—**

“veteran activity” includes the following activities:

_(a) organizing any welfare and socioeconomic programs for_

veterans and their dependants;


-----

4 _Bill_

_(b) organizing any campaign in relation to the interests of_

veterans and their dependants;

_(c) raising funds, donations or monetary contributions for_

the welfare of veterans or members of any veteran
associations;

_(d) organizing any appreciation and acknowledgement programs_

for veterans;

_(e)_ organizing or promoting any awareness programs for the

interest and benefit of veterans; and

_(f) participating in conferences, meetings, congregations or_

programs in relation to veterans held in Malaysia or
elsewhere;

“Department” means the Malaysian Armed Forces Veterans
Affairs Department;

“Council” means the National Veterans Council;

“Minister” means the Minister charged with the responsibility
for defence;

“Registrar” means the Registrar appointed under section 7;

“association” means any organization, society, association,
club or body consisting of at least fifty percent membership
amongst former members of the Malaysian Armed Forces or the
Commonwealth Forces who have served in Malaysia;

“veterans association” means any association which is registered
under this Act;

“veteran” means a Malaysian citizen who has served full time
and has not been retired or discharged from service on grounds
of misconduct in any of the following Forces:

_(a) regular forces of the Malaysian Armed Forces;_

_(b) volunteer forces mobilized full time by the Malaysian_

Armed Forces regardless of length of service;

_(c) Force 136;_

_(d) British Forces who served in Malaya, Malaysia or_

Singapore; and

_(e) Sarawak Rangers._

Part II

ESTABLISHMENT AND FUNCTIONS OF THE NATIONAL VETERANS

COUNCIL

**National Veterans Council**

**3.** (1) A body to be known as the National Veterans Council
shall be established for the purpose of this Act.

(2) The Council shall consist of the following members:

_(a) the Minister who shall be the Chairman;_

_(b) the Secretary General of the Ministry of Defence who_

shall be the Deputy Chairman;

_(c) the Director General of the Malaysian Armed Forces Veterans_

Affairs Department who shall be the Secretary;

_(d) the Assistant Chief of Staff (Personnel Services) of the_

Malaysian Armed Forces Headquarters; and

_(e) not more than five persons who have the experience,_

knowledge or expertise in matters relating to veterans’
affairs to be appointed by the Minister.

(3) A member of the Council appointed under paragraph
(2)(e) shall, unless he sooner resigns or vacates his office or
his appointment is sooner revoked, hold office for a term not
exceeding two years and is eligible for reappointment for a term
not exceeding two consecutive terms.

**Functions of the Council**

**4.** (1) The functions of the Council are as follows:

_(a) to formulate policies and plans of action relating to_

veterans’ affairs;

_(b) to oversee the implementation of policies and plans of_

action relating to veterans’ affairs;

_(c) to coordinate and monitor the implementation of policies_

and plans of action relating to veterans with the relevant
ministries, government agencies, bodies or organizations
and the private sector;


-----

6 _Bill_

_(d) to advise and make recommendations on the formulation of_

any legislation or amendment of any existing legislation
in order to secure the welfare and interests of the
veterans;

_(e) to advise the Government on the issues of veterans’ affairs_

including the developments at the international level;

_(f) to act as the monitoring body for all veterans associations_

in Malaysia; and

_(g)_ to perform any other functions as directed by the Minister

for the proper implementation of this Act.

(2) The Council shall have power to do all things expedient
or reasonably necessary for, or incidental to, the performance of
its functions.

(3) The Council shall regulate its own proceedings.

**Allowances**

**5. The members of the Council appointed in subsection 3(2) may**
be paid such allowances as the Minister may determine.

**Council to be assisted by the Department**

**6. The Council shall be assisted by the Department in the**
performance of its functions and the exercise of its powers under
this Act.

Part III

REGISTRATION OF VETERANS

**Registrar**

**7. The Director General of the Malaysian Armed Forces Veterans**
Affairs Department shall be the Registrar.

**Register of veterans**

**8.** (1) The Registrar shall keep and maintain or cause to be kept
and maintained a register of veterans in such manner and form
as may be prescribed.


-----

_Veterans_ 7

(2) Any register of veterans kept and maintained by the Ministry
of Defence and the Department immediately before the coming
into operation of this Act shall, on the coming into operation of
this Act, be deemed to be registers kept and maintained under
subsection (1) and shall be deemed to form part of such register
of veterans.

**Application for registration**

**9.** (1) An application for registration as a veteran shall be made
in writing to the Registrar.

(2) Every application under subsection (1) shall be accompanied
by such document or information as may be prescribed.

**Power of Registrar to require applicant to provide additional**
**document or information**

**10.** (1) The Registrar may, in relation to any application made
under section 9, require the applicant to provide such additional
document or information within the time as may be specified by
the Registrar.

(2) Where the applicant fails to provide the additional document
or information required by the Registrar under subsection (1) within
the specified time or any extended time as may be specified by
the Registrar, the application is deemed to have been withdrawn
and shall not be proceeded with by the Registrar, but without
prejudice to a fresh application being made by the applicant.

**Registration or refusal to register**

**11.** (1) After considering an application under section 9 and any
additional document or information provided under section 10,
the Registrar may approve or refuse the application.

(2) Where the application is approved, the Registrar shall
register the applicant by entering the applicant’s particulars in
the register of veterans referred to in section 8.

(3) Where the application is refused, the applicant may appeal
to the Minister whose decision shall be final.

(4) Notwithstanding subsection (1), and sections 9 and 10, any
person who immediately before the coming into operation of this
Act is registered as a veteran with the Jabatan Arah Rekod dan
Pencen Markas Angkatan Tentera Malaysia shall, on the coming
into operation of this Act, be deemed to be a veteran registered

**Issuance of Veteran Card**

**12.** (1) The Registrar shall issue to a person who is registered
as a veteran under section 11 a Veteran Card.

(2) The Veteran Card issued under subsection (1) shall be

duly registered as a veteran under this Act.

(3) Any person to whom a Kad Bekas Tentera has been issued

Malaysia immediately before the coming into operation of this
Act and deemed to be a veteran under subsection 11(4) shall be
issued a Veteran Card under this Act.

Part IV

REGISTRATION AND SUPERVISION OF VETERANS ASSOCIATIONS

**Application to be registered to carry out veteran activity**

**13.** (1) No association shall carry out any veteran activity unless
it is registered as a veterans association under this Act.

(2) An association which intends to carry out any veteran
activity shall apply to the Registrar for registration under this
Act in such manner and form as may be prescribed.

(3) The Registrar may require the applicant to provide any
document or information as may be prescribed.

**Registration of veterans association**

**14.** (1) After considering an application by an association under
section 13, the Registrar may approve or refuse the application
for registration.


-----

_Veterans_ 9

(2) Without prejudice to the generality of subsection (1), if
the Registrar is satisfied that such association is a non-political
organization for the advancement of the welfare of veterans and
that the constitution of such association contains provisions for
such matters as set out in the Schedule, the Registrar shall approve
the registration of such association as a veterans association under
this Act.

**Register of veterans association**

**15. (1) Where the application for registration is approved by the**
Registrar, he shall register the association as a veteran association
in the register of veterans associations to be kept and maintained by
the Registrar in such manner and form as may be prescribed.

(2) The Registrar may impose any conditions, including the
payment of any fees, as he deems fit upon the registered veterans
association.

(3) A veterans association registered under this Act shall, if its
constitution does not contain the matters set out in the Schedule,
amend its constitution within twenty-one days after its registration
to include such matters.

**Certificate of registration**

**16.** (1) The Registrar shall issue to a veterans association registered
under section 15 a certificate of registration in the prescribed
form.

(2) A certificate of registration, unless proved to have been
subsequently revoked, shall be conclusive evidence for all purposes
that the association has been duly registered by the Registrar as
a veterans association under this Act.

**Association registered under the Societies Act 1966**

**17.** Any association registered under the Societies Act 1966

[Act 335] on the date of coming into operation of this Act shall
cease to be registered under the Societies Act 1966 and shall
within three months thereof apply to the Registrar to be registered
under this Act as a veterans association to carry out any veteran
activity; and such association shall continue to operate as an
association until notified otherwise by the Registrar.

**Revocation or suspension of registration by Registrar**

**18.** (1) The Registrar may revoke or suspend the registration
of a veterans association if the Registrar is satisfied that such
veterans association—

_(a) has been registered as a result of a fraud, mistake or_

misrepresentation;

_(b)_ has failed to comply with any of the conditions imposed

by the Registrar under section 15;

_(c) has contravened any provision of this Act or any regulations_

made under this Act;

_(d) is inactive or is no longer a fit and proper entity to_

be registered to represent veterans in Malaysia or
elsewhere;

_(e) is hindering the advancement of veterans and it is in the_

public interest to revoke or suspend its registration;

_(f) has failed or neglected to remedy any malpractice,_

misconduct or irregularity on the part of its office-bearers
or its members within the period specified in the written
notice given by the Registrar; or

_(g) is inactive, uncooperative or obstructive in the implementation_

of the regulations, policies or guidelines made under
this Act.

(2) Before revoking or suspending the registration of a veterans
association under subsection (1), the Registrar shall give the
association an opportunity to submit reasons, if any, against the
proposed revocation or suspension.

**Appeal**

**19.** (1) Any association aggrieved by a decision of the
Registrar—

_(a)_ in refusing to register such association;

_(b)_ in imposing conditions on the registration of such

association; or

_(c)_ in revoking or suspending the registration of such

association,


-----

_Veterans_ 11

may, within thirty days from the date of the notification of the
decision of the Registrar, appeal to the Minister whose decision
shall be final.

(2) Before making any decision under subsection (1), the
Minister may refer the matter to the Council for its advice.

**Directions to continue functions of association**

**20.** The Minister may, pending his decision on an appeal under
section 19, give any direction so as to enable such association to
continue its functions and may appoint an _ad hoc_ committee to
manage the affairs of the association for a period not exceeding
twelve months.

**Internal procedures for resolution of dispute**

**21.** Every veterans association shall resolve any dispute arising
amongst its members or with its committee or governing body
in accordance with the internal procedures prescribed in the
regulations.

**Referral of dispute to Minister**

**22.** (1) Where a dispute cannot be resolved under the internal
procedures referred to in section 21, any aggrieved member or the
veterans association itself may refer the dispute to the Minister
for resolution.

(2) Notwithstanding subsection (1), the Minister may, upon the
advice of the Registrar or at any time he deems it necessary and
expedient, require any veterans association to refer any dispute
to him for resolution.

(3) The decision of the Minister under this section shall be
final; but the Minister may seek advice of the Council or any
other committee established by the Minister for that purpose
before making his decision.

**Consequences of refusal, revocation or suspension of**
**registration**

**23.** Upon the refusal of the Registrar to register any association
under section 17 or upon the revocation or suspension of the
registration of any veterans association under this Act, the following
provisions shall apply:

_(a)_ such association shall not organize, conduct, sanction or

participate in any veteran activity or any events or forum
relating to veterans affairs whether within or outside
Malaysia; and

_(b)_ such association shall not receive or continue to receive

funding for veterans activity or any matter in connection
with the interest, affairs or welfare of veterans from the
Government or any statutory body.

Schedule

[Section 14]

CONSTITUTION OF VETERANS ASSOCIATION

The following matters shall be provided in the constitution of a veterans
association:

**1.** The association shall have the following aims and objects:

_(a) for the advancement of the welfare of the veterans;_

_(b) to maintain good terms with and to be in good standing with the_

Department;

_(c) to ensure that its constitution is observed and abided by all its members;_

and

_(d) to control and exercise jurisdiction over all activities in relation to_

the affairs of veterans.

**2.** Its activities are conducted without discrimination as to gender, race,
religion, social origin, language, political inclination or any other opinion
held by its members.

**3.** The selection procedures for committee members in the association are
open and fair.

**4.** Its fully audited accounts with reports on all its activities and the use and
disbursement of all its funds for its preceding financial year be submitted to
the Registrar together with such other reports as the Registrar may require.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to provide for the establishment of the National Veterans
Council, to provide for the registration of veterans and veterans associations
and for matters connected therewith.

2. Part I deals with preliminary matters.

_Clause 1_ contains the short title and the commencement provision of the

proposed Act.


-----

14 _Bill_

_Clause 2 contains the definition of certain terms used in the proposed_

Act.

3. Part II contains provision on the establishment of the National Veterans
Council.

_Clause 3 provides for the establishment of the Council while_ _clause 4_

deals with its functions.

_Clause 5_ provides for the payment of such allowances to the members of

the Council as the Minister may determine.

_Clause 6 provides that the Council shall be assisted by the Department in_

the performance of its functions and exercise of its powers.

4. Part III contains provisions relating to the registration of veterans.

_Clause 7 provides for the appointment of the Registrar._

_Clause 8 provides for the keeping and maintenance of a register of_

veterans.

_Clause 9_ deals with the application for registration.

_Clause 10_ empowers the Registrar to request for additional document or

information from an applicant.

_Clause 11_ empowers the Registrar to approve or refuse to approve the

application for registration of an applicant.

_Clause 12 deals with the issuance of the Veteran Card to a registered_

veteran.

5. Part IV contains provisions relating to the registration and supervision of
veterans associations.

_Clause 13 deals with the application for registration of veterans associations._

No association shall carry out any veteran activity unless it is registered as
a veterans association under this Act.

_Clause 14_ empowers the Registrar to approve or refuse to approve the

application for registration by an applicant as a veterans association.

_Clause 15 provides for the keeping and maintenance of a register of veterans_

associations.

_Clause 16 deals with the issuance of the certificate of registration to the_

veterans association.

_Clause 17 deals with any association carrying out veteran activity registered_

under the Societies Act 1966. The association shall cease to be registered
under the Societies Act 1966 and shall be registered under this Act.


-----

_Veterans_ 15

_Clause 18_ sets out the situations in which the Registrar may revoke or

suspend any registered veterans associations.

_Clause 19_ deals with appeals to the Minister by any association aggrieved

by the decision of the Registrar.

_Clause 20 seeks to enable the Minister to issue directions for the continuation_

of the functions of an association in a case where an appeal by the association
is still pending.

_Clause 21 stipulates that a veterans association must resolve its disputes_

in accordance with the prescribed internal procedures.

_Clause 22 deals with referral of disputes to the Minister._

_Clause 23_ deals with the consequences of refusal of registration and the

revocation or suspension of registration.

6. Part V contains a general provision.

_Clause 24_ empowers the Minister to make regulations.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure.
