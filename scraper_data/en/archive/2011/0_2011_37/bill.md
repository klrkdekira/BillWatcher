_Perumahan Rakyat 1Malaysia_ 5

A BILL

_i n t i t u l e d_

An Act to provide for and regulate matters relating to the development
and construction of housing accommodation, infrastructure and
facilities under the Perumahan Rakyat 1Malaysia programme,
to address the need for sustainable housing and community
living in urban areas in Malaysia towards creating a strategic
socio-economic housing development model, to provide for the
establishment of Perumahan Rakyat 1Malaysia Corporation, and
to provide for related matters.

[ ]

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:**

PART I

PRELIMINARY

**Short title and commencement**

**1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Perumahan Rakyat 1Malaysia**
Act 2011.

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed
by the Minister by notification in the _Gazette, and the Minister_
may appoint different dates—

_(a) for the coming into operation of this Act in different parts_
of Malaysia;

_(c) for the coming into operation of different provisions or_

**2. In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—**

“prescribed” means—

_(a) prescribed by the Minister by regulations; or_

_(b) prescribed by the Corporation by rules,_

as the case may be;

_(a) the Federal Government, the relevant State Government_

section 3A of the Housing Development (Control and Licensing)
Act 1966 [Act 118];

“Perumahan Rakyat 1Malaysia homes” or “PR1MA homes”
means the housing accommodation constructed in accordance with
the quality, requirement, specification and standard prescribed
under this Act;

PART II

THE CORPORATION

**Establishment of the Corporation**

**3. (1) A body corporate to be known as the “Perumahan Rakyat**
1Malaysia Corporation” or “PR1MA Corporation Malaysia” is
established.


-----

_Perumahan Rakyat 1Malaysia_ 9

(2) The Corporation shall have perpetual succession and a
common seal.

(3) The Corporation may sue and be sued in its corporate
name.

(4) Subject to and for the purposes of this Act, the Corporation
may, upon such terms as the Corporation thinks fit—

_(a) enter into contracts;_

_(b) acquire, purchase, take, hold and enjoy movable and_
immovable property of every description; and

_(c) convey, assign, surrender, yield up, charge, mortgage,_
demise, reassign, transfer or otherwise dispose of, or
deal with, any movable or immovable property or any
interest in the property vested in the Corporation.

**Common seal**

**4. (1) The Corporation shall have a common seal which shall**
bear a device approved by the Corporation and the seal may from
time to time be broken, changed, altered and made anew as the
Corporation thinks fit.

(2) Until a seal is provided by the Corporation, a stamp bearing
the words “PR1MA Corporation Malaysia” may be used and shall
be deemed to be the common seal of the Corporation.

(3) The common seal shall be kept in the custody of the
Chairman of the Corporation or any other person authorized by
the Corporation, and shall be authenticated by either the Chairman
of the Corporation or such other person as may be authorized by
the Chairman of the Corporation in writing.

(4) All deeds, documents and other instruments purporting to
be sealed with the seal and duly authenticated shall, until the
contrary is proved, be deemed to have been validly executed.

(5) Any deed, document or other instrument which, if executed
by a person not being a body corporate, is not required to be
under seal may in like manner be executed by a member of the
Corporation or an employee of the Corporation authorized in that
behalf.


-----

10 _Bill_

(6) The common seal of the Corporation shall be officially
and judicially noticed.

**Membership of the Corporation**

**5.** (1) The Corporation shall consist of—

_(a) the following members who shall be appointed by the_
Prime Minister:

(i) a Chairman;

(ii) not less than two but not more than five representatives
of the Federal Government; and

(iii) not less than three but not more than five other
members; and

_(b) the Chief Executive Officer._

(2) The persons appointed under paragraph (1)(a) shall be
persons who, in the opinion of the Prime Minister, have experience
and shown capacity and professionalism in matters relating to
housing and community development, finance, construction,
engineering, business or administration, or are otherwise suitable
for appointment because of their special knowledge, experience
or suitable qualifications.

**Temporary exercise of functions of the Chairman**

**6. The Prime Minister may appoint temporarily any member of**
the Corporation, other than the Chief Executive Officer, to act
as the Chairman of the Corporation for the period when—

_(a) the office of the Chairman of the Corporation is_
vacant;

_(b) the Chairman of the Corporation is absent from duty or_
from Malaysia; or

_(c) the Chairman of the Corporation is, for any other reason,_
unable to perform the duties of his office.

_(d) to collect, record and maintain data, information and_
statistic concerning PR1MA development and PR1MA

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

The proposed PR1MA Homes Act 2011 (“the proposed Act”) seeks to provide
for and regulate matters relating to the development and construction of housing
accommodation, infrastructure and facilities under the Perumahan Rakyat
1Malaysia Programme, to address the need for sustainable housing and community
living in urban areas in Malaysia towards creating a strategic socio-economic
housing development model, to provide for the establishment of Perumahan
Rakyat 1Malaysia Corporation, and to provide for related matters.

PART I

2. Part I deals with preliminary matters.

3. _Clause 1 contains the short title and provisions on the commencement of_
the proposed Act. In relation to the commencement, the Minister may appoint
different dates for the coming into operation of different provisions of the
proposed Act.

4. _Clause 2 contains the definitions of certain words used in the proposed_
Act.

PART II

5. Part II contains provisions relating to the establishment of the
Corporation.

6. _Clause 3 seeks to provide for the establishment of the Corporation._

7. _Clause 4_ seeks to provide for the common seal of the Corporation.

8. _Clause 5 contains provisions on the membership of the Corporation._

9. _Clause 6 empowers the Prime Minister to appoint temporarily any member_
of the Corporation, other than the Chief Executive Officer, to exercise the
functions of the Chairman of the Corporation where the office of the Chairman
of the Corporation is vacant or when the Chairman is absent from duty or
from Malaysia or is unable to perform the duties of his office.

10. _Clause 7 provides for the procedures to be adhered to by the Corporation_
which is set out in the Schedule.

11. _Clause 8 requires a member of the Corporation to seek the prior approval_
of the Prime Minister before holding any other office.

12. _Clause 9 deals with the tenure of office of members of the_
Corporation.

13. _Clause 10 provides for the payment of remuneration and allowances to_
the members of the Corporation.

14. _Clause 11 allows the Corporation to provide subsidies, grants and certain_
form of facilities to the members of the Corporation and their dependents.

15. _Clause 12 provides for the revocation of appointment of members of_
the Corporation and their resignation.

16. _Clause 13 sets out the situations in which the office of a member of the_
Corporation is vacated.

17. _Clause 14_ empowers the Corporation to establish committees to assist
it.

18. _Clause 15 requires any member of the Corporation, the Council, the_
Approvals and Implementation Committee or any committee established by
the Corporation to disclose his interest in any matter under discussion by the
Corporation or committee. Non-disclosure is an offence and is subject to a
penalty.

19. _Clause 16 clarifies that the provisions relating to the tenure of office_
of members of the Corporation, payment of remunerations and allowances of
members of the Corporation as well as revocation of appointment of members
of the Corporation provided under this Part shall not apply to the Chief
Executive Officer.

PART III

20. Part III deals with the functions and powers of the Corporation.

21. _Clauses 17,_ _18 and_ _19 set out the functions and powers of the_
Corporation.

22. _Clause 20 allows the Corporation to delegate its functions and powers to_
the Chairman of the Corporation, a member of the Corporation, the Approvals
and Implementation Committee, a committee of the Corporation, an employee
of the Corporation or a body corporate established by the Corporation.

23. _Clause 21 confers on the Minister the power to issue directions to the_
Corporation.

24. _Clause 22 places a duty on the Corporation to furnish certain returns,_
reports, accounts and information to the Minister or any public authority.

25. _Clause 23 allows the Corporation, with the approval of the Minister_
and the concurrence of the Minister responsible for finance, to establish and
participate in any body corporate to carry out any project, scheme or enterprise
as determined by it in the performance of its functions or the exercise of its
powers.

PART IV

26. Part IV deals with the employees of the Corporation.

27. _Clause 24 empowers the Prime Minister to appoint the Chief Executive_
Officer and sets out the duties of the Chief Executive Officer of the
Corporation.

28. _Clause 25 empowers the Prime Minister to appoint temporarily any_
employee of the Corporation to exercise the duties of the Chief Executive
Officer where the office of the Chief Executive Officer is vacant or when the
Chief Executive Officer is absent or unable to perform his duties.

29. _Clause 26 empowers the Corporation to employ its employees._

30. _Clause 27 allows the Corporation to determine the conditions of service of_
its employees subject to the approval of the Minister and empower the Corporation
to have disciplinary authority and control over all its employees.

31. _Clause 28 deals with the power of the Corporation to make arrangements_
for payments for retirement benefits, medical benefits, gratuities and other
allowances to its employees and their dependents and to make donations.

32. _Clause 29 allows the Corporation to adopt regulations, rules, policies,_
circulars or directives issued by the Federal Government in relation to any
matter as regards the employees of the Corporation.

PART V

33. Part V deals with PR1MA development and PR1MA complexes.

34. _Clause 30 seeks to provide for the establishment of the PR1MA Advisory_
Council, its membership, functions and its power to give directions to the
Corporation on matters consistent with the provisions of this Act.

35. _Clause 31 provides for, among others, the responsibility of the_
Corporation to take into consideration any written laws and policy of the
Federal Government and Government Entities, in particular the policy relating
to PR1MA development and PR1MA complexes and sets out the functions
incidental to such responsibility.

36. _Clause 32 seeks to provide for the criteria of eligible person for the_
purpose of application to purchase or rent PR1MA homes.

37. _Clause 33 requires that the allocation of PR1MA homes to eligible_
persons shall be carried out in a transparent and fair manner.

38. _Clause 34 seeks to provide for, among others, the eligibility criteria_
to participate in PR1MA development, application to participate in PR1MA
development, power of the Corporation to impose conditions and appeal on
the conditions imposed.

39. _Clause 35 seeks to empower the Corporation to issue directions pertaining_
to the approval given under clause 34. This clause also provides for the manner
in which a direction shall be issued.

PART VI

40. Part VI contains provisions relating to co-ordination between the
Corporation and the Government Entities in relation to PR1MA development
and PR1MA complexes.

41. _Clause 36 provides for the Corporation to act as the principal coordinating_
agent or authorized agent on behalf of the Government Entities in dealing
with Approvals in relation to PR1MA development.

42. _Clause 37 provides for the establishment of the Approvals and Implementation_
Committee, its membership and responsibilities in assisting the Corporation
in relation to Approvals and implementation of PR1MA development.

43. _Clause 38 provides that the Corporation may, subject to certain conditions,_
enter into an agreement or arrangement with any Government Entity concerning
the carrying out of their respective functions and powers.

44. _Clause 39 sets out certain responsibilities of Government Entities to_
co-operate with and assist the Corporation.

PART VII

45. Part VII contains provisions on finance.

46. _Clauses 40 and 41 provide for the establishment of the PR1MA Corporation_
Malaysia Fund and the expenditure to be charged to the Fund.

47. _Clause 42 provides for the establishment of the Eligible Persons’ Assistance_
Fund and the purpose for which moneys from the Fund may be disbursed.

48. _Clause 43 places a duty on the Corporation to conserve the PR1MA_
Corporation Malaysia Fund.

49. _Clauses 44, 45 and 46 allow the Corporation to open and maintain bank_
accounts, to borrow money and to invest the moneys of the Corporation,
respectively.

50. _Clause 47 requires the Corporation to seek approval of the Minister_
and the Minister responsible for finance before it enters into a contract under
which the Corporation is to pay or receive an amount exceeding ten million
ringgit.

51. _Clause 48 allows the Corporation to determine its own financial_
procedure.

52. _Clause 49 states that the financial year of the Corporation shall begin_
on 1 January and end on 31 December of each year.

53. _Clause 50 requires the Corporation to keep proper accounts of the_
PR1MA Corporation Malaysia Fund and the Eligible Persons’ Assistance Fund
and proper reports of its activities. The Corporation is required to have its
statement of accounts and a statement of its activities for the financial year
to be sent to the Minister who will then cause them to be laid before both
Houses of Parliament. This _clause also provides that the Statutory Bodies_
(Accounts and Annual Reports) Act 1980 shall apply to the Corporation.

PART VIII

54. Part VIII deals with the right to repurchase PR1MA homes by the
Corporation.

55. _Clause 51 empowers the Corporation to repurchase PR1MA homes from_
the owner of the PR1MA homes who, among others, commits serious breach
or repeatedly commits serious breach of any regulations or rules relating to
PR1MA homes, made a misrepresentation of a material fact relating to the
purchase of PR1MA homes or at any time, ceased to be a citizen of Malaysia.
The purpose of this provision is to ensure harmonious environment within
the area of PR1MA homes and that the PR1MA homes repurchased by the
Corporation be allocated for another eligible person.

56. _Clause 52 provides for the procedures relating to the repurchase of_
PR1MA homes by the Corporation.

57. _Clause 53 provides for the transfer or vesting of ownership of the_
repurchased PR1MA homes.

58. _Clause 54_ provides for the manner of determination and payment of
repurchase price of PR1MA homes.

59. _Clause 55 provides for the taking of possession by the Corporation of_
the repurchased PR1MA homes under this Part.

PART IX

60. Part IX deals with the Appeal Tribunal.

61. _Clauses 56 to_ _67 seek to establish an Appeal Tribunal and provide for_
matters pertaining to the Appeal Tribunal. The rationale for the establishment of
the Appeal Tribunal is to establish an expert review body, which provides the
industry with an avenue which may not be available through court proceedings;
to promote transparency and eliminate the pressures on the Minister to review
detailed administrative decisions and to promote fast and cost-effective avenue
of appeal.

PART X

62. Part X contains miscellaneous provisions.

63. _Clause 68 requires the Corporation to maintain a register of all matters_
required to be registered under the proposed Act and under the rules and
regulations made under the proposed Act.

64. _Clause 69 provides for the exclusive right of the Corporation to use the_
symbol or representation selected or created by the Corporation. It also seeks
to prohibit the misuse of such symbol or representation.

65. _Clause 70 creates an offence for unauthorized modification of information_
in the custody of the Corporation, whether in electronic form or otherwise.

66. _Clause 71 deals with the prosecution of offences under the proposed_
Act.

67. _Clause 72 states that the members of the Corporation, the Council, the_
Appeal Tribunal, the Approvals and Implementation Committee, or a committee
or employees or agents of the Corporation or the officers of the Appeal Tribunal
are deemed to be public servants within the meaning of the Penal Code.

68. _Clause 73 seeks to provide for the protection of the Minister, the_
Corporation, any member of the Corporation, the Council, the Appeal Tribunal,
the Approvals and Implementation Committee, or a committee or employees
or agents of the Corporation or officer of the Appeal Tribunal, and any other
person lawfully acting on behalf of the Corporation in respect of any act,
neglect, default or omission done or omitted by it or him in good faith in
such capacity.

69. _Clause 74 confers the protection afforded in the Public Authorities_
Protection Act 1948 to any action, suit, proceedings or prosecution against the
Corporation or against a member of the Corporation, the Council, the Appeal
Tribunal, the Approvals and Implementation Committee, or a committee or
employees or agents of the Corporation or officers of the Appeal Tribunal, in
respect of any act, neglect, default or omission done or omitted by it or him
in good faith in such capacity.
