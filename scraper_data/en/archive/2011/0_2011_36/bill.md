A BILL

_i n t i t u l e d_

An Act to amend the Standards of Malaysia Act 1996.

[ ]

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:**

**Short title and commencement**

**1.** (1) This Act may be cited as the Standards of Malaysia
(Amendment) Act 2011.

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed
by the Minister by notification in the _Gazette._

**Amendment of long title**

**2. The Standards of Malaysia Act 1996 [Act 549], which is**
referred to as the “principal Act” in this Act, is amended in
the long title by inserting after the word “standards” the words
“, accreditation”.

22 _Bill_

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Standards of Malaysia Act 1996 (“Act 549”).

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and the power of the Minister to appoint_
the commencement date of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend the long title of Act 549 to include accreditation_
as one of the roles and functions of the Department of Standards, Malaysia.

4. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 549 to ensure that the definitions_
under that section are in line with internationally accepted definitions and to
ensure that the definitions are accurate and suitable with the context used in
Malaysia. The proposed amendments are also in line with the expansion of
the role and function of the Department of Standards, Malaysia.

5. _Clause 5 seeks to amend section 10 of Act 549 to revise the duties and_
functions of the Director General in line with the role of the Department of
Standards, Malaysia as the standards and accreditation body. This amendment
seeks to delete the function on registration and to insert new functions of the
Director General on the cooperation with the relevant agencies in facilitating
the development of technical regulations, principles and procedures relating
to the implementation of technical regulations and conformity assessment.

The proposed amendment also seeks to expand the Department’s accreditation

services to abroad in order to be at par with other international bodies and to
promote co-operative arrangements with organizations engaged in standardization
and accreditation at international level.

6. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 13 of Act 549 to increase the membership_
of the Malaysian Standards and Accreditation Council for the purpose of
having a wider representation. The proposed amendment also seeks to revise
the duties of the Malaysian Standards and Accreditation Council to approve
criteria relating to the preparation, approval, acceptance and designation of
standards in Malaysia, and accreditation of conformity assessment body.

The proposed amendment also seeks to introduce new provision on the

appointment of alternate members of the Malaysian Standards and Accreditation
Council and the provision on casting vote.

7. _Clause 7 seeks to amend the heading of Part V of Act 549 to include_
“accreditation” as one of the main activities of the Department of Standards,
Malaysia.

8. _Clause 8 seeks to amend section 15 of Act 549 to include the provision_
on the approval and declaration of Malaysian Standards by the Minister.

The proposed amendment also seeks to provide for the revision of standards

in line with international practice that standards are to be revised periodically.
Further, any person who is affected by any declaration, amendment or withdrawal
of Malaysian Standards may give comment through a mechanism which will
be determined by the Malaysian Standards and Accreditation Council.

9. _Clause 9 seeks to amend section 16 of Act 549 to provide for the_
application to be accredited as a conformity assessment body. Further, the
proposed amendment seeks to provide that a prescribed accreditation fee
shall be payable by the conformity assessment body to the Director General.
The amendment also seeks to provide that any accreditation certificate holder
whose accreditation certificate has been suspended or revoked may make an
appeal to the Malaysian Standards and Accreditation Council.

10. _Clause 10 seeks to amend section 17 of Act 549 to protect the name_

of the Department of Standards, Malaysia from being misused and to include
punishment of imprisonment as a penalty under that section.

11. _Clause 11 seeks to substitute section 18 of Act 549 with a new section 18_

to include punishment of imprisonment as a penalty to penalize any person
making any misleading statement or representation in contravention of
Act 549 and who uses the accreditation symbol in contravention of the terms
and conditions of the accreditation certificates.

12. _Clause 12 seeks to provide for the ownership of Malaysian Standards_
by the Government of Malaysia.

13. _Clause 13 seeks to introduce a new Part Va to provide for the powers_

relating to enforcement, namely powers of investigation, search and seizure. The
proposed amendment also seeks to authorize any officer of the Department of
Standards, Malaysia or any public officer to exercise the enforcement powers
under Act 549.

14. _Clause 14 seeks to amend section 22 of Act 549 by deleting the power_

to make regulations on registration matters as this is not the duty and function
of the Department of Standards, Malaysia. The proposed amendment also seeks
to insert a new subsection to penalize any act done in contravention of the
regulations made under section 22 of Act 549.

15. _Clause 15 seeks to amend section 25 of Act 549 by deleting the provision_

on licence to use standards marks. This provision is no longer applicable under
the existing role of the Department of Standards, Malaysia.

16. _Clause 16 seeks to provide for savings provisions._

17. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are

amendments which are minor or consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will involve the Government in extra financial expenditure the amount
of which cannot at present be ascertained.
