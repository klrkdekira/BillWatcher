EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Securities Commission Act 1993 (“Act 498”).

2. The proposed amendments seek to clarify and expand the Commission’s
regulatory functions, to provide a framework for the recognition of foreign
auditors and foreign audit firms by the Audit Oversight Board, to streamline
the Audit Oversight Board’s operational processes, to enhance investigation and
evidence gathering processes, miscellaneous and consequential amendments.

3. _Clause 1 contains the short title. It also seeks to empower the Minister_
to appoint a date of coming into operation of the Act and to appoint different
dates for the coming into operation of different provisions of this Act, or all
or different provisions of this Act in respect of different classes or categories
of persons or securities.

4. _Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 to substitute for the words “futures_
contracts”, “futures exchange” and “futures market” the words “derivatives”,
“derivatives exchange” and “derivatives market”. Further, subsection 2(2) is
deleted as it is replaced with the new section 159A.

5. _Clause 3_ seeks to amend section 15 as follows:

_(a)_ in paragraphs _(a),_ _(b),_ _(g),_ _(i),_ _(j),_ _(k),_ _(l) and_ _(n) to expand the_
Commission’s regulatory oversight to cover derivatives;

_(b)_ in paragraph (e) to extend the Commission’s oversight over all fund
management activities;

_(c)_ in paragraph _(m) to enable the Commission to supervise all persons_
engaging in regulated activities or providing capital market
services;

_(d)_ in paragraph _(o) to enable the Audit Oversight Board to provide for_
recognition of foreign auditors and foreign audit firms;

_(e)_ to insert a new paragraph (p) to expand the Commission’s regulatory
function to include the monitoring, mitigating and managing of
systemic risk in the capital market;

_(f)_ to insert a new paragraph (q) to recognise the Commission’s existing
role in promoting and regulating corporate governance and approved
accounting standards in respect of listed corporations; and

_(g)_ to insert a new paragraph _(r) to enable the Commission to set and_
approve standards for professional qualification in the capital
market.

6. _Clause 4_ seeks to amend section 22A in line with the expansion of the
Commission’s regulatory oversight over derivatives.

7. _Clause 5 seeks to amend section 24 by removing the reference to the_
words “long position” and “short position”.

8. _Clause 6_ seeks to amend section 31A to insert the definition of “relevant
foreign authority” which is currently found in subsection 31ZD(2) and to insert
the definitions of “recognised auditor” and “registered auditor”.

23. _Clause 26 seeks to amend section 134 to enable the recording of evidence_
by audio or visual recording or a combination of both when the statement is
recorded under subsection 134(1) and to enable substituted service of notice if
the said notice cannot be served under situations set out in subsection 134(6)
or (8).

24. _Clause 27_ seeks to amend section 146 to streamline all general appeal
provisions under the securities laws into Act 498.

25. _Clause 28 seeks to amend section 147 to streamline existing provisions_
on the finality of the Minister’s and the Commission’s decisions under the
securities laws into Act 498.

26. _Clause 29 seeks to amend subsection 150A(4) to enable the admission of_
oral evidence recorded by a foreign supervisory authority by means of audio
or visual recording or combination of both and the admission of an affidavit
affirmed by a person who is a witness but is residing outside Malaysia, in
court. Further subsection 150A(5) is amended to enable an affidavit to be
admitted in court when it is either sworn or affirmed by a witness.

27. _Clause 30 seeks to insert section 150B to enable admissibility of statements_
taken and documents seized from a person who is dead or cannot be traced,
_etc., in court._

28. _Clause 31_ seeks to amend section 152A to enable the Commission to
publish information on actions taken by the Commission under section 125
or 220 Capital Markets and Services Act 2007 (Act 671).

29. _Clause 32_ seeks to amend section 159 to consolidate existing powers to
make regulations under securities laws into Act 498.

30. _Clause 33_ seeks to insert section 159A to consolidate the Minister’s
powers to amend schedules.

31. _Clause 34 seeks to substitute for the words “futures contracts” the word_
“derivatives” in Schedule 1.

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure.
