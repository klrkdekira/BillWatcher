**Short title and commencement**

**1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Pensions (Amendment)**

section 7 and subsections 10(1), (2) and (3) come into operation
on 1 January 2009.

(3) Paragraph 2(b), section 9 and subsection 10(4) are deemed
to have come into operation on 1 January 2008.

(4) The amendments in paragraphs 4(b) and 5(b), sections 6
and 8, in so far as they relate to—

_(a)_ the reduction of the amount of the derivative pension or
derivative retiring allowance to seventy per cent after the
period of twelve and a half years, come into operation
on 1 January 2009; and

_(b)_ the requirement of residency in Malaysia, are deemed to
have come into operation on 1 January 2008.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Pensions Act 1980 (“Act 227”) to enable officers
who had opted for the Employees Provident Fund Scheme to opt for pension,
gratuity or other benefit, to provide for the abolishment of requirement of
residency in Malaysia for the grant of derivative pension or derivative retiring
allowance, the increase of reckonable service from twenty-five years to thirty
years, and the abolishment of reduction of derivative pension or derivative
retiring allowance to seventy per cent after the period of the twelve and a
half years from the date of retirement of an officer or the date immediately
following the date of death of an officer in service, all for the improvement
of pension benefits to officers of the public service.

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title, provisions on the commencement and_
application of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend the definition of “pensionable officer” to include_
officers under the Employees Provident Fund Scheme who have opted for
pension, gratuity or other benefit under subsection 6a(6a) but die before the
option becomes irrevocable. This _clause_ also seeks to delete the definition of
“resident in Malaysia”.


-----

6 _Bill_

4. Clause 3 seeks to amend section 6a of Act 227 to enable an officer who
had opted for the Employees Provident Fund Scheme to be given an option
to opt for pension, gratuity or other benefit under Act 227. The officers
who are allowed to do so are those who had opted for the Employees
Provident Fund Scheme before 1 January 2009 and have been confirmed
in their appointment before that date. The option shall be irrevocable on
1 February 2009.

5. Clauses 4, 5, 6 and _8 respectively seeks to amend sections 12, 14, 15_
and 25 of Act 227 to abolish the requirement of residency in Malaysia for the
grant of derivative pension or derivative retiring allowance and the reduction
of derivative pensions or derivative retiring allowance to seventy per cent
after the period of twelve and a half years from the date of retirement of an
officer or the date immediately following the date of death of an officer in
service.

6. Clause 7 seeks to amend section 22 of Act 227 to enable maximum pension
that can be given to an officer be increased from half of his last drawn salary
to three-fifths of his last drawn salary.

7. Clause 9 seeks to delete section 25a of Act 227 as consequential amendment
to the abolishment of requirement of residency in Malaysia.

8. Clause 10 deals with savings and transitional provisions. _Subclause (1)_
seeks to enable an officer, who has opted for the Employees Provident Fund
Scheme before 1 January 2009 but the confirmation in his appointment has
been backdated to before 1 January 2009, to be allowed to opt for pension,
gratuity or other benefit under Act 227. _Subclause (2) seeks to provide that_
this option shall be irrevocable one month from the date he exercises the
option. _Subclause (3) seeks to provide that the grant of pension, derivative_
pension, retiring allowance or derivative retiring allowance shall be based
on the amendments made to the principal Act only from 1 January 2009. In
relation to the abolishment of requirement of residency in Malaysia, the grant
of derivative pension or derivative retiring allowance and the payment thereof
are clarified in subclause _(4)._

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will involve the Government in extra financial expenditure the amount
of which cannot at present be ascertained.
