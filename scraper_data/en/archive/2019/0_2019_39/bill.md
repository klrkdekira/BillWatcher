_i n t i t u l e d_

An Act to amend the Income Tax Act 1967.

[ ]

**ENACTED** by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:

**Short title**

**1.** This Act may be cited as the Income Tax (Amendment)
Act 2019.

**General amendment**

**2.** The Income Tax Act 1967 [Act 53], which is referred to as
the “principal Act” in this Act, is amended by substituting for
the word “Clerk”, wherever appearing in section 98 including
the shoulder note, sections 100, 101 and Schedule 5 the word
“Secretary”.

**Amendment of section 2**

**3.** Section 2 of the principal Act is amended—

_(a)_ by deleting the definition of “Clerk”; and

_Income Tax (Amendment)_ 7

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Income Tax Act 1967 [Act 53].

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title of the proposed Act._

3. Clause 2 seeks to substitute for the word “Clerk” the word “Secretary”
wherever appearing in section 98 including the shoulder note, sections 100,
101 and Schedule 5 to Act 53.

4. _Clause 3_ seeks to amend subsection 2(1) of Act 53 to introduce the
definition of “Secretary”.

5. _Clause 4_ seeks to amend Schedule 5 to Act 53.

_Paragraph 4(a)_ seeks to substitute for paragraph 1a of Schedule 5 to

Act 53 to empower the Chairman to determine the sitting of an appeal to be
heard by a Special Commissioner sitting alone if the Chairman deems it fit
for the expeditious and efficient conduct of the appeal. Currently, the hearing
of the appeal is by three Special Commissioners.

_Paragraph 4(b) seeks to introduce a new paragraph_ 1b into Schedule 5 to

Act 53 to allow for appeal proceedings, with the consent of the parties, to be
continued or to be heard afresh by the remaining Special Commissioners if any
of the three Special Commissioners hearing an appeal is unable to complete
the hearing of the appeal due to expiration of his term of appointment or other
reasons. The proposed new subparagraph 1b also seeks to allow for appeal
proceedings, with the consent of the parties, to be continued or to be heard
afresh by another Special Commissioner if the Special Commissioner hearing
an appeal is unable to complete the hearing of the appeal due to expiration
of his term of appointment or other reasons.

_Paragraph 4(c) seeks to delete paragraph 4 of Schedule 5 to Act 53 to_

remove the requirement for the Secretary of the Special Commissioners to
inform the Director General of the programme drawn up under paragraph 3
of Schedule 5 to Act 53.

_Paragraph 4(f) seeks to substitute paragraph 34 of Schedule 5 to Act 53_

whereas _paragraph 4(g)_ seeks to introduce new paragraphs 34a, 34b and 34c
into Schedule 5 to Act 53 to provide for the procedures for the filing of an
appeal to the High Court by the party aggrieved by a deciding order of the
Special Commissioners.

_Paragraph 4(i)_ seeks to substitute paragraph 37a of Schedule 5 to Act 53

to provide that the appellant shall pay for the cost of the notes of proceedings
or other documents at such rate as may be prescribed by the Minister.

6. _Clause 5_ seeks to provide for savings provision.

7. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Explanatory Statement
are minor or consequential in nature.
