**53f.** Where contributions under section 53d have been

the contributions transferred.”.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Employees Provident Fund Act 1991 (“Act 452”).

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title of the proposed Act and the power of_
the Minister to appoint the commencement date of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2_ seeks to amend section 4 of Act 452 regarding the membership
of the Board.

_Subclause 2(a) seeks to amend paragraph 4(1)(d) of Act 452 to increase the_

total number of members of the Board to be appointed under that paragraph
from not more than eighteen to not more than twenty.

_Subclause 2(e) seeks to amend subparagraph 4(1)(d)(iv) of Act 452_

to increase the number of members of the Board to be appointed under that
subparagraph from not more than three to not more than five. The amendment

further provides that the members under that subparagraph shall be appointed
from amongst persons who have knowledge or experience in finance, business,
economy, social security or other relevant knowledge or experience.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 6 of Act 452 to introduce a new_
subsection 6(2) into Act 452. The new subsection 6(2) provides that a member
of the Board, other than the chief executive officer, shall be eligible for
reappointment but no member shall hold office for a total term of more than
twelve years.

5. _Clause 4_ seeks to amend subsection 9(1) of Act 452 to increase the
number of members of the Board from six to eight, excluding the Chairman
or Deputy Chairman, to form a quorum at all meetings of the Board.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to amend section 18 of Act 452._

_Subclause 5(a) seeks to delete paragraph 18(1)(c) of Act 452 to remove the_

membership of the Governor of Bank Negara Malaysia or his representative
from the Investment Panel for the purpose of avoiding any conflict of interest
in discharging his duties.

_Subclause 5(b) seeks to amend paragraph 18(1)(e) of Act 452 to increase_

the number of members of the Investment Panel to be appointed under
that paragraph from three to four. The amendment further provides that the
members under that paragraph shall be appointed from amongst persons who
have knowledge or experience in finance, business, economy or other relevant
knowledge or experience.

7. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 39 of Act 452._

_Subclause 6(a) seeks to amend paragraph 39(1)(a) of Act 452 to provide_

for measures to prevent a person from leaving Malaysia if the chief executive
officer has reason to believe that such person is about or is likely to leave
Malaysia without paying any outstanding and due contributions under Act 452.

_Subclause 6(b) seeks to introduce new subsections 39(3) and (4) into Act 452._

The proposed subsection (3) seeks to make it an offence for any person
who leaves Malaysia without paying the outstanding and due contributions
or furnishing security for its payment. The proposed subsection (4) provides
that for the purposes of section 39 of Act 452, “contributions” includes any
dividend or late payment charges due on any contributions.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to amend paragraphs 43(8)(c) and (d) of Act 452 to allow_
a person, whether or not he is an employer or employee within the meaning
of Act 452, to elect to pay monthly contributions. An example of this category
would be a housewife.

9. _Clause 8_ seeks to introduce a new Part Vb into Act 452. The new Part
contains four new provisions namely sections 53c, 53d, 53e and 53f.

The proposed section 53c provides that the contributions of a member of

the Fund affected by the transfer into the account of another member of the
Fund under Part Vb shall be the employee’s contributions of the first mentioned
member of the Fund.

The proposed section 53d provides that a member of the Fund who fulfills

the conditions and criteria as specified in the Eighth Schedule may elect to
transfer his contributions at the rate specified in the Eighth Schedule into
the account of another member of the Fund who is or are his lawful wife or
wives.

The proposed section 53e further provides that upon the occurrence of any

circumstances specified in the Eighth Schedule, a member of the Fund who
has elected to transfer his contributions under section 53d may apply to the
Board to cease the transfer.

Currently, under the Eighth Schedule, transfer of contributions is allowed

for the purpose of transferring contributions of a member of the Fund into the
account of his lawful wife or wives, who is or are also a member or members
of the Fund. If the marriage between him and his wife has been dissolved or
his wife has died, the member of the Fund may apply to cease the transfer.

The proposed section 53f seeks to empower the Board to re-transfer any

contributions transferred, including any dividend, into the account of the member
of the Fund who has elected to transfer the contributions if the contributions
have been erroneously transferred.

10. _Clause 9 seeks to amend subsection 55a(2) of Act 452 to enable_

a member of the Fund who has attained the age of fifty-five years to withdraw
all sums of money standing to his credit under section 54 of Act 452 if he is
physically or mentally incapacitated from engaging in an employment or he
is not a Malaysian citizen and is about to leave Malaysia.

11. _Clause 10 seeks to amend section 55b of Act 452 to introduce a new_

subsection 55b(3) into Act 452. The proposed subsection (3) provides that
section 55b of Act 452 shall not apply to a member of the Fund who has
attained the age of fifty-five years who falls within the category of the member
of the Fund under paragraph 54(1)(c) or _(e) or paragraph 70c(1)(b) or_ _(c)._

12. _Clause 11 seeks to introduce a new Eighth Schedule to deal with matters_

relating to transfer of contributions under Part Vb.

13. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Explanatory Statement

are minor or consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
