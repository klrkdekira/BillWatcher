A BILL

_i n t i t u l e d_

An Act to amend the Poisons Act 1952.

[ ]

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:**

**Short title and commencement**

**1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Poisons (Amendment)**
Act 2019.

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed
by the Minister by notification in the _Gazette_ and the Minister
may appoint different dates for the coming into operation of
different provisions of this Act.

**Amendment of long title**

**2. The Poisons Act 1952 [Act 366], which is referred to as**
the “principal Act” in this Act, is amended in the long title
by inserting after the words “use of poisons” the words “, and
psychoactive plants, and the planting and cultivation of psychoactive
plants”.

“Control of planting or cultivation of psychoactive plants

**30a. (1) No person shall plant or cultivate any psychoactive**
plant.

(2) Any person who contravenes subsection (1) shall be
guilty of an offence and shall, on conviction, be liable to
a fine not exceeding two hundred thousand ringgit or to
imprisonment for a term not exceeding seven years or to both.

**Possession, import, export, etc., of psychoactive plants**

**30b. (1) No person shall possess, import, export, compound,**
mix, sell, supply or use any psychoactive plant otherwise
than in accordance with any regulations made under this Act.

(2) Any person who is found to have in his custody or
under his control any psychoactive plant shall be deemed to
have been in possession of the plant and to have known the
nature of the plant, until he proves to the contrary.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Poisons Act 1952 (“Act 366”) to expand the
scope of Act 366 to include the control of the planting, cultivation, importation,
exportation, compounding, mixing, selling, supply, possession or use of any
psychoactive plant. This Bill also seeks to enhance certain penalties in Act 366
relating to the use of poisons and psychotropic substances. Other important
features of the proposed Act are to provide flexibility in the handling of poison
by allowing the use of document in electronic form and retail sale of poison
by registered pharmacist at or from a premises where a licensed pharmacist
is licensed to retail poisons or at or from a premises specified in a license
issued under Act 366.

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and seeks to empower the Minister to_
appoint the date of commencement of the proposed Act including the power
to appoint different dates for the coming into operation of different provisions
of the proposed Act.

3. _Clauses 2 and 3 seek to amend the long title and the short title, respectively,_
of Act 366 to reflect the expanded scope of Act 366.

4. _Clause 4_ seeks to provide that all references to the Poisons Act 1952 in
any written law or document shall, after the proposed Act comes into operation,
be construed as references to the Poisons and Psychoactive Plants Act 1952.

5. _Clause 5 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 366 to introduce new definitions_
and to amend the existing definition of certain terms used in Act 366.

6. Clause 7 seeks to introduce a new section 4a into Act 366 to enable
resolution to be passed without meeting subject to certain conditions.

7. _Clause 8 seeks to amend paragraph 7(3)(b) of Act 366 to exempt the_
sale or supply of any poison by a person employed in any hospital or other
institutions at which animal ailments are treated from the application of Act 366
if such sale or supply was made in the course of his duty for the use in the
wards, operation theaters or other sections thereof.
