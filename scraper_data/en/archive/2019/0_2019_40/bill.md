A BILL

_i n t i t u l e d_

An Act to amend the Petroleum (Income Tax) Act 1967.

[ ]

**ENACTED** by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:

**Short title**

**1.** This Act may be cited as the Petroleum (Income Tax)
(Amendment) Act 2019.

**General amendment**

**2.** The Petroleum (Income Tax) Act 1967 [Act 543], which is
referred to as the “principal Act” in this Act, is amended by
substituting for the word “Clerk” wherever appearing in section 42
including the shoulder note, sections 44, 45 and the Third Schedule
the word “Secretary”.

**Amendment of section 2**

**3.** Section 2 of the principal Act is amended—

_(a)_ by deleting the definition of “Clerk”; and

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Petroleum (Income Tax) Act 1967 [Act 543].

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title of the proposed Act._

3. Clause 2 seeks to substitute for the word “Clerk” the word “Secretary”
wherever appearing in section 42 including the shoulder note, sections 44, 45
and the Third Schedule to Act 543.


-----

4. _Clause 3_ seeks to amend subsection 2(1) of Act 543 to introduce the
definition of “Secretary”.

5. _Clause 4_ seeks to amend the Third Schedule to Act 543.

_Paragraph 4(a)_ seeks to substitute for paragraph 1a of the Third Schedule

to Act 543 to empower the Chairman to determine the sitting of an appeal
to be heard by a Special Commissioner sitting alone if the Chairman deems
it fit for the expeditious and efficient conduct of the appeal. Currently, the
hearing of the appeal is by three Special Commissioners.

_Paragraph 4(b) seeks to introduce a new paragraph_ 1b into the Third

Schedule to Act 543 to allow for appeal proceedings, with the consent of
the parties, to be continued or to be heard afresh by the remaining Special
Commissioners if any of the three Special Commissioners hearing an appeal
is unable to complete the hearing of the appeal due to expiration of his term
of appointment or for other reasons. The proposed new subparagraph 1b also
seeks to allow for appeal proceedings, with the consent of the parties, to
be continued or to be heard afresh by another Special Commissioner if the
Special Commissioner hearing an appeal is unable to complete the hearing of
the appeal due to expiration of his term of appointment or for other reasons.

_Paragraph 4(e) seeks to substitute paragraph 29 of the Third Schedule to_

Act 543 whereas _paragraph 4(f)_ seeks to introduce new paragraphs 29a, 29b
and 29c into the Third Schedule to Act 543 to provide for the procedures for
the filing of an appeal to the High Court by the party aggrieved by a deciding
order of the Special Commissioners.

_Paragraph 4(h)_ seeks to substitute paragraph 32a of the Third Schedule

to Act 543 to provide that the appellant shall pay for the cost of the notes
of proceedings or other documents at such rate as may be prescribed by the
Minister.

6. _Clause 5_ seeks to provide for savings provision.

7. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Explanatory Statement
are minor or consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
