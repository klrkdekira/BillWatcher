# intituled

An Act to amend the Income Tax Act 1967, the Petroleum (Income
Tax) Act 1967, the Stamp Act 1949 and the Sales Tax Act 1972.

ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:

CHAPTER I

PRELIMINARY

## Short title

1. This Act may be cited as the Finance Act 1999.

## Amendments of Acts

Tax) Act 1967 [Act 543], the Stamp Act 1949 [Act 378] and the
Sales Tax Act 1972 [Act 64] are amended in the manner specified
in Chapters II, III, IV and V respectively.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Income Tax Act 1967, the Petroleum (Income
Tax) Act 1967, the Stamp Act 1949 and the Sales Tax Act 1972.

CHAPTER II

AMENDMENTS TO THE INCOME TAX ACT 1967

The Income Tax Act 1967 is referred to in this Chapter as "Act 53".

2, _Clause 4 of the Bill seeks to amend section 46 of Act 53 to provide for_

an increase in the personal deduction allowed for an individual or a Hindu

joint family from four thousand ringgit to eight thousand ringgit. In the case

of an individual, the personal deduction is given to the individual in respect
of himself and his dependent relatives (if any). This amendment is effective
from the year of assessment 2000 on a current year basis.

3. _Clause 5 of the Bill seeks to amend section 49 of Act 53. Subsection_

49(1B) is amended to provide that the deduction of two thousand ringgit given
to an individual for any premium paid by him for the purchase of insurance
on education or for medical benefits is to be increased to three thousand ringgit.

A new subsection 49(lc) is introduced to provide that a deduction of one

thousand ringgit is to be given to an individual for any premium paid by him
for the purchase of an insurance policy determined by the Employees Provident
Fund Board utilizing the amount standing to his credit in the Employees Provident
Fund. If subsection 50(2) of Act 53 applies, a further deduction of one thousand
ringgit is to be given but if the wife has no total income, the total deduction
allowed under this subsection shall not exceed one thousand ringgit.


-----

_Finance_

This amendment is effective from the year of assessment 2000 on a current

year basis.

4. _Clause 7 of the Bill seeks to delete section 60D of Act 53. It is proposed_

to exempt venture capital companies from income tax by orders made under
section 127 of Act 53. This amendment is effective from the year of assessment
2000 on a current year basis.

5. _Clause 8 of the Bill seeks to amend section 107B of Act 53. The proviso_

to subsection 107B(2) is amended to provide that a person who is required to
make payment by instalments on account of tax may apply to the Director
General not later than the thirtieth day of June to vary the amount of instalments
and the number of instalments. Before this proposed amendment, a person was
allowed to do so before the fifteenth day of April. This amendment is effective
from the year of assessment 2000 on a current year basis.

6. _Clause 9 of the Bill seeks to amend Parts I and IV of Schedule 1 to Act_

53. The proposed amendment to Part I of Schedule 1 seeks to provide for the
rates of tax in respect of resident persons to range from 0 to 29 per cent. This
represents a reduction in the existing rates of tax in respect of resident persons.
In relation to non-resident persons other than companies, the rate of tax is
reduced from 30 per cent to 29 per cent. The proposed amendment to Part IV
of Schedule I is to reduce the existing rates of tax for co-operative societies
which range from 0 to 30 per cent to range from 0 to 29 per cent. This
amendment is effective from the year of assessment 2000 on a current year
basis.

7. _Clause 10 of the Bill seeks to amend Schedule 3 to Act 53 by introducing_

new paragraphs 16A and 67B into Schedule 3. Paragraph 16A provides that a
person who has incurred qualifying building expenditure on the construction
of a building to which paragraph 67B applies where at the end of the basis
period for a year of assessment the building was on lease to the Government
is to be given an allowance equal to three-fiftieths or such other prescribed
fraction of that expenditure.

Paragraph 67B provides that a building constructed on a build-lease-transfer

basis pursuant to an agreement entered into between a person and the Government
shall, subject to the approval of the Minister of Finance, be treated as an
industrial building for the purposes of giving the allowance.

This amendment is effective from the year of assessment 2000 on a preceding

year basis.

8. Clause 11 of the Bill seeks to amend paragraph 32A of Schedule 6 to Act
53 to increase the exemption in respect of income earned by music composers

and translators to RM20,000. This amendment is effective from the year of
assessment 2000 on a current year basis.

CHAPTER III

**AMENDMENTS TO THE PETROLEUM (INCOME TAX) ACT 1967**

The Petroleum (Income Tax) Act 1967 is referred to in this Chapter as "Act
543".

9. _Clause 13 of the Bill seeks to amend section 13A of Act 543 to provide_

that the disposal value of a vested asset shall not be treated as gross income
of Petroleum Nasional Berhad or the Malaysia-Thailand Joint Authority where
the vested asset that has been disposed of continues to be used for petroleum
operations by a chargeable person under another petroleum agreement and that
chargeable person has not incurred qualifying expenditure in respect of that
asset. This amendment is effective from the year of assessment 2000.

10. _Clause 14 of the Bill seeks to amend the Second Schedule to Act 543._

Paragraphs 8, 12, 13 and 14 of the Second Schedule are amended to enable
the Minister of Finance to prescribe the rates for initial allowances in respect
of plant and machinery as well as annual allowances for plant, machinery,
fixed off-shore platforms and industrial buildings. These amendments shall be
deemed to have been effective from the year of assessment 1996.

Paragraphs 2lA and 23A are introduced into the Second Schedule to Act 543

to provide for the transfer of the residual expenditure of a vested asset under
the first petroleum agreement to the second petroleum agreement where—

_(a) the relationship specified in subsubparagraph 21A(a), (b), (c) or (d)_

exists between the disposer and the acquirer of a vested asset;

_(b) the vested asset continues to be used for petroleum operations by a_

chargeable person under the second petroleum agreement; and

_(c) the chargeable person has not incurred qualifying expenditure in respect_

of the vested asset.

This amendment is effective from the year of assessment 2000.

Subsubparagraph 4l(b) of the Second Schedule to Act 543 is amended to

provide that the disposer of a vested asset shall not be entitled to a claim for
balancing allowance. This amendment is effective from the year of assessment
2000.

CHAPTER IV

AMENDMENT TO THE STAMP ACT 1949

11. _Clause 16 of the Bill seeks to amend the Second Schedule to the Stamp_

Act 1949 to provide that an immigration officer is required to cancel the
adhesive stamps on security bonds for the grant of a Professional Visit Pass
for an Artiste, a Social Visit Pass and a Work Permit Pass. This amendment
is effective upon the coming into operation of the proposed Act.
