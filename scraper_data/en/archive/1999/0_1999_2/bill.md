An Act to amend the Customs Act 1967.

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:**

**Short title**

1. This Act may be cited as the Customs (Amendment) Act 1999.

**Amendment of section 2**

2. The Customs Act l967[Act 235], which in this Act is referred
to as the "principal Act" is amended in section 2—

_(a) in subsection (1) by substituting for the definition of_

"petroleum" the following definition:

' "petroleum" means any mineral oil or relative

hydrocarbon in its solid, liquid or gaseous form existing
in its natural condition and includes casing head
petroleum spirit, bituminous shales, other stratified
deposits from which oil can be extracted commercially
and petroleum products obtained from the process of
manufacture;'; and

_(b) in subsection (2) by inserting after the word "aircraft"_

the words ", pipeline".

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Customs Act 1967("Act 235").

2. _Clause I deals with the short title._

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 235—_

_(a) to introduce a new definition for "petroleum"; and_

_(b) to widen the scope "under customs control" for the purpose of Act_
235 to encompass liquid stored in pipeline.


-----

## Bill

4. Clause 3 seeks to introduce into Act 235 a new section 16 to enable
importers to apply for duty drawback claims where duty has been paid under
protest, within one year after the decision on classification or valuation on the
goods is made known to the claimant.

5. Clause 4 seeks to introduce into Act 235 a new section 17A to prevent
owners of firms, director of companies or anyone who have not settled customs
duties from leaving the country.

6. Clause 5 seeks to amend subsection 65B(1) of Act 235 to ensure that
liquidators pay any customs duties that are payable. Any liquidator who fails
to comply with subsection (1) shall be guilty of an offence.

7. Clause 6 seeks to amend subsection 65c(l) of Act 235 to ensure that
receivers pay any customs duties that are payable. Any receiver who fails to
comply with subsection (1) shall be guilty of an offence.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to amend section 99 of Act 235 with the aim of promoting_

the use of the local ports and airports.

9. Clause 8 seeks to introduce into Act 235 a new section 102 to provide
for the service of notices or documents to companies, firms or associations.

10. Clause 9 seeks to introduce into Act 235 a new section 122D to provide

for the certificate of accuracy of any measuring device for petroleum by any
officer responsible under any written law for such measurement to be accepted
by the courts.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
