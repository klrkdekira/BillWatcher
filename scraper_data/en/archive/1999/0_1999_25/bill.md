EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

1. This Bill seeks to amend the Sales Tax Act 1972 ("Act 64").

2. _Clause 1 seeks to provide for the short title and the commencement of the_

proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 defines "appointed date" for the purpose of the proposed Act to_

mean 1 January 2000.


-----

_Sales Tax (Amendment)_

_4._ _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 64 by extending the definition_

of "manufacture" to incorporate the manufacture of petroleum and to introduce
a new definition for "petroleum".

5. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 16 of Act 64 to allow sales tax to be_

computed on the quantity of goods sold, used or disposed of, or imported, in
addition to being computed on sale value.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to amend subsection 17(1) of Act 64 to require quantities_

of goods sold to be recorded in invoices to be issued.

7. _Clauses 6 and 7 seek to amend subsections 69(1) and 70(1) of Act 64_

respectively. The amendments seek to require liquidators and receivers of importers
of goods including petroleum, for which sales tax are payable, to give notice
to the Director General of Customs within fourteen days of their appointment,
to set aside a sufficient sum out of any assets to pay sales tax before disposing
of such assets and to pay the sales tax payable. Currently, only liquidators and
receivers of licensed manufacturers are subject to these obligations.

8. _Clause 8 seeks to introduce a new Part XVIII to Act 64 to deal specially_

with petroleum. The new Part XVIII introduces new sections 91 to 95. Section
91 states that Act 64 applies to petroleum subject to sections 92 to 95. Section
92 empowers the Minister to make an order varying, in respect of petroleum,
the period for sales tax to be due and payable and the taxable period as well
as to attach conditions to such variations. Section 93 permits any person to
obtain delivery of imported taxable petroleum from the control of Customs even
if any part or all of the sales tax on such goods have not been paid. Section
94 does not permit drawback in respect of petroleum. Section 95 provides that
for the purpose of petroleum the special provisions in Parts XIV and XVII
relating to Labuan and Langkawi in Act 64 are to be of no effect, and petroleum
in Labuan and Langkawi will be subject to the rest of that Act.

9. _Clause 9 deal.s with the transitional provisions for petroleum that are subject_

to excise duties under the Excise Act 1976 when the proposed Act comes into
operation on 1 January 2000.

_F1NANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
