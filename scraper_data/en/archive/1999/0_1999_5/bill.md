# intituled

An Act to amend the Sales Tax Act 1972.

1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Sales Tax (Amendment) Act

1999.

(2) This Act shall come into operation on a date to be appointed

by the Minister by notification in the Gazette, and the Minister
may appoint different dates for different provisions of this Act.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Sales Tax Act 1972 ("Act 64").

2. _Clause 1 seeks to provide for the short title and commencement of the_

proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 64 to introduce the definition of_

"surcharge" into that Act.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 5 of Act 64 to require an officer of sales_

tax to produce on demand a badge or an authority card as he has been directed
to carry under subsection 9(1) of the Customs Act 1967.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to introduce a new subsection 7(3) into Act 64 to provide_

for the sale value of goods where the goods are manufactured on behalf of any
supplier of taxable goods from taxable goods supplied by that supplier and
where the goods so manufactured are returned to that supplier.

6. Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 12A into Act 64 which provides
that if the Director General is satisfied that any separation of business activities
is artificial, he may direct that the persons named in the direction be treated
as a single taxable person.


-----

7. _Clause 6 seeks to introduce a new subsection (4) into section 13A of Act_

64 which provides that before the Director General revokes a licence under
subsection 13A(2), he shall serve a notice of revocation of licence on the person
to whom the licence was issued.

_Clause 6 also seeks to introduce new subsections (5) and (6) into section 13A_

of Act 64 to require the Director General, before he revokes a licence under
subsection 13A(3), to allow representation to be made by the person to whom
the licence was issued. If after considering the representation the Director
General is satisfied that the licence should be revoked, he shall serve upon that
person a notice of revocation of the licence.

8. Clause 7 seeks to introduce a new section 1 7 A into Act 64 to provide for
the production of invoices by computer.

9. _Clause 8 seeks to amend subsection 23(1) of Act 64 so that any surcharge_

accruing under Act 64 may be recovered as a civil debt due to the Government.
_Clause 8 also seeks to introduce a new subsection 23(3) into Act 64 to provide_
that a penalty or surcharge imposed under Act 64 will not be subject to a
limitation period of one year from the date on which the cause of action accrued.

This clause also seeks to introduce new subsections (4) and (5) into section

23. The new subsection (4) deals with the amount of sales tax that is recoverable
from a taxable person who issued an invoice. The new subsection (5) enables
the Director General to recover sales tax from persons who have issued an
invoice charging an amount which purports to be sales tax.

10. _Clause 9 seeks to amend paragraph 24(b) of Act 64 to increase the rate_

of penalty payable from two per cent to ten per cent.

11. _Clause 10 seeks to amend section 26 of Act 64 to make the directors of_

a company, partners of a firm or members of an association of persons, together
with the company, firm or association of persons liable to pay a penalty or
surcharge, jointly and severally liable for the penalty or surcharge.

12. Clause 11 seeks to introduce a new section 27A into Act 64 to enable any

sales tax, penalty, surcharge or other moneys payable under Act 64 to be
recovered from any person who is believed to be about to leave the Federation
before paying any sales tax, penalty, surcharge or other moneys payable by him.

13. _Clause 12 seeks to amend subsection 28(1) of Act 64 to provide that_

where any surcharge is due and payable by a taxable person, the Director
General may by notice in writing require any person owing money to the taxable
person to pay such moneys to him.

14. _Clause 13 seeks to amend subsection 30(1) of Act 64 to provide for the_

recovery of penalties that are unpaid, short paid or erroneously refunded. This
_clause also seeks to amend subsection 30(lB) of Act 64 to provide for the_
imposition of surcharge if any one of the instalment payments authorized under
this section is not paid on its due date.


-----

15. _Clause 14 seeks to introduce a new section 31B into Act 64 to allow a_

licensed manufacturer to deduct sales tax from the amount payable in a tax
return for which credit notes have been issued for taxable goods returned by
the purchaser or discounts subsequently given to the purchaser.

16. _Clause 15 seeks to amend section 32 of Act 64 to empower the Director_

General to reject an application for the refund of sales tax or penalty that has
been overpaid or erroneously paid on the grounds that it would unjustly enrich
the claimant.

17. _Clause 16 seeks to introduce two new subsections, namely subsections_

(2A) and (2B), into section 36 of Act 64 to allow any senior officer of sales tax

the right to be given access to any recorded information or computerized data,
whether stored in a computer or otherwise, and to inspect and check the operation
of any computer which is or has been in use in connection with that information
or data.

18. _Clause 17 seeks to amend paragraph 43(a) of Act 64 which provides that_

any person who fails or refuses to comply with section 12A or section 17A is
guilty of an offence. This clause also seeks to insert a new paragraph (ea) into
section 43 which provides that the contravention of subsection 63A(2) of Act
64 is an offence.

19. Clause 18 seeks to introduce a new section 43A into Act 64 which provides
that any person who assists any other person to evade sales tax commits an
offence.

20. _Clause 19 seeks to amend section 44 of Act 64 to provide that any person_

who aids, abets, assists, incites or induces any other person to commit an
offence under section 43 is guilty of an offence.

21. _Clause 20 seeks to amend subsection 57(1) of Act 64 to increase the_

maximum amount which can be accepted for the compounding of offences from
one thousand ringgit to five thousand ringgit.

22. _Clause 21 seeks to introduce a new section 59A into Act 64 to empower_

the courts to order any person found guilty of an offence under section 43 or
43A to pay to the Director General the amount of any sales tax due and payable,
or any penalty payable under Act 64.

23. _Clause 22 seeks to amend section 61 of Act 64 to empower the Minister_

to make regulations with regard to the production of invoices by computer and
credit notes.

24. _Clause 23 seeks to amend section 62 of Act 64 to provide for the service_

of notices, directions or other documents on companies, firms and associations
of persons, apart from individual persons.

25. _Clause 25 seeks to introduce a new section 63A into Act 64. That section_

provides that other than the persons stated in section 63, no person can transact
business on behalf of a taxable person except on matters with regard to refund,


-----

remission, exemption or any other matters as approved by the Director General.
This clause also sets out certain conditions that need to be fulfilled by a person
who transacts business on behalf of the taxable person.

26. _Clause 26 seeks to amend section 69 of Act 64 to provide that a liquidator_

of a company commits an offence if he fails to set aside and pay the sales tax
payable by the company.

27. _Clause 27 seeks to amend section 70 of Act 64 to make a receiver of a_

licensed manufacturer guilty of an offence if he fails to set aside and pay the
sales tax payable by the licensed manufacturer.

28. Other amendments not specifically referred to are minor or consequential

in nature.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
