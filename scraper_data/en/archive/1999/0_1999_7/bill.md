_Bill_

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Excise Act 1976 ("Act 176").

2. Clause I seeks to provide for the short title and the commencement of the
proposed Act.

3. Clause 2 seeks to amend subsection 2(1) of Act 176 to provide for the
definition of "licensed carrier" and to amend the definition of "petroleum".

4. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 5 of Act 176 to require an officer of excise_

to produce, on demand, a badge or an authority card as he has been directed
to carry under subsection 9(1) of the Customs Act 1967.

5. Clause 6 seeks to introduce a new section 15A into Act 176 to enable any
excise duty payable under the Act to be recovered from any person believed
to be leaving Malaysia before paying the excise duty payable by him.

6. Clause 7 seeks to amend section 23A of Act 176 to provide that the liquidator
of a company commits an offence against the Act if he fails to set aside and
pay the excise duty that is payable by the company.

7. Clause 8 seeks to amend section 23B of Act 176 to provide that the receiver
of the property of a company commits an offence against the Act if he fails
to set aside and pay the excise duty that is payable by the company.

8. _Clause 9 seeks to amend section 42 of Act 176 to provide for the service_

of notices or other documents on companies, firms and association of persons,
apart from individual persons.

9. Other amendments not specifically referred to in this Statement are minor

or consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL_ **_IMPLICATIONS_**

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
