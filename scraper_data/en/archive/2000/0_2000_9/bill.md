PART II

PROTECTION OF GEOGRAPHICAL INDICATIONS

**Protection of geographical indications**

**3. Protection under this Act shall be given to a geographical**
indication-

_(a) regardless whether or not the geographical indication is_
registered under this Act; and

_(b) as against another geographical indication which, although_
literally true as to the country, territory, region or locality
in which the goods originate, falsely represents to the
public that the goods originate in another country, territory,
region or locality.

**Exclusion from protection**

**4. Notwithstanding section 3, the following shall not be protected**
as geographical indications:

_(a) geographical indications that do not correspond to the_
meaning of "geographical indication" as defined in section
2;

_(b) geographical indications which are contrary to public_
order or morality;

_(c) geographical indications which are not or have ceased to_
be protected in their country or territory of origin; or

_(d) geographical indications which have fallen into disuse in_
their country or territory of origin.

**Institution of proceedings for injunction and damages**

**5.** (1) Any interested person may institute proceedings in the
Court to prevent, in respect of geographical indications-

_(a) the use in the course of trade of any means in the designation_
or presentation of any goods that indicates or suggests,
in a manner which misleads the public as to the geographical

origin of the goods, that the goods in question originate
in a geographical area other than the true place of origin;

_(b) any use in the course of trade which constitutes an act_
of unfair competition within the meaning of Article IObis
of the Paris Convention as set out in the Schedule;

(c) any use in the course of trade of a geographical indication
which, although literally true as to the country, territory,
region or locality in which the goods originate, falsely
represents to the public that the goods originate in another
country, territory, region or locality; or

_(d) any use in the course of trade of a geographical indication_

use of the geographical indication and award any damages and any
other legal remedy or relief as it deems fit.

**Failure to take action**

**6.** (1) No action under section 5 shall be brought against a person
for the use of a trade mark which contains or consists of a geographical
indication after the expiry of five years-

_(a) from the date such use by that person or his predecessor_
in title has become generally known in Malaysia; or

_(b) from the date of registration of the trade mark by that_
person under the Trade Marks Act 1976 [Act 175],

whichever is earlier.

(2) Subsection (1) shall not apply where the trade mark was
used or registered in bad faith.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

**This Bill seeks to make provisions for the protection of geographical indications**
in Malaysia, in line with Malaysia's obligations under the Agreement on Trade
Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights (TRIPS). TRIPS is an integral
part of the Final Act of the Uruguay Round of the General Agreement of Tariffs
and Trade (GATT) of which Malaysia is a signatory.

2. Part I contains preliminary provisions.

_Clause 1 provides for the short title and seeks to empower the Minister to_
appoint the date of coming into operation of the proposed Act.

_Clause 2 seeks to define certain terms used in the proposed Act._

3. Part II contains provisions relating to the protection of geographical indications
under the proposed Act.

_Clause 3 deals with the availability of protection under the proposed Act._
Paragraph (a) of this clause seeks to provide protection to all geographical
indications irrespective of registration in Malaysia while paragraph (b) seeks
to provide protection as against the use of geographical indications which,
though literally true as to the origin of the goods in question, falsely represent
to the public that the goods originate in another country, territory, region or
locality.

_Clause 4 lists geographical indications which are not protected under the_
proposed Act. They are set out in four categories, that is, geographical indications
which do not fall within the meaning of the definition of geographical indication
in the proposed Act, geographical indications that are contrary to public order
or morality and, in line with Article 24.9 of the TRIPS Agreement, geographical
indications which are no longer protected or have fallen into disuse in their
**country of origin.**

_Clause 5 seeks to provide that civil proceedings may be instituted to prevent_
unlawful use of geographical indications. This clause also makes clear that the
**Court may, in addition to issuing an injunction to prevent such unlawful use,**
award or grant any other civil remedies that may be appropriate.

_Clause 6 seeks to provide that no action can be brought against a person for_
**the use of a trade mark which contains or consists of a geographical indication**
after the expiry of five years where the trade mark is used in good faith.

_Clause 7 deals with homonymous geographical indications for wines and is_
in line with Article 23.3 of the TRIPS Agreement. It envisages that concurrent
use of such indications may be permitted but that use would be regulated in
order to ensure equitable treatment of the producers and to ensure that the
public are not misled.

_Clause 28 seeks to safeguard the prior use of a particular geographical_
indication of another country identifying wines or spirits in connection with
goods and services by any national of Malaysia who have used that geographical
indication in a continuous manner with regard to the same or related goods and
services in Malaysia for at least 10 years preceding 15 April 1994 or in good
faith preceding that date. The proposed Act shall not prejudice the registrability
or the validity of the registration or the right to use any trade mark on the basis
that the trade mark is identical with or similar to a geographical indication.

_Clause 29 seeks to protect the right of use by any person of a personal name_
except where the name is used in such a manner to mislead the public.

8. Part VII deals with miscellaneous provisions such as the power to appeal
against the decision of the Registrar and the power of the Minister to make
regulations for the purpose of carrying into effect the provisions of the proposed
Act.
