EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Income Tax Act 1967, the Petroleum (Income
Tax) Act 1967 and the Stamp Act 1949.

CHAPTER II

AMENDMENTS TO THE INCOME TAX ACT 1967

The Income Tax Act 1967 is referred to in this Chapter as "Act 53".

_2._ _Clause 5 of the Bill seeks to amend section 46 of Act 53 to provide for_
an increase in the personal deduction allowed for an individual or a Hindu
joint family from four thousand ringgit to eight thousand ringgit. In the case
of an individual, the personal deduction is given to the individual in respect


-----

_3._ _Clause 6 of the Bill seeks to amend section 49 of Act 53. Subsection_
49(1B) is amended to provide that the deduction of two thousand ringgit given
to an individual for any premium paid by him for the purchase of insurance on
education or for medical benefits is to be increased to three thousand ringgit.

A new subsection 49(1c) is introduced to provide that a deduction of one
thousand ringgit is to be given to an individual for any premium paid by him
for the purchase of an insurance policy determined by the Employees Provident
Fund Board utilizing the amount standing to his credit in the Employees Provident
Fund. If subsection 50(2) of Act 53 applies, a further deduction of one thousand
ringgit is to be given but if the wife has no total income, the total deduction
allowed under this subsection shall not exceed one thousand ringgit.

This amendment is effective from the year of assessment 2000 on a current
year basis.

_4._ _Clause 8 of the Bill seeks to delete section 60D of Act 53. It is proposed_
to exempt venture capital companies from income tax by orders made under
section 12iof Act 53. This amendment is effective from the year of assessment
2000 on a current year basis.

_5._ _Clause 9 of the Bill seeks to amend section 101 of Act 53. The proposed_
amendment seeks to require the Director General to carry out a review of an
assessment within twelve months from the date of receipt of the notice of
appeal or within such extended period as may be granted by the Minister,
which shall not exceed six months from the date of expiry of the twelve-month
period. This amendment is effective upon the coming into operation of the
proposed Act.

_6._ _Clause 10 of the Bill seeks to amend section 102 of Act 53 to require the_
Director General to send an appeal forward to the Special Commissioners
within twelve months from the date of receipt of the notice of appeal or within
such extended period as may be granted by the Minister if the Director General
is of the opinion that there is no reasonable prospect of settling the matter in
accordance with section 101 of Act 53. This amendment is effective upon the
coming into operation of the proposed Act.

_7._ _Clause 11 of the Bill seeks to amend section 107B of Act 53. The proviso_
to subsection 107B(2) is amended to provide that a person who is required to
make payment by instalments on account of tax may apply to the Director
General not later than the thirtieth day of June to vary the amount of instalments
and the number of instalments. Before this proposed amendment, a person was
allowed to do so before the fifteenth day of April. This amendment is effective
from the year of assessment 2000 on a current year basis.

_8._ _Clause 12 of the Bill seeks to amend Parts I and IV of Schedule 1 to Act_


-----

p g p p
In relation to non-resident persons other than companies, the rate of tax is
reduced from 30 per cent to 29 per cent. The proposed amendment to Part IV
of Schedule 1 is to reduce the existing rates of tax for co-operative societies
which range from 0 to 30 per cent to range from 0 to 29 per cent. This
amendment is effective from the year of assessment 2000 on a current year
basis.

_9._ _Clause 13 of the Bill seeks to amend Schedule 3 to Act 53 by introducing_
new paragraphs 16A and 67B into Schedule 3. Paragraph 16A provides that a
person who has incurred qualifying building expenditure on the construction
of a building to which paragraph 67B applies where at the end of the basis
period for a year of assessment the building was on lease to the Government
is to be given an allowance equal to three-fiftieths or such other prescribed
fraction of that expenditure.

Paragraph 67B provides that a building constructed on a build-lease-transfer
basis pursuant to an agreement entered into between a person and the Government
shall, subject to the approval of the Minister of Finance, be treated as an
industrial building for the purposes of giving the allowance.

This amendment is effective from the year of assessment 2000 on a preceding
year basis.

_10._ _Clause 14 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new paragraph 42A into_
Schedule 5 to Act 53 to provide for the adoption of the procedure and practice
in force or in use in the subordinate court and the High Court if such procedure
or practice is not expressly provided for in Schedule 5. This amendment is
effective upon the coming into operation of the proposed Act.

11. _Clause 15 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new paragraph 320 into_
Schedule 6 to Act 53 to provide for an exemption in respect of income earned
for musical compositions up to an amount of RM20,000. This amendment is
effective from the year of assessment 2000 on a current year basis.

CHAPTER III

AMENDMENTS TO THE PETROLEUM (INCOME TAX) ACT 1967

The Petroleum (Income Tax) Act 1967 is referred to in this Chapter as "Act
543".

_12._ _Clause 17 of the Bill seeks to amend section 13A of Act 543 to provide_
that the disposal value of a vested asset shall not be treated as gross income
of Petroleum Nasional Berhad or the Malaysia-Thailand Joint Authority where
the vested asset that has been disposed of continues to be used for petroleum
operations by a chargeable person under another petroleum agreement and that


-----

Paragraphs 8, 12, 13 and 14 of the Second Schedule are amended to enable the
Minister of Finance to prescribe the rates for initial allowances in respect of
plant and machinery as well as annual allowances for plant, machinery, fixed
off-shore platforms and industrial buildings. These amendments shall be deemed
to have been effective from the year of assessment 1996.

Paragraphs 21A and 23A are introduced into the Second Schedule to Act 543
to provide for the transfer of the residual expenditure of a vested asset under
the first petroleum agreement to the second petroleum agreement where-

_(a) the relationship specified in subsubparagraph 21A(a), (b), (c) or (d)_
exists between the disposer and the acquirer of a vested asset;

_(b) the vested asset continues to be used for petroleum operations by a_
chargeable person under the second petroleum agreement; and

_(c) the chargeable person has not incurred qualifying expenditure in respect_
of the vested asset.

This amendment is effective from the year of assessment 2000.

Subsubparagraph 41(b) of the Second Schedule to Act 543 is amended to
provide that the disposer of a vested asset shall not be entitled to a claim for
balancing allowance. This amendment is effective from the year of assessment
2000.

CHAPTER IV

AMENDMENTS TO THE STAMP ACT 1949

The Stamp Act 1949 is referred to in this Chapter as "Act 378".

_14._ _Clause 20 of the Bill seeks to amend the First Schedule to Act 378 by_
introducing a new item 49A into the First Schedule to provide that a lease or
an agreement for a lease under the principles of Al-Ijarah of the Syariah law
for the purpose of financing or securing repayment of money will be charged
the same duty that is chargeable on an instrument of security under item 27 of
that Schedule. This amendment shall be deemed to have come into operation
on 30 October 1999.

introducing a new item 19 into the Second Schedule to provide that an immigration
officer is required to cancel the adhesive stamps on security bonds for the
grant of a Professional Visit Pass for an Artiste, a Social Visit Pass and a
Work Permit Pass. This amendment is effective upon the coming into operation
