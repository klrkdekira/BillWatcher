EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to provide for the licensing and control of
entertainment and places of entertainment in the Federal Territory
of Kuala Lumpur.

2. Clause 2 defines words and expressions used in the Act. The
word "entertainment" is broadly defined to include a wide range
of activities in any place of entertainment to which members of the
public are admitted.

3. _Clause 3 makes provision for the Minister to give directions_

to the Commissioner as to the exercise of the powers and
discretions conferred on him.

4. Clause 4 prohibits any entertainment to be provided in any
place of entertainment unless there are licences issued in respect
of that entertainment and that place of entertainment. Contravention
of this provision constitutes an offence which is punishable with
a fine not exceeding twenty thousand ringgit or imprisonment not
exceeding five years or both.

5. Clause 5 sets out the requirements in relation to applications
for an entertainment licence and a licence to open an entertainment
place. The applications are to be made to the Commissioner in the
forms to be prescribed. The Commissioner has the discretion
whether or not to grant a licence. Where an application is granted
the licence will be issued upon payment of the prescribed fee.
Under subclause (5), monies collected from the issuance of licences
are to be paid into the fund constituted under any law relating to
local government.

6. _Clause 6 empowers the Commissioner to require an applicant_

to furnish him with the script (if any) and such other information
as he may require for the purpose of deciding whether or not to
grant a licence.

7. _Clause 7 provides that a licence issued shall be for such_

duration as the Commissioner may determine.

8. _Ciause 8 requires that the licence be exhibited in a conspicuous_

place at the place of entertainment at all times.

9. Clause 9 provides that a licence is not renewable as of right.

10. Clause 10 empowers the Commissioner to require a licensee

to give security for the observance of the conditions and restrictions
of the licence as well as the provisions of the Act or rules made
thereunder. In the event of a breach the security provided may be
forfeited in whole or in part.

11. Clause 11 empowers the Commissioner to revoke an

entertainment licence or a licence to open a place of entertainment,
or both if there has been a breach of the conditions or restrictions
of the licence or a contravention of any of the provisions of the
Act or rules made thereunder. Subclause (2) sets out the manner
in which service of the notice of revocation is to be effected.

12. Appeal to the Minister against a refusal to grant a licence or

a revocation of a licence, or the imposition of conditions or
restrictions on a licence or the forfeiture of security is provided in
_clause 12. Such an appeal must be lodged within thirty days after_
being informed as to the refusal, revocation, imposition of
conditions or restrictions or forfeiture, as the case may be.

13. _Clause 13 prohibits any person to give, distribute, sell or_

offer for sale any admission ticket to an entertainment or to issue
invitations to any entertainment unless a licence has been issued
under clause 4. It also prohibits any person to advertise or publicise
any entertainment unless a similar licence has been issued.

14. _Clause 14 restricts the sale of tickets to an entertainment to_

the box-office, booth, turnstile or counter appointed for the purpose
of sale of tickets.

15. Under clause 15 the Commissioner is given the power to

exempt any entertainment generally or specifically from all or any
of the provisions of the Act on certain grounds.

16. _Clause 16 empowers the Commissioner, any police officer_

not below the rank of Inspector or any authorized person to enter
