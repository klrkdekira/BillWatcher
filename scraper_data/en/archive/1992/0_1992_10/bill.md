EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Telecommunications Act 1950 (in this
Statement referred to as the "Act").

2. Clause 1 provides for the short title.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend section 3 in order to clarify the_
additional requirement for a licence under section 37 whenever the
telecommunications network involves a radiocommunication
station. It also clarifies the need for Ministerial consent whenever
a licence is to be assigned, transferred or sublet.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 35 to include the definition_
of "television receive only station" in the interpretation provision
for Part V and to make consequential amendments thereto.

5. Clause 4 seeks to introduce a new subsection (2A) to section
37 to empower the Minister to exempt either absolutely or


-----

conditionally, any low power radiocommunication apparatus or any
class thereof from the requirement of a licence under that section.

6. Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 3?A to enable the
possibility of implementing systems licensing for certain specific
services in which case the licence fee is collected only from the
radiocommunications network operator, and a subscriber to that
network will not be required to have a separate licence.

7. Clause 6 seeks to amend section 38 to expressly exclude a
"television receive only station" from the application of the proviso
to subsection (1), and to introduce a new subsection (6) for the
admissibility of a certificate given by an authorized telecommunication officer as prima facie evidence of the facts stated
therein.

8. Clause 7 seeks to amend section 40 to empower the Minister
to make regulations for prohibiting or regulating the working or
using of any radiocommunication apparatus in any premises in
Malaysia, and for giving effect to international treaties relating to
radiocommunication to which Malaysia is a party.

9. _Clause 8 seeks to increase the maximum fine which may be_
imposed under section 43 to fifty thousand ringgit in order to be
consistently proportionate with the maximum fine of one hundred
thousand ringgit provided under section 39 of the Act.

10. Clause 9 seeks to introduce a new section 44A to provide for
the powers of examination exerciseable by an authorized
telecommunication officer for the purpose of ensuring an
interference-free environment.

11. Clause 10 seeks to introduce a new section 47 A to provide for
the admissibility of statements in evidence.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 1660.]
