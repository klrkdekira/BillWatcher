EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Criminal Procedure Code (P.M.S.
Cap. 6) ("the Code") to provide for the abolition of preliminary inquiries
in the Magistrate's Court for the purpose of committing an accused
person for trial in the High Court, and trials with jury or trials with
the aid of assessors.

2. _Clause 2 seeks to delete section 8 of the Code whereby hearing_
in camera in a prelirainary inquiry is abolished in view of the fact
that preliminary inquiries have also been abolished by this Bill.

3. Clause 5 seeks to delete Chapters XVII and XVIIA of the Code
whereby provisions relating to preliminary inquiries are abolished.
With the abolition of these provisions there is no necessity for a
preliminary inquiry to be held in a Magistrate's Court for the purpose
of committing an accused person to be tried in the High Court for
offences triable in the High Court.

CRIMINAL PROCEDURE CODE (AMENDMENT) 7

4. Clause 8 provides for the transmission of cases to the High Court,
without holding a preliminary inquiry under Chapter XVII of the
Code, to be tried by such Court.

5. _Clause 9 seeks to amend the heading of Chapter XX of the Code_
to delete the words "WITHOUT ASSESSORS". In view of the abolition
of trials by jury or trials with the aid of assessors, there is no such
special procedure applicable to the High Court.

6. _Clause 11 seeks to delete Chapters XXI, XXII and XXIII of the_
Code whereby provisions relating to trials by jury or trials with aid
of assessors in the High Court are abolished. With this amendment
the trial before a High Court is the same as in a Magistrate's Court
or Sessions Court.

7. Clau.se 21 seeks to amend section 281 of the Code in view of
the creation of the Court of Appeal to provide that the report by the
judge passing the sentence of death is to be forwarded to the Federal
Court only after the Court of Appeal has determined the appeal.

8. _Clause 22 seeks to amend section 288 of the Code to provide_
that for offences under the Penal Code relating to criminal
misappropriation, criminal breach of trust and cheating, whipping shall
be inflicted with a light cane as used for juvenile offenders.

9. Clause 38 seeks to delete Forms XXXII and XXXIX in the Second
Schedule to the Code in view of the abolition of trials by jury or trial
with the aid of assessors and preliminary inquiries.

10. _Clause 39 seeks to provide that any preliminary inquiry, trial_
by jury or trial with the aid of assessors that has been commenced
before or on the commencement of this Act shall be continued or
concluded in all respects as if this Act had not been passed. Any
preliminary inquiry or trial has been commenced when the accused
has appeared before or has been brought before the Magistrate's Court
or the High Court, as the case may be, and evidence has begun to
be adduced in the inquiry or trial.

11. Other amendments to the Code are minor or consequential in
nature.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 1589/IH.]
