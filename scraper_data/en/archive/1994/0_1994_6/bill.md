EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Price Control Act 1946 ("the Act").

2. Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 of the Act to include
definitions for words used in the Act.

3. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 3 of the Act to empower the_
Minister to appoint the Price Controller, the Deputy Price Controller,
Assistant Price Controllers and such other officers as may be
necessary for the purposes of the Act.

4. Clause 4 seeks to re-enact the existing subsection 3(4) on the
National Advisory Council for Consumers' Protection as a new
section 3A.

5. Clause 5 seeks to amend subsection 12(2) to delete the reference
to the Chief Price Inspector. Similar amendments are made in
_clauses 6(a) and 8 whilst in clause 7 both the terms "Chief Price_
Inspector" as well as "Price Inspector" are deleted.

6. Clause 6(b) seeks to amend subsection 14(2) of the Act to
enable the Price Controller to dispose off goods in cases where no
proceedings have been instituted against any person and where the
owner of such goods cannot be found, the matter shall be referred
to a Magistrate for a decision as to the disposal of the goods. Where
proceedings are in fact instituted, the question as to whether the
goods are to be forfeited or released will be determined by the court
before which the prosecution is held.


-----

PRICE CONTROL (AMENDMENT)

7. Clause 9 seeks to introduce a new section 17A to empower the
Price Controller and the Deputy Price Controller to compound
certain offences under the Act and the regulations.

8. Clause 10 seeks to amend section 23 of the Act to confer upon
a Magistrate of the First Class the jurisdiction to try offences under
the Act and the regulations and to impose the full punishment for
such offences.

9. Clause 11 seeks to amend subsection 26(1) of the Act in relation
lo those powers, duties and functions of the Price Controller under
thc Act which cannot be delegated by him.

10. Clause 12 seeks to amend subsection 29(2) to enable the
Minister to prescribe the offences that may be compounded, the
amount of compound and the procedure to be followed in
compounding offences.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 1812.]
