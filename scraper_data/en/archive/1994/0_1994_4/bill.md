EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Merchant Shipping Ordinance 1952 (the
"Ordinance").

2. _Clause 2 seeks to amend certain definitions in section 2 of the_
Ordinance.

3. Clause4 seeks to introduce new sections 58A, 58B and 58c. Anew
form of registry is introduced by the new section 58A, that is, a
terminable certificate of registry for small ships. The new sections 58B
and 58c provide for matters relating to the ownership of a ship and
using any ship which has been granted a terminable certificate of
registry as security for a loan or for other valuable consideration.

4. Clause 6 seeks to amend section 65K of the Ordinance to allow an
appeal to be made against the decision of any person or body of
persons delegated with the powers and duties of the Domestic
Shipping Licensing Board.

5. Clause 7 seeks to delete subsection 65KA(4) since it is no longer
considered necessary in view of the introduction of the new section
65T.

6. Clause 8 seeks to introduce a new section 65u to empower the
Minister to exempt any ship from the provisions of Part 11B of the
Ordinance.

7. _Clause 14 seeks to amend section 474 of the Ordinance to extend_
its requirements on the licensing of boats.

8. Clause 15 seeks to introduce a new section 483c to extend the
provisions on manning in the Ordinance to a boat licensed under Part
XIII.

9. Clause 16 seeks to introduce a new Part XIIIA, The new section
491B of the new Part requires any ship which is engaged or intends to
engage in any of the activities stipulated therein in Malaysian waters
to notify the Director of Marine of such activity. The Director of
Marine may impose the fees or terms and conditions as he thinks fit on
the activity. The new section 491c deals with the powers of the
Director of Marine to carry out investigation of an offence under this
new Part. The new section 49ID seeks to allow the Government to
recover as a civil debt the cost of holding any ship or thing in its
custody in the event of any person being found guilty of any offence
under this new Part.

10. Clause 18 seeks to introduce new section 512A to empower the
Director of Marine to compound any offence under the Ordinance.

11. _Clauses 5,9,10,11,12,13 and 17 seek to make amendments to_
the Ordinance which are consequential or minor in nature.

_FINANCIAL_ **_IMPLICATIONS_**

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
