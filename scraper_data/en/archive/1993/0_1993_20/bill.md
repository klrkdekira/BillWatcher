other usage, shall be deemed to be expressed by such

"pay to the holder", or words to that effect, has been
written over the signature.'.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Penal Code (F.M.S. Cap. 45).

2. Clause 2 seeks to amend section 24 to provide that irrespective
of whether there is actual wrongful loss or gain, a person who does
anything with the intention of causing wrongful gain or loss is said
to do that "dishonestly".

3. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 29 to provide a definition of_

"document" that is consistent with an amendment effected to the
definition of the same word in the Evidence Act 1950.


-----

**10** **BILL**

4. Clause 4 and clause 5 seek to provide for the definition of the
words "unlawful", "agent", "company", "director" and "officer" in
the context of various provisions of the Penal Code.

5. _Clause 6 seeks to amend the scope of the offence of criminal_

misappropriation in section 403 to provide that a person could
commit criminal misappropriation if he dishonestly causes any other
person to dispose of property of another person. The amendment
to section 403 also seeks to enhance the penalty for criminal
misappropriation.

6. Clause 7 seeks to amend section 404 to enhance the penalty
for criminal misappropriation of property belonging to a deceased
person and to effect the amendment consequential to the amendment
made to section 403.

7. Clause 8 seeks to amend section 405 to provide that where a
person who is entrusted with property together with another, and
who dishonestly misappropriates or dishonestly uses or dispose of
that property, either of the two persons has committed criminal
breach of trust.

8. Clauses 9, 10 and II seek to enhance the penalty in sections
406, 407 and 408.

9. Clause 12 seeks to amend section 409 to provide the punishment
for criminal breach of trust committed by an agent as well as to
provide a minimum sentence of imprisonment of two years,

10. Clause 13 seeks to provide for the new sections 409A and 409B.

The new section 409A provides that it is not a defence for any
offence of criminal misappropriation or criminal breach of trust to
show that the appropriation was done openly or openly recorded
and entered into books. Section 409e provides that where in any
proceedings for the offence of criminal misappropriation or criminal
breach of trust it is proved that a person had misappropriated any
property, or used or disposed of the property in violation of a trust,
or had suffered any person so to do, there is a presumption that
he had acted dishonestly until the contrary is proved. This
presumption is also applicable to offences of abetment and attempt
in relation to criminal misappropriation or criminal breach of trust.

11. _Clause 14 seeks to improve the definition of "cheating" in_

section 415 to provide that cheating is committed whether or not
deception was the sole or main inducement.

12. _Clause 15 seeks to enhance the penalty for cheating._
