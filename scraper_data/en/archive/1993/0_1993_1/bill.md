**EXPLANATORY STATEMENT**

This Bill seeks to amend the Penang Commission Act 1955.

1. Clause 1 enables the Minister to appoint the commencement
date of this Bill.

2. Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 of the Act and introduces
a new definition of a "licensed operator".

3. Clause 3 seeks to introduce a new section 14A to provide for
the establishment of a port security force.

4. Clause 4 seeks to substitute section 24(5) with a new section
24(5) to enable the Commission to give various loans to its officers
and servants for such purpose and on such terms and conditions as
the Commission thinks fit.

_5. Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 25(3A) to enable the_
Commission to dispose of any capital asset or any asset kept in the
store which is no longer required by the Commission.

6. Clause 6 seeks to amend section 31(2) to define the "water
authority" as the Penang Water Authority.

7. Clause 7 seeks to delete paragraph (f) of section 32(1) on the
rationale that it should be correctly placed after section 36.

8. Clause 8 seeks to introduce a new section 36A to replace and
restate the provisions of paragraph (f) of section 32(1) which have
been deleted by clause 7,

9. Clause 9 seeks to amend section 37(1) by deleting the words
", and of the auditor appointed by the Minister," as they are now
considered unnecessary.

10. Clause 10 seeks to introduce a new section 38A to allow the
Commission, with the approval of the Minister, to write off any bad
debts.

11. _Clause 11 seeks to delete section 49 as it is now considered_
unnecessary. Therefore, the provisions of the Statutory Bodies
(Accounts and Annual Reports) Act 1980 will apply automatically
to the Commission.

12. Clause 12 seeks to introduce a new section 58A to provide
for the additional duties of the Commission. The Commission may,
with the approval of the Minister, operate any port-related activities.
In the exercise of such duties the Commission may expend such
moneys as are necessary.

23. Clause 23 seeks to introduce a new section 86A. It makes it
an offence for any person to hinder or obstruct any member, officer
or servant of the Commission in the performance or execution of
his duty.

24. _Clause 24 seeks to amend section 87(2) to increase the fine_

25. _Clause 25 seeks to amend section 88 to increase the fine from_

be made under this Act. The penalty shall be a fine not exceeding

recover all expenses incurred for making good any loss or damage

as a civil debt. Section 89B provides for offences committed by a
body corporate including its director, secretary or manager.

90A empowers the General Manager or any person authorised by
him to board any vessel within the port whenever he suspects that

to give orders for scuttling the vessel in the event of any fire breaking
out.

licensed under section 70CA(l).

31. _Clause 31 seeks to amend section 92c to widen its scope in_
order to bring within its ambit any offence committed by any
