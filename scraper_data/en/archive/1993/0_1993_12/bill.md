**EXPLANATORY STATEMENT**

This Bill seeks to amend the Public Health Ordinance of Sabah in
force in the Federal Territory of Labuan as modified by the Federal
Territory of Labuan (Modification of Public Health Ordinance) Order

1985.

2. Clause 1 enables the Minister to appoint the commencement date
of this Bill.

3. Clause 2 seeks to define terms used in the Ordinance.

4. Clause 3 seeks to amend section 111 of the Ordinance as regards
the duty of a local authority. It provides that a local authority is no
longer responsible for sewerage services in its area.

5. Clause 4 seeks to amend section 112 of the Ordinance as regards
sewers. Therefore, a local authority is no longer responsible to
provide and maintain sewers.

6. Clauses 5,6 and 7 seek to amend sections 116,117 and 118 of the
Ordinance by deleting the words "sewers and", "public and private

sewers and" and "sewerage" as they are now considered unnecessary.

_7. Clause 8 seeks to amend section 119 of the Ordinance by deleting_
the words "cesspool or septic tank", "sewerage or" and "sewer or" as
they are now considered unnecessary.

8. Clause 9 seeks to amend section 121 of the Ordinance by deleting
the words "sewers or" as they are now considered unnecessary.

9. Clause 10 seeks to introduce a new provision. The new section

12lA provides that buildings shall not be erected over sewers without

obtaining the consent of the Director General of Sewerage Services.

10. Clause 11 seeks to amend section 122 of the Ordinance by

deleting the words "private sewer or" and paragraph (c) as they are
now considered unnecessary.

11. Clause 12 seeks to amend section 123 of the Ordinance to

provide that any works carried out to abate nuisance shall comply with
the requirements of the Sewerage Services Act 1993.

12. Clause l3 seeks to delete sections 125 and 128 of the Ordinance.

13. Clause 14 seeks to amend section 130 of the Ordinance by
deleting the words "cesspools, septic tanks, private sewers", etc. as


-----

**6** **BILL**

they are now considered unnecessary. Therefore, a local authority is
no longer responsible to provide the service of cleansing septic tanks
or sewers.

14. _Clause 15 seeks to amend section 131 of the Ordinance to_

provide that a local authority can no longer impose the duty of
cleansing septic tanks or sewers on occupiers.

15. _Clause 16 seeks to amend section 132 of the Ordinance as_

regards the power to make by-laws. A local authority no longer has the
power to make any by-laws relating to sewerage services.

_FINANCIAL_ **_IMPLICATIONS_**

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 1764.]
