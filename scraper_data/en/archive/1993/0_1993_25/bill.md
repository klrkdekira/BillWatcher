EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Income Tax Act 1967, the Petroleum
(Income Tax) Act 1967 and the Stamp Act 1949.

CHAPTER II

AMENDMENTS TO THE INCOME TAX ACT 1967

2. Clause 4 seeks to amend the definition of "approved scheme"
to include the Employees Provident Fund. This amendment will be
effective from the 1st June 1991.

3. Clau.se 5 seeks to increase the rebate allowed to an individual
from RM60.00 to RM90.00; the rebate allowed in respect of a wife
is increased from RM30.00 to RM50.00. This amendment will be
effective from the year of assessment 1994.

4. Clause 6(a) seeks to increase contributions by an employer in
respect of an employee's remuneration from 15 per cent to 16 per
cent. Clause 6(b) seeks to introduce a new paragraph (g) into
subsection 34(6) to allow deductions for expenditure incurred on
the provision of public library facilities and in respect of
contributions to libraries in schools and institutions of higher
education. The amount that may be deducted is limited to
RM 100,000.00. This amendment will be effective from the year of
assessment 1994.

_5. Clause 8 seeks to grant double deduction in respect of donations_
made during the period 1st November 1993 to 31si January 1994
to the Government low-cost housing fund managed by the Central
Bank. This clause also seeks to include in the definition of
"organisation" an organisation established for the conservation of
the environment.

6. Clause 9 seeks to amend section 48 to provide for an increase
in relief in respect of a child receiving education at a university
or institution of higher education in Malaysia, to a maximum of four
