EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Co-operative College (Incorporation) Act 1968
(“Act 437”).

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and the provision on commencement of_
the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 437 to introduce new definitions_
into Act 437.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 3 of Act 437 to provide for the establishment_
of the “Co-operative College of Malaysia” or “Maktab Koperasi Malaysia”
as a body corporate.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 4 of Act 437 to provide a more_
comprehensive provision on the functions for the Co-operative College of
Malaysia.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to insert new sections into Act 437, namely sections 4A_
to 4P.

Section 4A seeks to provide the powers of the Co-operative College of
Malaysia.

Section 4B seeks to provide for the establishment and composition of the
Board of the Co-operative College of Malaysia. This Board is established to
replace the Council of the Co-operative College of Malaysia as the administrative
body of the College.

Section 4C seeks to provide the criteria for disqualification from being a
member of the Board of the Co-operative College of Malaysia.

Section 4D seeks to provide for the temporary exercise of functions of
Chairman during any period of vacancy.

Section 4E deals with vacation of office.

Section 4F deals with the revocation of appointment of a member of the
Board of the Co-operative College of Malaysia.

Section 4G relates to Board meetings and the keeping of minutes.

Section 4H makes provision on the disclosure of interest by a member of
the Board of the Co-operative College of Malaysia.

Section 4I seeks to allow the Board to invite others to attend its
meetings.

Section 4J makes provision on the allowances to be paid to the members
of the Board of the Co-operative College of Malaysia and members of any
committee.

Section 4K provides for the validity of acts and proceedings of the Board of
the Co-operative College of Malaysia should a vacancy in the membership, or
a defect in the constitution, of the Board or an omission, defect or irregularity
not affecting the merits of the case occur.

Section 4L relates to the appointment of the Director General of the
College.

Section 4M makes provision on the appointment of officers and staff as
may be necessary for carrying out the purposes of the College.

Section 4N seeks to enable the College to establish a branch or branches
of the College.

Section 4O relates to the power of the Minister to give directions to the
Board.

Section 4P deals with the common seal of the College.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to insert section 5A into Act 437 to allow the Board to_

the Contributory Provident Fund.

Board, on behalf of the College, to accept any property and money by way
of grant, gift, testamentary disposition, subvention, legacy or otherwise, in
aid of finances of the College.

11. _Clause 13 seeks to insert new sections 12A to 12G into Act 437. Section_

meaning of the Penal Code [Act 574] while discharging his duties under Act 437.

Section 12C imposes an obligation of secrecy in respect of any information
related to the College on the member of the Board, or any officer, staff or
agent of the College, any member of a committee, or any person invited to
attend any meetings of the Board or any committee.

done in good faith.

Section 12F provides for the power of the Board to make rules and
guidelines.

Section 12G provides for the power of the Minister to make regulations.
