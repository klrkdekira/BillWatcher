An Act to amend the Hire-Purchase Act 1967.

[ ]

**ENACTED** by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:

**Short title and commencement**

**1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Hire-Purchase (Amendment)**
Act 2010.

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed
by the Minister by notification in the _Gazette._

**Amendment of long title**

**2.** The Hire-Purchase Act 1967 [Act 212], which is referred to
as “the principal Act” in this Act, is amended by substituting for
the long title the following long title:

“An Act to regulate the form and contents of hire-purchase
agreements, the rights and duties of parties to such agreements
and to make provisions for other matters connected therewith
and incidental thereto.”.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Hire-Purchase Act 1967 (“Act 212”).

2. _Clause 1 of the Bill contains the short title and the provision on the_
commencement date of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 of the Bill seeks to amend the long title of Act 212 to expand_
the scope of Act 212 to include other matters relating to hire-purchase
agreements.

4. _Clause 3 of the Bill seeks to amend section 4A of Act 212 by introducing_
subsection (1A) to require hire-purchase agreements to be either in the national
language or English language.


-----

_Hire-Purchase (Amendment)_ 19

5. _Clause 4 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new subsection (2A) into_
section 4B of Act 212 to clarify further the duties and responsibilities of an
owner to complete hire-purchase agreements and other documents related to
hire-purchase agreements.

6. _Clause 5 of the Bill seeks to introduce new sections 4E, 4F and 4G into_
Act 212. The proposed section 4E seeks to allow the hirer to keep the motor
vehicle registration certificate upon request made to the owner. The proposed
section 4F seeks to prohibit any hire-purchase agreement in respect of altered
or modified motor vehicles. The proposed section 4G seeks to require a
declaration to be made of any defect on second-hand motor vehicles if the
motor vehicles are goods comprised in hire-purchase agreements.

7. _Clause 6 of the Bill seeks to amend section 5 of Act 212 to provide_
more time for an owner to complete and serve hire-purchase agreements and
other related documents on hirers by extending the period of fourteen days to
twenty-one days. The amendment further provides that the failure of an owner
to serve the hire-purchase agreement and other related documents within the
stipulated time is an offence.

8. _Clause 7 of the Bill seeks to amend section 16 of Act 212 to impose new_
conditions on the owner in relation to repossession under this Act.

9. _Clause 8 of the Bill seeks to introduce new sections 17A and 17B into_
Act 212 respectively to require any person who is engaged in repossession
activity to obtain a permit from the Controller and to require an owner to
appoint only permit holders under section 17A to undertake repossession.

10. _Clause 9 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 30A into Act 212_
to regulate booking fees.

11. _Clause 10 of the Bill seeks to introduce new subsections (1A) and (1B)_
into section 31 of Act 212 to require deposit to be collected upon the signing
of hire-purchase agreements and to provide remedy to the hirer in the event the
owner, dealer, agent or person acting on behalf of the owner fails to deliver
the goods intended for the hire-purchase agreement.

12. _Clauses 11 and 12_ of the Bill seek to amend sections 36 and 38 of
Act 212 respectively to provide for an increase in penalty.

13. _Clause 13 of the Bill seeks to amend section 45 of Act 212 to provide_
that a hire-purchase agreement which is not printed in black shall be deemed
not to be in writing.

14. _Clause 14_ of the Bill seeks to amend section 46 of Act 212 to provide
general penalty for bodies corporate and to increase the general penalty for
offences committed under Act 212.

15. _Clause 15 of the Bill seeks to amend section 50 of Act 212 to provide_
that computerized data may also be seized in the exercise of the power of
enforcement under Act 212.

16. _Clause 16 of the Bill seeks to introduce new sections 50A and 50B into_
Act 212 to provide that Assistant Controllers shall have access to computerized
data. The new section 50B seeks to require Assistant Controllers to prepare a
list of things seized.

17. _Clause 17 of the Bill seeks to amend section 51 of Act 212 to increase_
the penalty for non-compliance.

18. _Clause 18 of the Bill seeks to amend section 51B to provide that Assistant_
Controllers shall have the power in relation to police investigation in seizable
cases under the Criminal Procedure Code.

19. _Clause 19 of the Bill seeks to introduce new sections 51C, 51D and 51E_
into Act 212. The proposed section 51C empowers an Assistant Controller to
arrest without warrant any person whom he believes has committed an offence
under Act 212 and such person is likely to abscond. The proposed section 51D
allows an Assistant Controller to order the attendance of persons acquainted
with a case under investigation. The proposed section 51E empowers an
Assistant Controller to examine orally any person supposed to be acquainted
with the facts and circumstances of the case.

20. _Clause 20 of the Bill seeks to substitute section 53 of Act 212 which_

deals with the admissibility of statement in evidence.

21. _Clause 21 of the Bill seeks to substitute section 55 of Act 212 to clarify_
that prosecution for an offence shall be instituted with the consent of the
Public Prosecutor.

22. _Clause 22 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 55A into Act 212_
which deals with the forfeiture of anything seized under Act 212.

23. _Clause 23_ of the Bill seeks to introduce new sections 55B and 55C into
Act 212. The proposed section 55B provides that no costs or damages arising from
the seizure of anything under the Act shall be recoverable in any proceedings
before any court unless the seizure was done without reasonable cause. The
proposed section 55C provides that the costs of holding anything seized and
held in the custody of the Government will be a debt due to the Government
and shall be recoverable accordingly if that person is found guilty.

24. _Clause 24 of the Bill seeks to substitute section 56 of Act 212 to require_
the Controller or, in his absence, the Deputy Controller, to obtain the consent
of the Public Prosecutor prior to compounding offences under Act 212.

25. _Clause 25_ of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 56B into
Act 212 which sets out provisions to protect informers in any civil or criminal
proceedings.

26. _Clause 26 of the Bill seeks to amend section 57 of Act 212 to empower_
the Minister to make regulations relating to the issuance of permits in respect
of repossession activity under Act 212 and to set out the penalties that may
be prescribed in regulations made under Act 212.
