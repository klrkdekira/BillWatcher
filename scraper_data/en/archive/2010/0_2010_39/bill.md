**ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:**

**Short title and commencement**

**1.** (1) This Act may be cited as the Merchant Shipping (Oil
Pollution) (Amendment) Act 2010.

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed
by the Minister by notification in the _Gazette._

**Amendment of long title**

**2.** The Merchant Shipping (Oil Pollution) Act 1994 [Act 515],
which is referred to as the “principal Act” in this Act, is amended
in the long title by inserting after the word “oil” the words “and
bunker oil”.

**3.** The principal Act is amended by substituting for the short
title “Merchant Shipping (Oil Pollution) Act 1994” the short title
“Merchant Shipping (Liability and Compensation for Oil and
Bunker Oil Pollution) Act 1994”.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT


This Bill seeks to amend the Merchant Shipping (Oil Pollution) Act 1994
(“Act 515”) to provide for matters relating to the implementation of the
International Convention on Civil Liability for Bunker Oil Pollution Damage
2001 (“Bunkers Convention”) in Malaysia.

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and provision on the commencement of_
the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend the long title of Act 515 to extend the scope of_
Act 515 to civil liability for bunker oil pollution.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend subsection 1(1) of Act 515 in respect of the short_
title of Act 515. The Act is to be cited as the “Merchant Shipping (Liability
and Compensation for Oil and Bunker Oil Pollution) Act 1994” upon the
coming into operation of the proposed Act.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to provide that any reference to the “Merchant Shipping_
(Oil Pollution) Act 1994” in any written law or document, shall, on the coming
into operation of the proposed Act, be construed as references to the “Merchant
Shipping (Liability and Compensation for Oil and Bunker Oil Pollution) Act
1994”. However, any reference to the “Merchant Shipping (Oil Pollution)
Act 1994” in any proceedings, whether civil or criminal, or any cause of
action commenced, pending or existing before the coming into operation of
this proposed Act, shall, on the coming into operation of this proposed Act,
continue to apply as if such reference had not been amended by this proposed
Act.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 515 to introduce new definitions_
and amend existing definitions for the purposes of the implementation of the
Bunkers Convention.

7. _Clause 6 seeks to amend the heading to Part II of Act 515 to extend its_
scope to provide in respect of civil liability for bunker oil pollution.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to introduce a new section 3A that provides for the owner_
of a ship to be liable for bunker oil pollution from the ship in any area of
Malaysia or any area in any other Bunkers Convention country.

9. _Clause 8_ seeks to amend section 4 of Act 515 to provide for exceptions
from liability for bunker oil pollution damage.


-----

18 _Bill_

10. _Clause 9_ seeks to amend section 5 of Act 515 to specifically provide
for circumstances when liability for oil pollution damage under section 3 of
Act 515 is excluded.

11. _Clause 10 seeks to introduce a new section 5A that establishes the_
circumstances when liability for bunker oil pollution damage under the new
section 3A, as introduced by _clause 7, is excluded._

12. _Clause 11_ seeks to introduce a new section 6A that provides for the
liability of an owner for bunker oil damage under the new section 3A, as
introduced by _clause 7, to be limited in accordance with the Convention on_
Limitation of Liability for Maritime Claims 1976 as amended by the Protocol
of 1996 to amend the Convention on Limitation of Liability for Maritime
Claims 1976.

13. _Clauses 12,_ _13, 14 and_ _15 seek to amend sections 7, 8, 9 and 10 of_
Act 515, respectively, to extend the application of these sections to liability
incurred for bunker oil pollution damage.

14. _Clause 16 seeks to state specifically that section 11 of Act 515 provides for_
compulsory insurance to be maintained by ships in respect of oil pollution.

15. _Clause 17_ introduces a new section 11A that requires all ships with a
gross tonnage of more than 1,000 tons to have compulsory insurance against
pollution from bunker oil. The provision includes the requirement for such
ships to carry a certificate and empowers the Director of Marine to issue such
certificates for Malaysian ships and ships registered in a country which is not
a Bunkers Convention country.

16. _Clause 18 seeks to amend section 12 of Act 515 to provide that the_
Director of Marine may issue a certificate to the registered owner, upon being
satisfied that there is in respect of the ship a valid contract of insurance or
other financial security satisfying the requirements of Article 7 of the Bunkers
Convention.

17. _Clause 19 seeks to amend section 13 of Act 515 to extend the application_
of the section to enforce a claim in respect of a liability incurred for bunker
oil pollution.

18. _Clause 20_ seeks to amend section 14 of Act 515 to provide for ships
owned by a State and used for commercial purposes to comply with the new
section 11A, as introduced by _clause 17, in respect of compulsory insurance_
for bunker oil pollution.

19. _Clause 21 seeks to amend section 23 of Act 515 to extend the application_
of this section to the enforcement of a claim arising from bunker oil pollution
damage.

20. _Clause 22 seeks to amend section 24 of Act 515 to extend the application_
of this section to a judgement given by a Court in a Bunkers Convention
country to enforce a claim in respect of a liability incurred from bunker oil
pollution damage.

21. _Clause 23_ seeks to delete subsection 26(3) of Act 515 as _clause 24_
provides in respect of the prosecution of any offences under Act 515 or any
regulations made thereunder.

22. _Clause 24 seeks to introduce a new section 28A which provides that the_
prosecution of an offence under Act 515 or any regulations made thereunder
shall be instituted with the consent of the Public Prosecutor, and provides for
persons that may conduct the prosecution.

23. _Clause 26 seeks to amend the Second Schedule of Act 515 to update the_
list of State Parties to the International Convention on Civil Liability for Oil
Pollution Damage 1992 and the International Convention on the Establishment
of an International Fund for Compensation for Oil Pollution Damage 1992,
and adds the list of State Parties to the Bunkers Convention.

24. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are minor
and consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure.
