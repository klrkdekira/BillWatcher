EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Valuers, Appraisers and Estate Agents Act 1981
(“Act 242”).

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title of the proposed Act._

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 242 to substitute certain definitions_
and to introduce new definitions consequent upon the amendments proposed
in this Act.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to clarify further the scope of research services that may_
be provided by the Director General under the existing provisions.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to provide for additional functions of the Board. These_
proposed functions are the power to prescribe rules on the taking out of
professional indemnity insurance and to prescribe standards, directives, circulars
and guidelines for the purposes of Act 242.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 10A into Act 242 to enable the_
Board to appoint committees comprising members of the Board or any other
persons to assist it in performing its functions. The Board is also empowered to
determine the functions and procedures of the committees under the proposed
section.

7. _Clause 6 is a consequence of the amendment made in_ _clause 3. The_
proposed provision seeks to enable utilization of the Fund of the Board for
the payment of the expenses for registration and other expenses for any
research, investigation, study, conference or seminar that is not conducted by
the Board.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to empower the Board to prescribe the conditions to_
practise. This clause also seeks to provide that an individual registered valuer,
appraiser or estate agent is only allowed to practise in accordance with section
23 of Act 242.

9. _Clause 8 seeks to provide further conditions for the registration of valuers_
and appraisers.

10. _Clause 9 seeks to provide for the registration of probationary valuers._

11. _Clause 10 seeks to expand the scope of valuation practice under the_
existing section 19 of Act 242 by authorizing a registered valuer or appraiser
to value all interest in lands or buildings including furniture, fixtures, trade
stocks, plant, machinery and equipment and other effects.

12. _Clause 11 seeks to provide further restrictions on valuation practice._

13. _Clause 12 seeks to provide further conditions for the registration of_
estate agents.

14. _Clause 13 seeks to provide further restrictions for estate agency_
practice.


-----

_Valuers, Appraisers and Estate Agents (Amendment)_ 19

15. _Clause 14 seeks to provide for the registration of probationary estate_
agents.

16. _Clause 15 seeks to allow a sole proprietorship to operate as a valuer,_
appraiser or estate agency firm. _Subclause 15(c) seeks to allow the opening_
up of the profession regulated under the Act to allied professionals and to
allow natural persons as well as a combination of registered valuers, appraisers
and estate agents, partnerships and bodies corporate to hold equity in firms
practising valuation, appraisal or estate agency. _Subclause 15(d) empowers_
the Board to cancel the registration of a firm if the Board finds that the firm
or any of its partners or directors has breached or failed to carry out any of
the terms and conditions imposed by the Board. Additionally, subclause 15(e)
seeks to secure the interest of the public in the case where a registered person
is removed or suspended from the Register or has passed away.

17. _Clause 16 seeks to provide further reasons for the cancellation, suspension_
and admonition of valuers, appraisers and estate agents as well as probationary
valuers and probationary estate agents. This _clause also seeks to increase the_
fine that may be imposed by the Board against the valuer, appraiser or estate
agent or probationary valuer or probationary estate agent under paragraph
24(1)(iv) and (v) from five thousand ringgit to ten thousand ringgit and from
ten thousand ringgit to twenty-five thousand ringgit respectively.

18. _Clause 17 seeks to provide further grounds for the removal of registered_
valuers, appraisers and estate agents and probationary valuers and probationary
estate agents from the Register.

19. _Clause 18 seeks to provide for a notice of appeal to be made in the_
prescribed form.

20. _Clause 19 seeks to create an offence for aiding and abetting in the_
commission of an offence under Act 242. This _clause also creates an offence_
for practising as a valuer, appraiser or estate agent without lawful authority.
This clause also proposes that the maximum fines to be increased from twentyfive thousand ringgit to three hundred thousand ringgit and from ten thousand
ringgit to twenty-five thousand ringgit.

21. _Clause 20 seeks to increase the maximum fine from ten thousand ringgit_
to two hundred thousand ringgit.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure.
