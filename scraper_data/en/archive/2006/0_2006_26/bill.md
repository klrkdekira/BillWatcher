**"Guarantee of the Government**

**11A.** The repayment of all moneys deposited in the

such deficiency to the Dewan Rakyat at the earliest possible
opportunity.".

**New section 22A**

**6. The principal Act is amended by inserting after section 22 the**
following sections:

**"The Chief Executive may issue certificate to the Inspector**

**General of Police or the Director General of Immigration**
**to prevent recipient student leaving Malaysia in certain**
**circumstances**

22A. (1) The Chief Executive may, where he is of the opinion
that any recipient student is about or likely to leave Malaysia
without paying any moneys due to the Perbadanan, issue to
the Inspector General of Police or the Director General of
Immigration a certificate containing the amounts payable to
the Perbadanan with request for that recipient student to be
prevented from leaving Malaysia unless and until he pays all
the amounts payable to the Perbadanan or furnishes security
to the satisfaction of the Chief Executive for their payment.


(2) Subject to any order issued or made under any written

law relating to banishment or immigration, the Inspector General
of Police or the Director General of Immigration upon receiving
a request under subsection (1) in respect of any recipient
student shall take or cause to be taken all such measures as
may be necessary to give effect to it and which measures may
include the use of reasonable force and the seizure, removal
or retention of any certificate of identity and any passport,
exit permit or other travel document relating to that recipient
student.

(3) Where a recipient student in respect of whom a certificate

has been issued under subsection (1) produces a written statement
signed on or after the date of the issue of the certificate by
the Chief Executive or an authorized officer to the effect that
moneys due to the Perbadanan specified in the certificate
have been paid or that security has been furnished for their
payment, the statement or the payment, as the case may be,
shall be sufficient authority for allowing that recipient student
to leave Malaysia.

(4) No legal proceedings shall be instituted or maintained

against the Perbadanan, its officers and servants, the Government
of Malaysia, a police officer or any public officer in respect
of anything lawfully done under this section.".

_(b) any officer of the Perbadanan authorized in that behalf_

conduct such proceedings and may make and do all appearances,

**Power to compound**

**59. (1) The Chief Executive may, with the consent in writing**
of the Public Prosecutor, compound any offence committed
by any person under this Act or any regulations made under
this Act by making a written offer to the person who committed
the offence to compound the offence upon payment to the
Chief Executive of an amount of money not exceeding fifty
percent of the amount of the maximum fine for that offence
within the time specified in the offer.

(2) An offer under subsection (1) may be made at any time

after the offence has been committed, but before any prosecution
for it has been instituted and if the amount specified in the
offer is not paid within the time specified in the offer or
within such extended period as the Chief Executive may grant,
prosecution for the offence may be instituted at any time after
that against the person to whom the offer was made.

(3) If an offence has been compounded under subsection

(1), no prosecution shall be instituted in respect of the offence
against the person to whom the offer to compound was made
and any thing seized in connection with the offence may be
released or forfeited to the Chief Executive, as may be determined
by the Chief Executive, subject to such terms and conditions
as he thinks fit.

(4) All sums of money received by the Chief Executive

under this section shall be paid into and form part of the
Federal Consolidated Fund.".

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Perbadanan Tabung Pendidikan Tinggi Nasional
Act 1997 ("Act 566").

2.  Clause 1 of this Bill contains the short title of the proposed Act.


-----

_Perbadanan Tabung Pendidikan Tinggi Nasional_ 7

_(Amendment)_

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend the definition of "Minister" in section 2 of_
Act 566 to refer to the Minister responsible for higher education following the
restructuring of the Ministry of Education.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 7 of Act 566 in order to change the_

memberships of the Board of Management of the Perbadanan by providing the
appointment of the Secretary General of the Ministry of Higher Education, the
Secretary General of the Ministry of Education and the Chief Executive as
members of the Board of Management of Perbadanan. It is also amended to
enable the Minister to appoint five more members of the Board of Management
of the Perbadanan instead of two notwithstanding whether he is the representatives
of higher educational institutions or not.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 11 of Act 566 to enable the Perbadanan_

to receive deposits from a Malaysian citizen for the purpose of saving towards
higher education for himself or any other person.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 11A into Act 566 to provide for_

the guarantee of the Government to all moneys deposited with the Perbadanan.

7. _Clause 6 seeks to introduce.a new section 22A into Act 566 to empowers_

the Chief Executive to issue certificate to the Inspector General of Police or
the Director General of Immigration to prevent any recipient student from
leaving Malaysia in certain circumstances especially when an educational loan
is not repaid or in arrears.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to introduce a new section 23A into Act 566 to extend the_

limitation period from six years under the Limitation Act 1953 to twelve years
for the recovery of educational loans under this Act.

9. _Clause 8 seeks to amend section 29 of Act 566 to enable an employer to_

assist the Perbadanan besides the Inland Revenue Board by deducting from the
salary of the recipient student every month towards the discharge of his educational
loan.

10. _Clause 9 seeks to amend section 32 of Act 566 to allow the Minister to_

appoint any member of the Board of Management of the Perbadanan except the
Chief Executive to carry out the duties of the Chairman including to preside
at all meetings of the Board of Management of the Perbadanan in the absence
of the Chairman for any reasons.

11. _Clause 10 seeks to amend Act 566 by deleting sections 36, 37, 38, 39,_

40, 41, 42, 43 and 44 of the Act since the Statutory Bodies (Discipline and
Surcharge) Act 2000 applies to the Perbadanan in respect of matters relating
to the discipline, termination of service in public interest and surcharge of
officers and servants of the Perbadanan.

12. _Clause 11 seeks to amend section 49 of Act 566 to empower the Perbadanan_

to make regulations regarding the responsibilities, conditions and procedures
for the grant of educational loans to recipient students.


-----

8 _Bill_

13. _Clause 12 seeks to introduce a new section 49A into Act 566 to provide_

for the penalties in respect of offences under subsidiary legislation.

14. _Clause 13 seeks to introduce new sections 58 and 59 into Act 566. The_

new section 58 seeks to allow the Federal Counsel or any officer of the
Perbadanan to act on behalf of the Perbadanan in any civil proceedings. The
new section 59 seeks to enable the Chief Executive with the consent in writing
of the Public Prosecutor to compound any offence under the Act or any regulations
made under the Act.

This Bill will involve the Government in extra financial expenditure the amount
of which cannot at present be ascertained.
