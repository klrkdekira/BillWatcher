EXPLANATORY STATEMENT This Bill seeks to

amend the Control of Supplies Act 1961 ("Act 122").

2. _Clause / contains the short title and provision on the commencement of_

the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 5 of Act 122 to provide for the power of_

the Minister to make orders to declare any article or food to be controlled or
rationed articles or both and to declare these articles to be marked in certain
manner.

4. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 6 of Act 122 to provide for the power_

of the Minister to make regulations relating to the marking of any controlled
article.

5. _Clause 7 seeks to introduce the new subsections, namely subsection 9(1A),_

(1B) and (2A) into Act 122.

The new subsection (IA) seeks lo empower the supplies officer to enter

into premises, examine and take samples of any controlled articles capable of
being used for the storage, usage and distribution of any controlled article.
The supplies officer may also examine any book or document found in thai
premises which is believed to contain any information relevant to the controlled
article.

The new subsection (1 B) makes it an offence for any owner or occupier or

person who fails to give any reasonable assistance to the supplies officer.

The new subsection (2A) allows any supplies officer to take with him such

other person or equipment as may appear to him necessary when entering any
premises.

6. _Clause 11 seeks to amend section 22 of Act 22 to increase the amount_

of penalty to be imposed for any offence under this Act.

7. _Clause 12 seeks to amend section 22A of Act 122 to empower the supplies_

officer with the written authorization of the Controller to compound any offence
which is prescribed to be a compoundable offence. It also seeks to provide
that any decision to compound any offences has to be with the written consent
of the Public Prosecutor.

8. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are minor

or consequential in nature.
