EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Customs Act 1967 ("Act 235").

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and the power of the Minister to appoint the_

commencement date of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2_ seeks to amend section 2 of Act 235 to introduce the definition of

"customs ruling" into that section.

4. _Clause 3_ seeks to introduce a new Part IIA into Act 235 that provides for an

application for a customs ruling, the making of the ruling and its applicability. This
_clause allows the Director General to amend, modify or revoke a customs ruling._
The Director General is also empowered to declare a customs ruling to be null, void
and of no effect if the ruling has been obtained by way of fraud, misrepresentation
or falsification of facts. An applicant is not allowed to hold two or more different
customs rulings on a same subject matter.

5. _Clause 5 seeks to amend section 135 of Act 235 to specify the minimum fines_

that can be imposed for offences committed under that section.

_6._ _Clause 6_ seeks to introduce a new Part XIVA into Act 235. Amongst others,

this _clause_ provides for the establishment of the Customs Appeal Tribunal
("Tribunal") and appointment of its members. This _clause_ also provides for the
jurisdiction, sitting and powers of the Tribunal.

7. _Clause 7 seeks to amend section 142 of Act 235 to empower the Minister to_

make regulations in respect of customs rulings.

8. _Clause 8_ seeks to amend section 143 of Act 235 to enable for any person

aggrieved by the decision of the Director General, other than decisions relating to
compound, to appeal to the Tribunal.

9. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are minor and

consequential in nature.
