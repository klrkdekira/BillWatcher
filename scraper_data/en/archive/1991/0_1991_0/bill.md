EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Telecommunications Act 1950 (in this
Statement referred to as the "Act").

2. Clause 1 provides for the short title.

3. Clause 2 seeks to introduce new definitions of "authorized
telecommunication officer", "premises" and "telecommunication
equipment" and also seeks to amend the definition of
"telecommunication officer".

4. Clause 3 seeks to amend section 3 to clarify that the approval by
the Land Authority has to be obtained by the licencee.

5. Clause 4 seeks to amend section 3B.

6. Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 3c allowing the
Director General to delegate the performance of any of his duties or
functions to any authorized telecommunication officer.

7. Clause 6 seeks to amend section 7 and to introduce new matters
for which the Minister may make regulations in relation to
telecommunication.

8. Clauses 7 and 8 seek to amend Part III of the Act by introducing
the words "telecommunication equipment" wherever appropriate.

_9. Clauses 9 to 18 seek to increase the fines provided for under Part_
IV of the Act.

10. Clause 19 seeks to introduce new sections 3 l A and 31B to
provide for the entry by warrant or otherwise of authorized
telecommunication officers.

11. Clause 20 seeks to delete section 33A.


General
amendment.


-----

TELECOMMUNICATIONS (AMENDMENT) 15

12. Clause 21 seeks to rearrange the definitions according to
alphabetical order and also seeks to introduce new definitions of
"radio broadcast receiver", "radiocommunication propagation path"
and "television broadcast receiver".

13. Clause 22 seeks to amend section 38 to provide that any person
licensed under the Broadcasting Act 1988 to install, work or deal in
radio broadcast receivers or television broadcast receivers or any
person exempted under section 15 of that Act shall not be required to
obtain a licence under section 37(1) to install, work or deal in such
receivers.

14. Clause 23 seeks to introduce a new section 38A to empower the
Minister to exempt, subject to such conditions as he may impose, any
person having a licence to install or work a radiocommunication,
issued by a country which has a reciprocal arrangement with Malaysia
by a treaty regarding such licences, from the operation of section 38.

15. Clause 24 seeks to increase the penalty for any person who is
guilty of establishing a radiocommunication station or working any
radiocommunication apparatus without a licence.

16. Clause 25 seeks to introduce a new section 39A to provide for
search without warrant.

17. Clause 26 seeks to introduce new matters for which the Minister
may make regulations in relation to radiocommunication.

18. Clause 27 seeks to introduce a new Part VI relating to
enforcement and investigation by authorized telecommunication
officers. Under this Pan the authorized telecommunication officers
will be conferred with powers of investigation to ensure effective
enforcement of the Act.

19. Other proposed amendments to the Act which have not been
specifically referred to are consequential or minor in nature.
