EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Bankruptcy Act 1967 ("the Act").

2. Clause 1 deals with preliminary matters including the Minister's
power to appoint the date for the commencement of the proposed
Act.

3. Clause 2 seeks to amend section 33 by deleting subsection (11)
as the provisions in that subsection are now contained in the proposed
section 35A.

4. Clause 3 seeks to introduce new sections 33A and 33e into the
Act.

Section 33A seeks to confer power on the Official Assignee to

discharge a bankrupt from bankruptcy by issuing a certificate of
discharge in deserving cases.

Section 33B seeks to permit a creditor to object to the Official

Assignee's proposal to discharge a bankrupt by the issuance of a
certificate of discharge.

5. Clause 4 seeks to replace the provisions in section 35 of the Act
with more comprehensive provisions on the effect of a discharge
made by an order of the court under section 33 and by a certificate
issued under the proposed section 33A.

6. Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 35A into the Act to
require a discharged bankrupt to give assistance to the Official
Assignee in the realization and distribution of his property as vested
in the Official Assignee. It also provides that a bankrupt who fails
to do so commits an offence.

7. Clause 6 seeks to amend section 39 of the Act to remove the
requirement of gazetting the list of undischarged bankrupts every
five years and a schedule of amendments to the list every year.

8. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement
are minor or consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 2014.]
