EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Companies Act 1965 ("the Act").

2. _Clause 1 deals with the short title and commencement date of_
the proposed Act.

3. Clause 2 seeks to introduce a new section 11A to enable documents
required under the Act to be electronically filed or lodged with the
Registrar.

4. Clause 3 seeks to amend subsection 16(2) of the Act to require
that a declaration of compliance made by the first secretary of the
company be lodged with the Registrar for the purpose of incorporating
a company. It also seeks to introduce a new subsection 16(8) to

5. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 139 of the Act to enable a_
secretary of a company to vacate his office where none of the
directors can be contacted and to introduce a requirement for a
person's consent before he is appointed as a secretary.

6. Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 166A. The new section
166A requires the accounts of a company and the consolidated accounts
of a holding company to be made out in accordance with the applicable
approved accounting standards with certain exceptions.

7. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 169 of the Act to require a_
statement to be made in accordance with a resolution of the directors
and signed by at least two directors stating whether the accounts
or consolidated accounts of the company have been drawn up to
give a true and fair view of the business and affairs of the company
and whether the accounts or consolidated accounts comply with the
applicable approved accounting standards.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to amend subsection 174(2) of the Act to require_
an auditor to state in his report whether the accounts or consolidated
accounts of the company have been properly drawn up in accordance
with the applicable approved accounting standards.

9. Clauses 8, 9, 12 and 13 seek to amend sections 194, 195 and
319 and paragraph 324(b) of the Act respectively so that the declaration
or specification under those provisions can be made by an order
instead of a proclamation.

10. _Clause 10 seeks to amend subsection 217(1) of the Act to_
enable the Registrar to petition the Court to wind up a company
on the ground specified under paragraph 218(1)(m) or (n) of the
Act.

11. Clause 11 seeks to amend subsection 218(1) of the Act to
provide additional grounds for the Court to compulsorily wind up
a company if it is being used for unlawful purposes or any purpose
prejudicial to or incompatible with peace, welfare, security, public
order, good order or morality or any purpose prejudicial to national
security or public interest.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
