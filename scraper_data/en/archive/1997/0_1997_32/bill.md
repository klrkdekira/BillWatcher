PRELIMINARY

**1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Counsellors Act**
1997 and shall come into operation on such date as the
Minister may, by notification in the Gazette, appoint.

_(a) any health professional; or_
_(b) any company, society or local authority providing_
healthcare services such as hospital, nursing
home, hospice, blood bank, psychiatric hospital,
ambulatory care centre, maternity home,
haemodialysis centre, community mental health
centre, psychiatric nursing home, medical or
dental clinic, or any other healthcare facility.

## 32 Bill

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to provide for the registration and practice of counsellors
and for matters connected therewith.

2. Part I contains preliminary provisions such as the short title,
commencement and application of the proposed Act (clause 1) and
the definitions of certain words and expressions used in this Bill
_(clause 2)._

3. Part II contains provisions relating to the Malaysian Counsellors
Advisory Council.

_Clauses 3 and 5 provides for the establishment of the Malaysian_

Counsellors Advisory Council and its membership, respectively,
while clause 4 contains the functions of the Council.

_Clause 6 contains provisions on the tenure of office of the members_

of the Council, while clauses 7, 8 and 9 deal with the revocation
of appointment, cessation of membership and resignation of members
of the Council, respectively.

4. Part III contains provisions relating to the Board of Counsellors.

_Clause 11 provides for the establishment of the Board while_

_clause 12 deals with its functions._

_Clause 13 contains provisions on the membership of the Board._

_Clause 16 requires the Board to furnish returns, reports, accounts_

and information to the Minister and such other public authority as
directed by the Minister.

_Clause 17 seeks to empower the Board to appoint an advocate and_

solicitor on its behalf to institute and conduct civil proceedings.

5. Part IV contains provisions relating to the registration of
counsellors.

_Clause 18 provides for the appointment of a Registrar of Counsellors_

and his duties.

_Clause 19 deals with the keeping and maintenance of a Register_

of Counsellors.

_Clause 20 seeks to empower the Board to remove the names of_

persons who have died or who have become incapable of performing
counselling duties due to physical or mental infirmity. Under this
_clause the Board may also remove the name of any person who fails_
to renew his certificate of temporary registration or practising certificate
within four years of its expiry, but such person may apply under
_clause 21 to be reinstated in the Register._


-----

## Counsellors 33

_Clauses 22 and 23 seek to prohibit any person from practising as_

a counsellor unless he is registered under the proposed Act and holds
a valid practising certificate.

_Clause 24 sets out the qualifications that a person must have_

before he can be registered under the proposed Act, while clauses
_25 and 26 deal with applications for registration and the process of_
registration, respectively. The name of every person registered under

the proposed section 26 will be published in the Gazette.

_Clause 27 seeks to permit counsellors who are neither Malaysian_

citizens nor permanent residents of Malaysia to be temporarily registered
under the proposed Act. They will be issued with a certificate of
temporary registration which is valid for only one year. However,
the certificate is renewable.

_Clause 29 seeks to require every registered counsellor to exhibit_

his certificate of registration and practising certificates at the premises
in which he practises. The addresses of such premises are stated in
the practising certificates. If the registered counsellor changes his
place of practice or practises at an additional place, he has to notify
the Registrar and the address of the new place or additional place
will be endorsed on the practising certificate. This is provided for
in clause 30.

6. Part V contains provisions dealing with bodies corporate practising
as counsellors.

Under clause 33, a body corporate may only practice as a counsellor

if all member of its board of directors are registered counsellors or
if the majority of them are registered counsellors and the remaining
members are persons belonging to professions allied to the counselling
profession. Under clause 35 any change in this membership has to
be notified to the Board.

7. Part VI contains provisions relating to disciplinary proceedings.

_Clauses 36,37 and 38 provide for the establishment of Investigation_

and Disciplinary Committees and their duties.

The Investigation Committee's duty is to investigate every complaint

made in accordance with clause 37.

All disciplinary inquiries are to be conducted by the Disciplinary

Commitee who may impose the disciplinary punishments set out in
_clause 40._

The Board may, under the proposed section 39, suspend a registered

counsellor's practising certificate pending the completion of
investigations and inquiry against the counsellor. The suspension
may not exceed six months.

## 34 Bill

_Clause 42 seeks to permit a person against whom a disciplinary_

punishment has been imposed to appeal to the Minister against the
decision of the Disciplinary Committee.

_Clause 41 seeks to require a registered person whose name has_

been ordered to be removed from the Register to surrender his
certificate of registration, certificate of temporary registration or
practising certificate to the Board.

Under clause 43 the name of every person removed from the

Register consequent to any disciplinary proceedings shall be published
in the Gazette.

8. Part VII contains provisions relating to offences.

_Clause 44 provides for general offences while clause 45 contains_

the general penalty.

_Clause 46 deals with offence committed by a body corporate._

_Clause 47 seeks to require the consent of the Public Prosecutor_

for the prosecution of offences under the proposed Act.

9. Part VIII contains miscellaneous provisions.

_Clause 48 seeks to empower the President of the Board or any_

officer authorised by him to enter, inspect and examine the premises
of a registered counsellor.

_Clause 49 seeks to empower the Minister to make regulations._
