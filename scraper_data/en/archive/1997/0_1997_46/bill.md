EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Securities Industry (Central Depositories)
Act 1991 ("the Act").

2. _Clause 1 seeks to provide for the short title and commencement_

of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend the definition of "security" in_

section 2 of the Act which is consequential to the amendment of
section 84 of the Companies Act 1965 made by the Companies
(Amendment) Act 1995.

This clause also seeks to insert a new definition of "unit

trust scheme" into section 2 of the Act to define the expression
which is used in the amended definition of "security".

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 14 of the Act to cater for_

the automatic prescription of additional issue of securities by an
issuer.

_5._ _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 22 of the Act to reflect_

the fact that the withdrawal of securities are also governed by the
rules of the central depository.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to amend section 34 of the Act to provide_

for the flexibility in the manner in which an issuer can request
for the record of depositors from the central depository.

7. Clause 6 seeks to amend section 35 of the Act. This amendment
is consequential to the amendment of section 84 of the Companies
Act 1965 made by the Companies (Amendment) Act 1995. After the
amendment to section 84 of the Companies Act 1965, all unit trust
schemes are regulated under the Securities Commission (Unit Trust
Scheme) Regulations 1996.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to amend section 38 of the Act to provide_
for situations involving an offer for sale of securities.

9. _Clause 8 seeks to introduce a new section 41A into the Act_
to ensure that the operations of the central depository are not
affected by trust notices.

10. The amendment to clause 9 is necessary following the
decision in the case of Repco Holdings Bhd. v. Public Prosecutor
_(1997)._

11. _Clause 10 seeks to introduce a new section 6l A into the_
Act to enable the Commission to be represented in any civil
proceedings by any officer of the Commission authorised by the
Commission for such purpose.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 1974.]
