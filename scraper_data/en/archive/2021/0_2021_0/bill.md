_EXPLANATORY STATEMENT_

This Bill seeks to amend the Employment Act 1955 (“Act 265”)
to comply with the international standards and practices as required by the
Trans-Pacific Partnership Agreement, the Malaysia-United States Labour
Consistency Plan and the International Labour Organization. The purpose
of this amendment among others, is to provide for the protection against
discrimination and forced labour, and to provide for maternity benefits.

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and the provision on the commencement_
of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause_ _2 seeks to amend subsection 2(1) of Act 265 to introduce_
new definition and to amend certain existing definitions.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 4 of Act 265 to extend the duration_
for an appeal to be made to the Director General.

5. _Clause 4_ seeks to introduce a new section 18a into Act 265 to provide
for the calculation of wages where an employee has not completed a whole month
of service.

6. Clause 5 seeks to amend subsection 22(2) of Act 265 to extend the definition
of “immediate family members” to spouse.

7. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 25 of Act 265 to be consistent_
with the Financial Services Act 2013 [Act 758], the Islamic Financial Services
Act 2013 [Act 759] and the Development Financial Institutions Act 2002

[Act 618]. Under the new subsection 25(4), the Minister may, by order,
recognize an approved issuer of designated payment instrument for the purpose
of payment of wages.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to amend section 25a of Act 265 to insert new requirements_
for the payment of wages other than through financial institution.

9. _Clause 9 seeks to amend section_ 33a of Act 265 to insert additional
requirements for a contractor for labour and to increase the amount of penalty
imposed for failure to comply with the requirements under section 33a
of Act 265 from ten thousand ringgit to fifty thousand ringgit.

10. _Clause 10_ seeks to delete Part VIII of Act 265 to comply with

the requirements of the International Labour Organization.

11. _Clause 12_ seeks to amend section 37 of Act 265 to increase the period

of maternity leave from sixty to ninety days and other matters relating to it.

12. _Clause 13_ seeks to introduce a new section 41a into Act 265 to restrict

the termination of pregnant female employee.

13. _Clauses 15_ and 29 seek to delete sections 44a, 69b, 69c, 69d and 69e

of Act 265 as a consequence of the extension of the scope of Act 265. This
would allow an employee to bring any dispute before the Director General
irrespective of the amount of wages he receives which under the current
provisions is capped at five thousand ringgit.

14. _Clauses 16 and 17 seek to amend the heading of Part XI and section 57_

of Act 265 to substitute for the word “servant” the word “employee”.

15. Clauses 18 and 19 seek to amend sections 57a and 57b of Act 265
to substitute for the word “servant” the word “employee” and to increase the
amount of penalty imposed from ten thousand ringgit to fifty thousand ringgit
for failure to inform the Director General on the employment and termination
of a foreign employee.

16. _Clauses 20_ and _21_ seek to amend sections 60a and 60c of Act 265

as a consequence of the reduction of hours of work in one week
from forty-eight to forty-five hours.

17. _Clause 22 seeks to amend section 60f of Act 265 to provide for matters_

relating to the entitlement of paid sick leave.

18. _Clause 23 seeks to introduce a new section 60fa into Act 265 to provide_

for matters relating to paternity leave for a married male employee.

19. _Clause 24_ seeks to substitute the existing section 60k of Act 265

to introduce new requirements for the employment of a foreign employee.

20. _Clause 25 seeks to introduce a new section 60ka into Act 265 to provide_

for matters relating to the termination of employment of a foreign employee.

21. _Clause 27_ seeks to introduce a new Part XIIc into Act 265 relating to

flexible working arrangement for an employee.

27. _Clause 40 seeks to introduce a new section 87a into Act 265 to empower_

the court to order an employer to pay any payment due to an employee.

28. _Clause 41_ seeks to introduce a new section 90b into Act 265 to provide

for the offence of forced labour.

29. _Clause 43 seeks to amend section 99a of Act 265 to increase the amount_

of penalty imposed from ten thousand ringgit to fifty thousand ringgit for
any offence under Act 265 and regulations made under the Act for which no
penalty is prescribed.

30. _Clause 44_ seeks to introduce a new section 101c into Act 265 on the

presumption as to who is employee and employer of such employee in the
absence of a contract of service.

31. _Clause 45 seeks to amend section 102 of Act 265 to provide for additional_

matters for which the Minister may make regulations under Act 265.

32. _Clause 46_ deals with the saving and transitional provisions.

33. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are minor

or consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will involve the Government in extra financial expenditure the amount
of which cannot at present be ascertained.
