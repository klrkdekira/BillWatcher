EXPLANATORY STATEMENT This Bill

seeks to amend the Explosives Act 1957 ("Act 207").

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and the provision on the commencement_

of the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 207 to introduce definitions for_

certain expressions used in Act 207.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend section 4 of Act 207 to extend the scope of the_

provision to the exportation of explosives and to enhance the fines from ten
thousand ringgit to fifty thousand ringgit.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to introduce new sections 4A,_ 4 B, 4 C and 4 D into

Act 207. The new sections are to give effect to the Convention on the Marking
_of Plastic Explosives for the Purpose of Detection concluded in Montreal on_
1 March 1991.

The new section 4A prohibits any person from manufacturing any unmarked

plastic explosive while the new section 4B prohibits any person from possessing
or transferring possession of any unmarked plastic explosive. The new section
4c prohibits any person from importing or exporting any unmarked plastic
explosive. Certain exceptions are provided for unmarked plastic explosives
that have been imported into or manufactured in Malaysia before the appointed
day.

# Explosives (Amendment)

The new section 4D seeks to require all persons possessing any plastic

explosive on the appointed day to report to the Inspector General of Police
within one hundred and twenty days after such appointed day the quantity of
such plastic explosives possessed, the manufacturer or importer of such plastic
explosives, any marks of identification on such plastic explosives, and such
other information as the Minister may by regulations prescribe.

6. _Clauses 5 and 6 seek to amend sections 11 and 14 of Act 207 respectively_

to enhance the fines imposed.

7. _Clause 8 seeks to amend section 26 of Act 207 to empower the Minister_

to make regulations to regulate the marking of plastic explosives.

8. _Clause 9 seeks to amend section 27 of Act 207 to empower the Minister,_

on application in writing, to exempt by order published in the Gazette any
unmarked plastic explosive, from the application of Act 207, that is held in
limited quantities for research, development, testing and training purposes or
for forensic science activity or that has been incorporated as an integral part
of an authorized military device and is possessed or transferred in the course
of his duty by an authorized military or police personnel or a member of a
visiting force lawfully present in Malaysia.

9. _Clause 11 seeks to amend the Schedule and to introduce a new Second_

Schedule into Act 207 to set out the substances that are to be used as detection
agents in plastic explosives for the purposes of Act 207.

10. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are minor

or consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure.
