**"Admissibility of evidence obtained through assistance from**

**a foreign supervisory authority**

**150A.** **(1)** If an Investigating Officer of the Commission
in the course of his investigation obtains evidence, oral or
documentary, through assistance obtained from a foreign
supervisory authority, such evidence shall be admissible as
_prima facie evidence._


(2) In this section, a copy of a documentary evidence

shall not be admissible in evidence unless the person who
has custody or control of the document affirms that he has
examined and verified the copy of that document with the
original.

(3) Notwithstanding subsection (2), where the original

document is not found or is lost, it shall be sufficient for the
person who has custody or control of the document to state
orally or in an affidavit given under this section that he had


-----

_Bill_

made reasonable efforts to locate the original documents and
thereafter the copy of such documents shall be admissible
as _prima facie_ evidence of such documents in any legal
proceedings.

(4) The oral evidence referred to in subsection (1) shall

be admissible as prima facie evidence if a person authorized
by the foreign supervisory authority records the oral evidence
in writing, affirms in an affidavit that the statement attached
to the affidavit has been recorded from a person acquainted
with the circumstances of the case and the recording of the
oral evidence was made in accordance with the laws enforced

(5) An affidavit under this section shall not be admissible

_(a)_ in the Republic of Singapore, before any Judge,

_(b)_ in England, Scotland, Ireland or the Channel Islands

before any Judge, Court, Notary Public or other
person lawfully authorized to administer oaths; or

_(c)_ in any other place, before any officer exercising

consular functions on behalf of Malaysia.

(6) The court shall take judicial notice of the seal or

signature, as the case may be, of any Judge, Court, Notary

## Securities Commission (Amendment)

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Securities Commission Act 1993 ("Act 498").
The proposed amendments are consequential to the enactment of the Capital
Markets and Services Act 2007.

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title. It also seeks to empower the Minister_

to appoint a date of coming into operation of the Act and to appoint different
dates for the coming into operation of different provisions of this Act or in
respect of different classes or categories of persons or securities.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 of Act 498 to amend or delete certain_

expressions to be in line with the expressions used in the Capital Markets and
Services Act 2007.

4. _Clauses 3 and 4 seek to delete sections 2B and 2c of Act 498 respectively_

as both the provisions are now being provided for in Part VI and subsection
378(3) of the Capital Markets and Services Act 2007.

5. _Clause 5 seeks to amend subsection 18(1) of Act 498 to state that any_

committee established by the Commission will assist the Commission in
performing its functions not only under Act 498 but also under the securities
laws which are defined in the proposed Act as the Securities Industry (Central
Depositories) Act 1991 and the Capital Markets and Services Act 2007.

6. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 24 of Act 498 to empower the Commission_

to impose levy at a rate to be specified by the Minister for trades effected
on electronic facility registered pursuant to section 34 of the Capital Markets
and Services Act 2007.

7. _Clause 7 seeks to delete Part IV and IVA of Act 498 as all provisions_

in those Parts are now incorporated in Part VI of the Capital Markets and
Services Act 2007 relating to the issuance of securities and take-overs and
mergers.

8. _Clause 8 seeks to amend section 125 of Act 498. It merely redraft the_

provision to remove reference to the laws that has become obsolete due to
the enactment of the Capital Markets and Services Act 2007.

9. _Clause 9 seeks to amend section 126 of Act 498 to provide for examination_

of books or other documents, accounts and transactions by the Commission.

10. _Clause 10 seeks to amend section 127 of Act 498 to remove reference_

of "licensed person" in consequence of the amendment made to section 126
of Act 498.

11. _Clause 11 seeks to amend section 128 of Act 498 to confer the Investigating_

Officer of the Commission the additional power to detain any person found
on the premises searched in order to facilitate the search.

12. _Clause 12 seeks to amend section 134 of Act 498 to make it an offence_

for any person who obstructs an Investigating Officer of the Commission in
carrying out an investigation under that section.


-----

_Bill_

13. _Clauses 15, 16, 17, 18 and 19 seek to amend sections 146, 147, 148,_

149 and 150 of Act 498 respectively to extend the application of securities
laws to those sections.

14. _Clause 20 seeks to introduce a new section 150A into Act 498 to provide_

for the admissibility of evidence obtained by the Investigating Officer of the
Commission through the assistance of a foreign supervisory authority as a

_prima facie evidence in court._

15. _Clause 21 seeks to amend section 151 of Act 498. The power of the_

Commission to specify the manner and form in which information may be
required from a person and the period for complying with the request under
Act 498 will now extend to such power being exercised under the securities
laws.

16. _Clause 22 seeks to introduce a new section 152A into Act 498 to allow_

the Commission to publish any information which the Commission thinks
necessary or expedient in the interest of the public or for the protection of
investors.

17. _Clauses 23, 24 and 25 seek to delete sections 153, 155 and 156 of Act_

498 respectively as the provisions are now incorporated into Part XI of the
Capital Markets and Services Act 2007.

18. _Clause 26 seeks to amend section 158 of Act 498 which provides that_

the Commission may require a person to give effect to any written notices,
circulars, conditions or guidelines issued by the Commission.

19. _Clause 27 seeks to delete paragraphs 159(2)(c) and (d) of Act 498_

relating to power of the Commission to make regulations to be in line with
the provisions in the Capital Markets and Services Act 2007.

20. _Clause 28 seeks to delete Schedules 1, 2, 3, 4 and 5 of Act 498 as_

similar Schedules have been provided for in the Capital Markets and Services
Act 2007.

21. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are

amendments which are minor or consequential in nature.


_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure.
