_I n t i t u l e d_

An Act to amend the Trade Unions Act 1959.

[ ]

ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:

**Short title and commencement**

**1.** (1) This Act may be cited as the Trade Unions (Amendment)
Act 2007.

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed by the

Minister by notification in the Gazette, and the Minister may appoint
different dates for the coming into operation of different provisions of
this Act.

**Amendment of section 2**

**2.** The Trade Unions Act 1959 _[Act 262],_ which is referred
to as the "principal Act" in this Act, is amended in subsection
2(1)
_(a)_ by inserting after the definition of "Government" the

following definition:

' "levy" means any mandatory contribution, other than

members' entrance fee or subscription, which is imposed
by a trade union on its members;';

_(b)_ in the definition of "Minister", by substituting for the

word "labour" the words "human resources";

**Substitution of section 31**

**10.** The principal Act is amended by substituting for section 31
the following section:


"31. (1) Any registered trade union may, subject to section 34,

change its name, provided that —

_(a)_ the votes of at least one-half of its members entitled to

vote are recorded; and

_(b)_ at least fifty per centum of the votes recorded are in

favour of the change of name.

its name without the consent of its members if there is a change
in the name of the establishment being effected under any
written law, occupation, ministry, department, or statutory
authority, as the case may be, in respect of which the trade union
is registered.".

**New section 40A**

**14.** The principal Act is amended by inserting after section 40
the following section:

**"Imposition of levy**

**40A.** A trade union may impose a levy on its members, provided that
for each and every imposition of the levy —


_(a)_ the votes of at least one-half of the members who are entitled

to vote are recorded; and

_(b)_ at least fifty per centum of the votes recorded are in favour of

the imposition of such levy.".

**Amendment of section 49**

**15.** Paragraph 49(1 _)(a)_ of the principal Act is amended by
substituting for the words "to sell, exchange, charge or lease"
the words "lease, or with the consent of its members obtained

**New section 50A**

**16.** The principal Act is amended by inserting after section 50
the following section:

**"Collection of funds and moneys**

**50A.** (1) No trade union shall enter into any agreement with any
person by which such person undertakes to raise funds or collect
moneys for or on behalf of the trade union except with the prior
approval in writing of the Director General and subject to such
conditions as he may impose.


(2) Subsection (1) shall not apply to any agreement where an

employer make deductions from the wages of his workmen for
payment to a trade union on account of subscriptions.".

**Validation**

**20. A trade union whose membership consists of workmen employed**
in a place of business or employment of an employer, including any
division or branch of the place of business or employment and which
was registered prior to 5 May 1989, being the date the term
"establishment" as defined in section 2 of the principal Act came into
operation, shall for all purposes be deemed to have been validly and
lawfully registered and continue to exist as if it had been registered
under the principal Act on 5 May 1989.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Trade Unions Act 1959 ("Act 262").

2.  Clause 1 contains the short title and the power of the Minister to appoint the
commencement date of the proposed Act.


-----

8 _Bill_

3. _Clause 2_ seeks to introduce the definitions of "levy" and "person". The

definition "Minister" is re-defined so as to be in line and consistent with the
provisions of the other related laws, whilst the insertion of the words "and
harmonious" is to reflect and promote the kind of employer and employee
relationship desired for industrial harmony.

4. _Clause 3_ seeks to enable the Deputy Director General, in the absence of the

Director General of Trade Unions, to have all powers to discharge all duties and
perform all functions conferred on the Director General of Trade Unions under Act
262.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to provide for the appointment of Deputy Director and Senior_

Assistant Director of Trade Unions.

6. _Clause 5_ seeks to stipulate that every application for registration of a trade

union must be accompanied by a printed copy in the national language of the rules
of the trade union.

7. _Clause 6_ seeks to allow the Director General to cancel the certificate of

registration of a trade union where the trade union has been dissolved but no request
for the cancellation of the certificate of registration is made by the trade union.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to clarify the status of a trade union whose registration has been_

rendered null and void by decision of the court.


9. _Clause 8 seeks to harmonize with the provision of the Industrial Relations Act_

1967 [Act 177] and to provide exclusive power to the Director General of Industrial
Relations to perform membership check.

10. _Subclause 9(a)_ seeks to provide further grounds for disqualification of a

person from being a member of the executive of a trade union or any branch
thereof, or of any federation of trade unions.

_Subclause 9(b)_ seeks to remove the requirement of at least one year of

employment in any establishment, trade, occupation or industry before a person
could act as a member of the executive of a trade union, any of its branch or
federation of the trade union.

11. _Clause 10_ seeks to facilitate the registration of change of name of a trade

union.

12. _Clause 11_ seeks to facilitate a more orderly transfer of trade union funds

where there is amalgamation of trade unions.

13. _Clause 12 seeks to prescribe the time within which a notice of any change of_

location of office of a trade union is to be submitted to the Director General.

14. _Clause 13 seeks to fix a reasonable sum that may be charged by a trade union_

for a copy of its rules.


-----

_Trade Unions (Amendment)_ 9

15. _Clause 14 seeks to introduce new section 40A into Act 262 to allow a trade_

union to impose levy on its members.

16. _Clause 15 seeks to safeguard the interest of trade unions members._

17. _Clause 16_ seeks to introduce new section 50A into Act 262 to prohibit an

unlawful raising and collection of moneys by a trade union.

18. _Clause 17_ provides for a stricter compliance of the law through joint and

collective responsibilities imposed upon its members of the executive of a trade
union to provide better protection to the interest of trade unions' members.

19. _Clause 18 to enhance the fines and impose heavier penalty._

20. _Clause 19 seeks to provide for the service of summons issued by the Director_

General.

21. _Clause 20_ seeks to validate the establishment of trade unions registered

before 5 May 1989.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will involve the Government in extra financial expenditure the amount of
which cannot at present be ascertained.
