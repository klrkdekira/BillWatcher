An Act to amend the Industrial Relations Act 1967.

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:**

**Short title and commencement**

**1.** (1) This Act may be cited as the Industrial Relations
(Amendment) Act 2007.

(2) This Act comes into operation on a date to be appointed by the

Minister by notification in the Gazette, and the Minister may appoint
different dates for the coming into operation of different provisions of
this Act.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT This Bill seeks to

amend the Industrial Relations Act 1967 ("Act 177").

2. _Clause 1_ contains the short title and provision on the commencement of the

proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend the long title of Act 177 to reflect the main objective_

of the Act which is to promote and maintain industrial harmony.

4. _Clause 3_ seeks to amend section 2 of Act 177 to introduce the definition of

"Minister".

5. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 2A of Act 177. Subclause 4(a) seeks to amend_

subsection 2A(2) of Act 177 to ensure the appointment of a Deputy Director
General for Industrial Relations by the Yang di-Pertuan Agong and also to create a
new post of Principal Assistant Director of Industrial Relations. _Subclause 4(b)_
seeks to introduce a new subsection 2A(2A) into Act 177 to clarify that the officers
appointed under subsections 2A(1) and (2) of Act 177 are officers from the
Industrial Relations Officers Scheme of Service. Subclause 4(c) seeks to introduce
a new subsection 2A(4) into Act 177 to provide that officers appointed under
subsection 2A(2) of Act 177 shall perform all the duties imposed and may exercise
all the powers conferred upon the Director General subject to any limitation as may
be prescribed by regulations.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 2B into Act 177 so that all officers_

appointed under section 2A of Act 177 be deemed to be public servants for the
purposes of the Penal Code [Act 574].

_1. Clause 6 seeks to amend paragraph 5(2)(b) of Act 177 to allow an employer to_
require a person who is or has been appointed or promoted to an

executive or a security position to cease to be a member or officer of a trade union
catering for workmen other than those in an executive or a security position.
