EXPLANATORY STATEMENT


This Bill seeks to amend the Passports Act 1966 ("the Act").

2. Clause 1 deals with preliminary matters, including the Minister's
power to appoint the date for the coming into force of this Act.

3. Clause 2 seeks to amend section 1A of the Act by deleting certain
definitions and introducing certain new definitions.


-----

PASSPORTS (AMENDMENT) 11

4. Clauses 3 and 5 seek to amend sections 2(1) and 9 of the Act
respectively by substituting for the words "an alien" the words "a
non-citizen".

5. Clause 4 seeks to amend subsection 6(3) of the Act to enhance
the penalty for the failure of the master of a vessel to comply with
the Director General's order under subsection 5(1) of the Act to remove
a person entering Malaysia contrary to the Act from Malaysia.

6. Clause 6 seeks to substitute section 10 of the Act to clarify the
powers of arrest and investigation under the Act.

7. Clause 7 seeks to introduce new sections 10A, 10B, 10C, 10D, 10E,
10F and 10G to strengthen the powers of enforcement under the Act.

8. _Clause 8 seeks to amend subsection 12(1) of the Act to clarify_
the offences under the Act.

9. Clause 9 seeks to introduce new sections 12A, 12B, 12C and 12D.
Section 12A deals with offences relating to the procurement of passports,
internal travel documents, or endorsements or visas on passports, for
other persons. Section 12B deals with the offence of making a false
document to be used as a passport, whether by the person who made
it or by any other person. Section 12C empowers the Director General
of Immigration to reward persons for services rendered in connection
with the detection and prosecution of offences under the Act. Section
12D provides that the Minister may give the Director General of
Immigration directions of a general or specific nature not inconsistent
with the Act and that the Director General shall give effect to all such
directions.

_FINANCIAL IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 1894.]
