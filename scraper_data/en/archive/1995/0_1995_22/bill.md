A BILL

# intituled

An Act to amend the Town and Country Planning Act
1976.

[ ]

WHEREAS it is expedient for the purpose only of
ensuring uniformity of law and policy to amend the Town
and Country Planning Act 1976:

NOW, THEREFORE, pursuant to Clause (4) of Article
76 of the Federal Constitution BE IT ENACTED by the
## Seri Paduka Baginda Yang di-Pertuan Agong with the
advice and consent of the Dewan Negara and Dewan
Rakyat in Parliament assembled, and by the authority of
the same, as follows:

**1. (1) This Act may be cited as the Town and Country**
Planning (Amendment) Act 1995.

a date to be appointed by the State Authority, with the
concurrence of the Minister, by notification in the State
_Gazette and the State Authority may appoint different_
dates—

_(a) for the coming into operation of this Act in_
different local authority areas or parts thereof;

_(b) for the coming into operation of different_
provisions of this Act; or

_(c) for the coming into operation of different_
provisions of this Act in different local authority
areas or parts thereof.

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Town and Country Planning Act 1976
("the Act").

2. _Clause I seeks to deal with the short title and commencement_
of this Bill.

3. Clause 2 seeks to define certain new words and expressions
used in this Bill.

4. Clause 3 seeks to amend subsection 4(1) of the Act to introduce
four new members to the State Planning Committee.

5. Clause 4 seeks to amend subsection 10(5) of the Act to extend
the period for the State Planning Committee to approve a draft
structure plan from three months to six months.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to amend paragraph 12(3)(a) of the Act to provide_
for additional matters to be incorporated in the draft local plan.

7. Clause 6 seeks to introduce a new subsection (3) to section 13
to enable the extension of the period for making objections to or
representations in respect of the draft local plan.

8. Clause 8 seeks to introduce three new sections.

The new section 2l A deals with the requirement on the submission
of a development proposal report when applying for planning
permission and its particulars.

The new section 21B gives details of the layout plans. Amongst
others, the layout plans shall contain measures for the protection
of the physical environment, preservation of landscapes, natural
topography and trees, making up of open spaces and the preservation
and enhancement of the character and appearance of buildings with
special architecture or historical interest.

The new section 21c requires a qualified person to prepare all
documents, drawings, layout plans, maps, models, plans, particulars
and reports required to be submitted under the Act.

9. _Clause 9 seeks to amend section 22 of the Act to ensure that_
the local planning authority shall have regard to the development
proposal report when considering an application for planning
permission, and in granting planning permission the local planning
authority shall have the power to impose certain new conditions.

10. Clauses 10, 11, 12, 13 and 14 seek to increase the penalties
for offences under sections 26, 27, 28, 29 and 30 of the Act
respectively. Penalty of fifty thousand ringgit is increased to one
hundred thousand ringgit. Penalty of ten and twenty thousand ringgit


-----

is increased to fifty thousand ringgit. Penalty for a continuous offence
is increased from five hundred ringgit to one thousand ringgit for
every day that the offence is continued after conviction.

11. Clause 15 seeks to introduce into the Act, a new Part VA
which contains the following provisions:

The new section 35A seeks to deal with the tree preservation
order.

Under subsection (1) the local planning authority is empowered
to make a tree preservation order. Subsection (2) sets out the provisions
that may be made in a tree preservation order.

Subsection (3) deals with situations where permission to fell, cut
down, top, lop or uproot a tree is not required.

Subsection (4) provides a penalty for a contravention of this
section. Subsection (5) deals with the presumption of liability on
the occupier of the land.

The new section 35B seeks to exempt the imposition of a tree
preservation order in respect of trees already subjected to conditions
on preservation of trees under paragraphs (f) and (g) of subsection
22 (5).

The new section 35c seeks to give the right of appeal to a person
aggrieved by a tree preservation order and matters related thereto.

The new section 35D seeks to provide for compensation to be
paid in consequence of a tree preservation order. Subsection (1)
seeks to enable the owner of the land to claim compensation if he
proves that he has suffered damages in the form of depreciation in
the value of the land in consequence of the tree preservation order.

Subsection (2) deals with the time for filing such claim. If he
is dissatisfied with the amount of compensation awarded, he may
appeal to the Appeal Board under subsection (3).

The new section 35E pertains to replacement of trees.

Subsection (1) imposes a duty on the person found guilty under
subsection 35A (4) for felling, cutting down, topping, lopping,
uprooting, damaging or destroying a tree in contravention of a tree
preservation order to replace such tree.

Such tree shall be replaced within the time specified by the local
planning authority which may be extended once under subsection
(2) on application.

Subsection (3) provides for appeal.

Subsection (4) requires the application of the tree preservation
order to a tree replanted under subsection (1) as it had applied to
the original tree.

Subsection (5) contains the penalty for non-compliance of this
section.

Where the person whose duty it is to replace a tree fails to do
so, the local planning authority may proceed to replace the tree
under the new section 35F and all costs and expenses incurred thereby
by it shall be reimbursed by him.

The new section 35G seeks to confer powers on the local planning
authority to amend or revoke a tree preservation order.

The new section 35H seeks to prohibit the felling, cutting down,
topping, lopping or uprooting of a tree with a girth exceeding 0.8
metre except in respect and for the purposes of the circumstances
in paragraphs (a), (b) and (c) of subsection (1). Subsection (2)
deals with the manner for measuring the girth of a tree. Subsection
(3) contains the penalty for an offence under this section.

12. _Clause 16 seeks to amend subsection 36(10) of the Act to_
regularize the procedure and powers of the Appeal Board.

13. _Clause 17 seeks to introduce a new section 52A to provide_
that where offences are committed by a body corporate, its directors
or officers shall be deemed to be guilty of those offences as well
as the body corporate itself.

14. _Clause 18 seeks to extend the power of the State Authority_
to makes rules arising out of the matters introduced in the other
proposed amendments in this Bill. The new subsection (3) to section
58 of this Bill seeks to ensure that the entitlement of a person to
submit plans, documents, particulars and layout plans relating to
town and country planning under any other written law is not affected
although his qualification may not be prescribed under paragraph
58(2)(h) of the Act.

15. Other amendments in this Bill which have not been specifically
referred to are amendments which are minor or consequential in
nature.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 1819.]
