EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Dewan Bahasa dan Pustaka Act 1959

("the Act") and to extend the operation of the Act to the States of
Sabah and Sarawak and the Federal Territory of Labuan.

2. Clause 3 seeks to amend subsection 1(2) of the Act to provide
for the application of the Act throughout Malaysia. With this, paragraph

_(d) of clause 5 seeks to delete the definition of "West Malaysia" in_
section 2 of the Act.

3. Clause 4 seeks to make general amendment in the national
language text of the Act by deleting the word "Barat" in subsection
3(1) and by substituting the words "penjawat" and "penjawat-penjawat"

with the words "pengkhidmat" and "pengkhidmat-pengkhidmat"
respectively.

4. Clause 5 seeks to introduce the definitions of "Director General"
and "disciplinary committee". It also seeks to substitute the word
"pelajaran" with the word "pendidikan" in the definition of "Menteri".

5. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 4 of the Act relating to the_

constitution of the Board. It seeks to provide for new appointment
of members and appointment of representatives to certain members.

6. Clause 7 seeks to amend section 5 of the Act. It seeks, among
other things, to introduce two new objects of the Board namely, to
encourage the proper usage of the national language and to promote
its wider use.

7. Clause 8 seeks to amend section 6 of the Act by deleting
subsection (2).

8. Clause 9 seeks to introduce new sections 6A and 6fi. The new
section 6A seeks to provide that the Board shall be the sole co-
ordinating authority pertaining to composing, devising and standardising
of terminologies in the national language. This new role is necessary
in view of the need for uniformity in the development of the national
language. The new section 65 empowers the Board to delegate, in
writing, any of its duties or powers except the powers under sections

18A, 18B, 19 and 27 to the Director General or the officers or servants

of the Board.

9. Clause 10 seeks to substitute section 7 of the Act. The new
section 7 seeks to set out the powers of the Board.

10. Clause 11 seeks to introduce new section 7A which empowers

the Board to transfer or dispose of any of its activity or duty in
relation to the carrying out of its object under paragraph 5(iii) of the
Act to any person or body subject to such terms and conditions as
the Board may impose.

11. _Clause 12 seeks to substitute section 11 of the Act. The new_

section 11 makes provisions for the Board to appoint one or more
committees to assist it in the performance of its duties and the
exercise of its powers. It also sets out the functions of the committees.

12. Clause 13 seeks to introduce new sections 11A and 11B. The

new section 11A seeks to make provisions for the committee to
appoint one or more sub-committees to assist it in the performance
of its duties and the exercise of its powers. It also sets out the
functions of the sub-committee. The new section 11B seeks to provide
for the payment of remuneration or allowance to any person other
than a member, officer or servant of the Board who is invited to
attend any meeting of the Board, committee or sub-committee.

13. _Clause 14 seeks to add a new heading to Part III of the Act_

pertaining to discipline.

14. _Clause 15 seeks to introduce a new section 16A which seeks_

to set out the duties of the Director General.

15. _Clause 16 seeks to delete matters relating to discipline from_

the scope of section 17 of the Act as such matters are dealt with in
the proposed new sections 18A and 18B.

16. Clause 17 seeks to introduce new sections 18A and 18B. The

new section ISA establishes disciplinary committees of the Board in
respect of the Director General and its officers and servants and sets
out their duties, powers and functions. The new section 18B empowers
the Board to make disciplinary regulations.

17. _Clause 18 seeks to add a new paragraph (e) to section 22 of_

the Act to enable the Board to make loans to its officers and servants
for any purpose it may approve.

18. Clause 19 seeks to introduce new sections 24A, 24B, 24c and

24D which relate to the imposition of surcharge on any person who
is or was in the employment of the Board.

19. _Clause 20 seeks to introduce a new section 25A which seeks_

to make the members, the Director General, officers and servants of
the Board public servants within the meaning of the Penal Code.
