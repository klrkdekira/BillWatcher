EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Co-operative Societies Act 1993.

2. Clause 2 seeks to amend subsection 3(2) in consequence of the
introduction of the new sections 7lA, 71B and 71c under clause 5.

3. Clause 3 seeks to introduce a new section 18A to provide additional
powers to the Registrar-General to direct a registered society to
convene a general meeting, within a period to be specified by him
which is not less than three months from the date of the notification
given by him, for the purpose of amending any of its by-laws which
is ambiguous or doubtful in interpretation, and is likely to mislead
or conflicts with or is inconsistent with the Act or any regulations
made thereunder or if the amendment is necessary or desirable in
the interest of the registered society. In the event that the registered
society fails to comply with such directive any amendment made to
the by-laws by the Registrar-General shall be deemed to be a registered
amendment under section 18.

4. Clause 4 seeks to substitute for the words "saham perdagangan"
the words "stok perniagaan" in paragraph 59(4)(d) of the national
language text.

5. Clause 5 seeks to introduce three new sections namely, sections
7lA, 71B and71c. Section 7lA makes provision whereby a registered
society may be dissolved with the view to its re-organisation as a
company. This is available only in certain specific circumstances.

In those circumstances as provided for under subsection 7l A(l), the
Registrar-General shall notify or cause to be notified all members
of the registered society of the proposal to dissolve and re-organise
the registered society. The notification shall specify the matters
stipulated in subsection 71A(6). Upon receipt of all the reply forms
returned by the members who have been duly notified by ordinary
post or by publication in the local newspapers, as the case may be,
the Registrar-General shall, on being satisfied that three-fourths of
the members have assented to the dissolution and re-organisation of
the registered society, make an order for the revocation of the registration
of the society. The order shall be conditional upon the incorporation
of the successor company or, in the case of an existing company,
the completion of any act necessary to prepare it to undertake the
activities under paragraph 75(1)(1), and all the approvals, consents
or endorsements referred to in paragraph 71A(6)(h) having been
obtained.

The new section 71B provides for the distribution of the assets
of the registered society to any member who has not assented to the
dissolution and re-organisation of the registered society as a company.

The new section 71c is to facilitate the transfer of the assets of
the registered society to the successor company by operation of law.

6. Clauses 6 and 7 seek to make minor consequential amendments
to sections 73 and 74 necessitated by the introduction of the new
section 7lA.

7. Clause 8 seeks to make a consequential amendment to section
81 to allow an aggrieved person to appeal to the Minister in respect
of an order of the Registrar-General made under the new section 71B.

8. Clause 9 seeks to amend subsection 86(2) by introducing a new
paragraph (q) to provide for matters relating to the new sections 7lA,
7IB and 71c.

9. Clause 10 seeks to amend paragraph 91(l)(g) of the principal
Act by substituting it with a new paragraph to make it an offence
if a registered society or any person does or causes to be done any
act or thing prohibited by, or fails to comply with, the requirements
of the Act.
