EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Customs Act 1967 ("the Act").

2. _Clause 2 seeks to amend the definition of "inland clearance_
depot" by deleting the words "in transit" so as not to restrict activities
in the depot to the handling and temporary storage of goods in
transit but also to allow the clearance of goods at the depot.

3. Clause 3 seeks to amend subsection 3(3) of the Act to substitute
the word "section" with the words "sections 13B,". This is a
consequential amendment necessitated by the introduction of the
new section 13B.

4. Clause 4 seeks to introduce a new section 8A to empower the
Minister to prescribe uniforms, rank markings, authority cards and
badges for officers of customs.

CUSTOMS (AMENDMENT) 5

5. Clause 5(a) seeks to amend section 9 of the Act to require an
officer of customs to produce on demand, in addition to the authority
card, such badge as the Director General may direct to be carried
by such officer. Clause 5(b) seeks to provide for the surrender of
the badge and authority card of an officer of customs when he
ceases to be such officer of customs or when directed to do so.

6. Clause 6 seeks to introduce a new section 9A to make it an
offence for a person to unlawfully wear, use, possess or display a
prescribed uniform, badge or authority card, otherwise than in a
theatrical performance.

7. Clause 7 seeks to delete subsection 11(6). This provision is reenacted as a new section 22B vide clause 10.

8. In the process of ascertaining the customs duty leviable on
goods, the proper officer of customs has, inter alia, to determine
the class of goods to which such dutiable or uncustomed goods
belong. Clause 8 seeks to amend subsection 13(1) to enable the
proper officer of customs not only to value goods but also to classify
them.

9. Clause 9 seeks to introduce two new provisions. The proposed
section 13A seeks to enable a person who is dissatisfied with a
classification or valuation to pay the customs duty under protest.
The proposed section 13B seeks to enable the Director General to
make a decision on any questions on classification that are referred
to him.

10. Clause 10 seeks to introduce two new sections. The proposed
section 22A seeks to provide that where the Director General has
made a decision under section 13B or 22, the production of a certificate
signed by the Director General in respect of such decision shall be
admissible in evidence and shall be sufficient evidence of the facts
stated therein without the need to call the Director General to give
evidence in court.

11. Clause 11 seeks to amend section 129 to enable the Director
General to order payment to be made before goods or amounts
forfeited are delivered or refunded to the owner or person entitled
thereto.

12. _Clause 12 seeks to amend section 131 of the Act to increase_
the amount of compound that may be accepted in respect of cases
under paragraphs (a), (b), (c), (d) and (e) of subsection 135(1). The
amount of compound that may be accepted shall be a sum not less
than two times and not more than ten times the customs duty.

13. _Clause 13 seeks to amend section 138 of the Act to provide_
that any breach of the conditions and restriction of an exemption
granted constitutes an offence. The penalty has also been increased
from a fine not exceeding five thousand ringgit to a fine not exceeding
ten thousand ringgit.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial
expenditure. [PN. (U[2]) 1806.]
