uniformity of law and policy to amend the law relating to

76 of the Federal Constitution, BE IT ENACTED by the

Parliament assembled, and by the authority of the same, as

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Control of Rent Act 1966 ("the Act").

1. Clause 1 contains the short title and seeks to empower the
Minister to appoint the date of coming into force of the Act.

_2. Clause 2 seeks to amend section 1 of the Act to empower the_
State Authority to exempt any area within any local authority area
from all or any of the provisions of the Act.

3. Clause 3 seeks to introduce new sections 1A and 1B. Section 1A
seeks to provide that the Act, unless sooner repealed, shall cease to
be in operation at the expiration of five years after the coming into
force of this section. The Act is preserved within the stated period
to serve as an advanced notice of its repeal and to enable tenants of
controlled premises to obtain alternative accomodation before the repeal.
However, an earlier repeal is permissible in any State which is ready
for the cessation of the Act. Hence by section 1B the Minister is

4. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 4 to provide an additional category_
of premises which is to be exempted from the operation of the Act.

5. _Clause 5 seeks to substitute the mechanism for determining the_
fair rent of controlled premises in paragraph 5(2)(b) with a new
mechanism which is based on the expected market price of the premises.

6. Clause 6 seeks to introduce a new section 8 A to enable the landlord
of a controlled premises to vary the fair rent of the premises.

7. Clause 7 seeks to introduce new subsections 12(2A) and (3 A) and
amend subsection (4). Subsection (2A) seeks to empower the Ruler
or the Yang di-Pertua Negeri to appoint any of the persons specified
in subsection (2) to perform the functions of the Chairman of the
Rent Tribunal during the period in which the Chairman is unable to
perform his functions. By subsection (3A), read with the amendment
to subsection (4), the Ruler or the Yang di-Pertua Negeri is empowered
to appoint a member of the Rent Tribunal from amongst the valuation
officers in the public service or in the employment of any local authority.

8. _Clause 8 seeks to enlarge the powers of the Rent Tribunal under_
section 13 of the Act. By an amendment to paragraph (l) _(a), the Rent_
Tribunal is empowered to hear any application for the varying of fair
rent under the new section 8A; and by the new paragraph (l) _(g), the_
Rent Tribunal is empowered to hear and determine any application
under section 20 or 24 of the Act.

9. _Clause 9 seeks to amend section 16 by substituting paragraph_
16(1)( f ) of the Act. The new paragraph (1)(f) is essentially the existing
paragraph (1)(f) but with a deletion of subparagraph (1)(f)(i). The
paragraph deals with the recovery of possession of a controlled premises
by an owner who requires the same for occupation by himself or any
member of his family.

10. _Clause 10 seeks to amend paragraph 18 (1)(c). By substituting_
for the word "three" the word "six" appearing therein, a landlord,
in his written undertaking accompanying an application for recovery
of possession of a controlled premises for purposes of development,
is given a realistic time period from the date of possession of the
controlled premises to commence work for the purpose of putting into
effect the development.
