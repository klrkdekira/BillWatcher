EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend certain provisions of the Malaysian Timber
Industry Board (Incorporation) Act 1973 (hereinafter referred to as
the "Act").

2. Clause 2 seeks to amend section 2 of the Act by redefining certain
expressions and terms used in the Act as well as to define certain new
expressions and terms to be used in the Act. Some of the new
expressions and terms now defined are "Chairman" which is defined
to mean the chairman appointed under section 4 of the Act; "Director-
General" which is defined to mean the Director-General appointed
under section 9 of the Act and shall include the Deputy Director-
General; and the expression "legal landing place" which is defined as
to have the meaning assigned to it in the Customs Act 1967.

3. Clauses 3,4 and 6 seek to amend sections 4,5 and? respectively
of the Act which relate to the composition of the Board and the
appointment of alternate members to the Board.

4. Clause 5 introduces a new section 5A into the Act. This new
provision stipulates the tenure of a member of the Board appointed
under section 4 of the Act which is two years but shall be eligible for
reappointment.


-----

28 BILL

5. Clause 7 of the Bill seeks to amend section 8 of the Act in order
to facilitate the proper functioning of the Board by designating the
person that can chair the meeting of the Board in the circumstances
stated in that section.

6. _Clauses 8 and 9 seek to amend section 9 of the Act and to_

introduce new sections 9A and 9s respectively. These sections deal
with appointment of the Director-General, terms and conditions of

services for officers and servants of the Board and the functions and
responsibilities of the Director-General.

7. Clause 10 seeks to amend section 12 of the Act. Clause 10 (a)
introduces three new paragraphs to subsection (1) of section 12 of the
Act, expanding the functions of the Board. Clause 10 (b) adds the
words "agreements and arrangements" to enable the Board to be more
effective in discharging its function.

8. Clauses I I , 13,15,16 and 17 seek to amend sections 13, 14, 15,

11 and 18 of the Act respectively. These sections deal with registration

of persons under the Act who carry on activity as timber exporter,
grader, jetty operator, timber processor, etc. Subsection (3) of section

14 provides that a registration under this Act shall be valid for one year

or for such longer period not exceeding five years. The discretion is
with the Board. Section 16 of the Act on the other hand empowers the
Board to refuse or suspend registration. But a new subsection (5A) of
section 16 (clause 16 (e)) and section 18 allow for appeal to be made
to the Minister in the event of a refusal of registration.

9. Clause 12 introduces a new section 13A into the Act. This section
empowers the Minister to exempt any person from registration under
the Act.

10. Clauses 18 and 19 seek to amend section 19 of the Act and to

introduce a new section 20A respectively. Section 19 is amended to
clearly state some of the sources of the Fund that can be obtained by
the Board. Section 20A empowers the Board to levy fee and charges for

its technical advisory services given to the industry.

11. Clauses 20, 21 and 22 seek to amend sections 22, 23 and 24

respectively to further clarify the existing provisions.

12. Clause 23 seeks to amend section 25 so that the Statutory Bodies
(Accounts and Annual Reports) Act 1980 may be made applicable to
the Board. Clause 24 is a consequential amendment.

13. Clause 25 seeks to amend section 27 to empower the Director-

General with the consent of the Chairman of the Board to conduct
inspection at any premises, mill or yard of a timber exporter and to
commit into custody any timber which is found in the course of such

investigation connected with the commission of any offence under the
Act.

14. Clause 26 seeks to introduce several new provisions namely

sections 21 A, 27B, 27C, 27D, 2?E, 27F, 27G, 27H, 27I, 27J and 27K. These
provisions deal with the powers of the Director-General or any
officer of the Board authorized by him to inspect and examine any
legal landing place and to detain or seize any document or timber
found in the course of such inspection of or examination which is
connected with the commission of an offence under this Act.
Provisions are also made to enable the Director-General to deal with
the timber seized as he sees proper and fit, and empowers him to
examine witnesses and to record statements.

15. Clauses 27,28 and 32 seek to amend sections 28, 29, 30, 33 and

34 of the Act so as to bring them in line with other provisions of the
Act.

16. _Clauses 29 and 30 introduce new provisions namely sections_

29A, 30A and 30B respectively. These new provisions deal with
protection given to the Board, its members, officers, and servants
against any law suit arising from any act done in the course of carrying
out its or their duties, except in cases involving negligence and mala

_fide. Sections 30A and 30B deal with the power to, conduct_

prosecution, and, compound offences.

17. Clause 31 introduces new sections 33A and 33B which deal with

offences by a body of persons incorporated or otherwise, as well as the
liability of principals for the act of their agents.

18. Clause 33 seeks to introduce provisions relating to the setting up

of a disciplinary authority and power to make regulations on
discipline and power to impose surcharge. These provisions are
namely new sections 34A, 34B and 34c.

19. Clauses 34,35 and 36 seek to amend the First, Second and Third

Schedules respectively.
