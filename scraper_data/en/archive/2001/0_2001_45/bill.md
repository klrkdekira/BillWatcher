A BILL

# intituled

An Act to provide for the grant of a remembrance allowance to
the recipients of the Decoration of Panglima Gagah Berani and for
matters connected therewith.

[ ]

**ENACTED by the Parliament of Malaysia as follows:**

**Short title**

**1. This Act may be cited as the Panglima Gagah Berani**

(Remembrance Allowance) Act 2001.

**Interpretation**

**2. In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—**

"child" includes a step-child and a child adopted by the recipient

under any written law relating to adoption, or under any custom
or usage;

"Decoration" means the Decoration of Panglima Gagah Berani

conferred under the Statutes of Panglima Gagah Berani (P.G.B.)

## [G.N. 3151/60]:

"remembrance allowance" means the remembrance allowance

granted under subsection 3{1);

"spouse" means the widow or widower of a deceased recipient

whose marriage is recognized in Malaysia as a valid marriage
under any written law, religion, custom or usage;

"recipient" means a person who has been conferred the Decoration.

**Grant of remembrance allowance**

**3. (1) There may be granted to a recipient of a Decoration a**
remembrance allowance of three hundred ringgit a month, and
where the Decoration is conferred on such recipient more than
once, there may be granted to him a separate remembrance allowance
of three hundred ringgit a month in respect of each Decoration
conferred on him.

(2) The remembrance allowance shall be payable in arrear no

earlier than the end of each month.

(3) Where the Decoration was conferred before the commencement

of this Act and the recipient is living upon such commencement,
the remembrance allowance shall be paid retrospectively to the
recipient, commencing with the month in which the Decoration
was conferred on him, but not earlier, in any case, than the month
of June 1983.

(4) Where the Decoration is conferred on or after the

commencement of this Act, the remembrance allowance shall be
paid to the recipient commencing with the month in which the
Decoration is conferred on him.

**Cessation of grant of remembrance allowance**

**4. (1) The grant of a remembrance allowance shall cease—**

_(a) upon the death of the recipient;_

_(b) upon the recipient being convicted for an offence by a_

court of law in the Federation and sentenced to death, or
to imprisonment for any term otherwise than imprisonment
in lieu of a fine; or

_(c) upon the cancellation or annulment of the conferment of_

the Decoration in accordance with section VIII of the
Statutes of Panglima Gagah Berani [G.N. 3151/60].

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to provide for the grant of a remembrance allowance to the
recipients of the Decoration of Panglima Gagah Berani and for matters connected
therewith.

2. Clause 2 contains the definitions of several terms used in this Bill.

3. Clause 3 seeks to provide for the grant of a remembrance allowance of three
hundred ringgit per month to a recipient of the Decoration of Panglima Gagah
Berani. The remembrance allowance shall be paid retrospectively commencing
with the month in which the Decoration is conferred on the recipient, but not
earlier than June 1983.

4. Clause 4 seeks to provide for the cessation of the grant of remembrance
allowance and its restoration on the occurrences stipulated in that clause.

5. Clause 5 seeks to provide for the grant of a total sum of fifteen thousand
ringgit once only to the next-of-kin of a recipient where the Decoration of
Panglima Gagah Berani is conferred posthumously.

6. _Clause 6 seeks to provide for the grant of a total sum of fifteen thousand_

ringgit once only to the next-of-kin of a recipient where the Decoration of
Panglima Gagah Berani was conferred before the commencement of this Bill
and the recipient had died before such date.

7. Clause 7 empowers the Prime Minister to apportion the amounts to be paid
to the next-of-kin of a deceased recipient.

8. Clause S provides that the remembrance allowance and sums granted under
this Bill shall be charged on the Federal Consolidated Fund.

9. Clause 9 provides that the remembrance allowance and sums paid to the
next-of-kin shall not be assignable, transferable, etc.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will involve the Government in extra financial expenditure the amount
of which cannot at present be ascertained.
