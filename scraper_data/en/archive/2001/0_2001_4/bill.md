EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Excise Act 1976 ("Act 176").

2. _Clause 1 of the Bill contains the short title._

3. _Clause 3 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 53A into Act 176 to_
enable an officer of excise exercising his powers under sections 51, 52 and 53 of
Act 176 to gain access to recorded information or computerised data.

4. _Clause 4 of the Bill seeks to amend subsection 55(1) of Act 176 to allow the_
amount secured or the proceeds of sale held under that section to be paid to the
Director General if such amount or proceeds of sale are forfeited under section 69
of Act 176.

5. _Clause 5 of the Bill seeks to amend section 56 of Act 176 to provide for the_
release of a person arrested under Act 176 from custody on his depositing such
reasonable sum of money as the proper officer of excise may require or on his
executing a bond, with such surety or sureties as the proper officer of excise may
require, or both.


-----

6 _Bill_

6. Clause 6 of the Bill seeks to delete section 57 of Act 176 as the authorisation
for the conduct of prosecutions is to be dealt with under section 377 of the
Criminal Procedure Code.

7. Clause 7 of the Bill seeks to amend section 60 of Act 176 to reduce the
percentage of goods required to be examined in a proportional examination from
ten per centum to five per centum and to provide that if it is necessary to test any
goods seized under Act 176, it shall be sufficient to test only a sample not
exceeding five per centum of the goods so examined.

8. Clause 8 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 63A into Act 176 to
impose a duty to keep the name and address of an informer and the substance of
the information received from an informer secret. A person who fails to comply
with this requirement shall be guilty of an offence and shall, on conviction, be
liable to a fine not exceeding ten thousand ringgit or to imprisonment for a term
not exceeding five years or to both such fine and imprisonment.

9. _Clause 9 of the Bill seeks to amend section 69 of Act 176 to provide for the_
forfeiture of the amount secured under paragraph 55(1)(a) or 55(1 ) _(b) of Act 176_
or of the amount realised by sale under paragraph 55(l)(c) of Act 176 when an
offence is compounded.

10. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are minor or
consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
