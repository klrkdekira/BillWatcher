EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Service Tax Act 1975 ("Act 151").

2. _Clause 1 of the Bill contains the short title._

3. _Clause 2 of the Bill seeks to amend the definition of "exported taxable_
service" in subsection 2(1) of Act 151 to clarify the position in relation to the
provision of insurance policies for the coverage of the international transportation


-----

8 _Bill_

of goods and the provision of export credit insurance policies. The clause also
seeks to insert a new subsection 2(2) to clarify the position of Labuan, Langkawi,
the Joint Development Area and the free zones for the purposes of that definition.

4. _Clauses 3 and 12 of the Bill seek to amend section 10A and paragraph_
41 _(cc) of Act 151 respectively to make provision for receipts produced by_
computer.

5. _Clause 4 of the Bill seeks to amend subsection 15(3) of Act 151 to provide_
that a penalty or surcharge imposed under Act 151 shall not be subject to the
one year period of limitation prescribed under the Limitation Ordinance of
Sabah and the Limitation Ordinance of Sarawak respectively.

6. _Clause 5 of the Bill seeks to amend section 17 of Act 151 to provide that_
if a company, a firm, a society, an association or other body of persons, as the
case may be, is liable to pay service tax or penalty, then the directors of the
company, the partners of the firm or the members of the society, association
or other body of persons shall, together with the company, firm, society, association
or other body of persons, be jointly and severally liable for the service tax or
penalty payable. However, in the case of a company that is being wound up,
the directors of such company shall only be so liable where the assets of the
company are insufficient to meet the amount due, after paying any sums having
priority under the Companies Act 1965 in relation to the application of the
assets of the company in such winding up over the service tax or penalty.

7. _Clause 6 of the Bill seeks to delete subsections 24(3) and 24(4) from Act_
151 as access to recorded information and computerized data is to be dealt with
under the new section 26A.

8. _Clause 7 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 26A into Act 151 to_
enable a proper officer exercising his powers under sections 24, 25 and 26 of
Act 151 to gain access to recorded information or computerized data.

9. _Clause 8 of the Bill seeks to amend section 28 of Act 151 to provide for_
the release of a person arrested under Act 151 from custody on his depositing
such reasonable sum of money as the proper officer may require or on his
executing a bond, with such surety or sureties as the proper officer may require,
or both.

10. _Clause 10 of the Bill seeks to delete section 36 of Act 151 as the authorization_
for the conduct of prosecutions is to be dealt with under section 377 of the
Criminal Procedure Code.

11. _Clause 11 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 37A into Act 151_
to impose a duty to keep the name and address of an informer and the substance
of the information received from an informer secret. A person who contravenes
this requirement shall be guilty of an offence and shall be liable on conviction
to a fine not exceeding ten thousand ringgit or to imprisonment for a term not
exceeding five years or to both.
