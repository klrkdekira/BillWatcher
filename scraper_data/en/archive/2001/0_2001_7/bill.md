## 14 Bill

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Registration of Businesses Act 1956 ("Act 197").

2. _Clause 1 contains the short title and provisions on the commencement of_
the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to introduce various definitions including the definition of_
Companies Commission of Malaysia and to state that the Registrar is the Chief
Executive Officer of the Commission.

4. Clause 3 seeks to amend section 3 of Act 197. The new section 3 seeks
to empower the Companies Commission of Malaysia to appoint Deputy Registrars,
Assistant Registrars, inspectors and other officers for the office of the Registrar
of Businesses.

5. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 5 of Act 197 to provide for simpler_
provisions in relation to the requirements for the registration of business.

6. Clause 7 seeks to introduce new sections 5A, 5B, 5C, 5D, 5E and 5F into
Act 197.

New section 5A seeks to provide for the renewal of the registration of a
business.

New section 5B deals with changes in the particulars registered in respect
of any associate of a business or in respect of the business.

New section 5c seeks to empower the Registrar to refuse to register any
business and to revoke the registration of any business if the Registrar is
satisfied that the business is likely to be used for unlawful purposes or any
purpose prejudicial to or incompatible with the security of the Federation,
public order or morality.

New section 5D deals with the termination of a business.

New section 5 E deals with the removal of a registered business from the
register.

New section 5F provides that a certificate of registration containing certain
specific information is conclusive that the requirements of Act 197 have been
complied with and that the business is duly registered.

7. _Clauses 8 and 9 seek to amend sections 9 and 10 respectively of Act 197_
to, inter alia, so that the powers presently exercisable under those sections by
an inspector can also be exercised by the Registrar, Deputy Registrars and
Assistant Registrars.

8. _Clause 10 seeks to amend section 11 of Act 197 to enable aggrieved_
persons to appeal to the Minister against decisions of the Registrar under
section 5, 5A, 5B, 5C, 5D or 5E.

## Registration of Businesses (Amendment) 15

9. Clause 11 seeks to introduce a new section 1 1 A into Act 197 to require
the display of the certificate of registration. Currently, this requirement is
found in section 5, which contains provisions on registration.

10. _Clause 12 seeks to amend section 12 of Act 197 to increase the penalties_
for offences in subsections 12(1) and (2). In subsection 12(1) of Act 197 the
maximum term of imprisonment is increased to two years from the exisiting
six months whilst subsection 12(2) imposes a term of imprisonment not exceeding
six months. The fines are increased to fifty thousand ringgit and two thousand
ringgit from the existing one thousand ringgit and two hundred ringgit,
respectively.

11. _Clause 13 seeks to introduce a new section 12A into Act 197. The new_
section provides for a general penalty for offences under the Act or rules made
under the Act for which no penalty is expressly provided for.

12. _Clause 14 seeks to amend section 13 of the Act to provide that prosecution_
for offences under the Act may only be instituted by or with the consent in
writing of the Public Prosecutor.

13. _Clause 15 seeks to amend section 14 of Act 197 which deals with the_
compounding of offences, so that the procedure is similar to that provided for
in more recent laws.

14. Clause 17 seeks to introduce a new section 18A into Act 197. The new
section deals with the service of notices, certificates or order documents required
to be given or served under Act 197.

15. Clause 18 seeks to amend section 19 of Act 197 to allow any person
to obtain a copy of a certificate of registration.

16. _Clause 20 seeks to introduce a new section 22A into Act 197 to enable_
documents required under Act 197 to be electronically filed or lodged with
the Registrar.

17. _Clause 21 deals with transitional matters relating to certificates of registration_
and appeals from the decision of the Registrar.

18. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are minor
or consequential in nature.
