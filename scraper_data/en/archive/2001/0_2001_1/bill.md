EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Customs Act 1967 ("Act 235").

2. _Clause I of the Bill contains the short title._

3. _Clause 2 of the Bill seeks to amend section 19 of Act 235 to provide that_
for the purpose of calculating the customs duty payable, the valuation applicable
to any goods shall be according to the World Trade Organization valuation
system to be prescribed by regulations made under Act 235.

4. _Clause 3 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 22c into Act 235 to_
provide that if a company, a firm, a society, an association or other body of
persons, as the case may be, is liable to pay customs duty, then the directors
of the company, the partners of the firm or the members of the society, association
or other body of persons shall, together with the company, firm, society, association
or other body of persons, be jointly and severally liable for the customs duty
payable. However, in the case of a company that is being wound up, the
directors of such company shall only be so liable where the assets of the
company are insufficient to meet the amount due, after paying any sums having
priority over the customs duty under the Companies Act 1965 in relation to the
application of the assets of the company in such winding up.

5. _Clause 6 of the Bill seeks to introduce new sections 106A and 106B into_
Act 235. Section 106A seeks to empower a senior officer of customs to enter
places or premises while section 106B seeks to empower a proper officer of
customs to carry out an inspection and to investigate the commission of any
offence under Act 235.

6. Clause 7 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 111B into Act 235
to enable an officer of customs exercising his powers under sections 106A, 107,
108, 109 and 111 of Act 235 to gain access to recorded information or computerized
data.

7. _Clause 9 of the Bill seeks to amend subsection 115( 1) of Act 235 to allow_
the amount secured or the proceeds of sale held under that section to be paid
to the Director General if such amount or proceeds of sale are forfeited under
section 131 of Act 235.

8. _Clause 10 of the Bill seeks to amend section 116 of Act 235 to provide_
for the release of a person arrested under Act 235 from custody on his depositing
such reasonable sum of money as the proper officer of customs may require
or on his executing a bond, with such surety or sureties as the proper officer
of customs may require, or both.

9. Clause 11 of the Bill seeks to introduce new sections 116A, 116B, 116c and
116D into Act 235. Section 116A seeks to empower a senior officer of customs
to examine persons while section 116B seeks to provide for the admissibility


-----

18 _Bill_

of statements in a trial or inquiry by a court into an offence under Act 235.
Section 116c seeks to provide the procedure where an investigation cannot be
completed within twenty-four hours while section 116D seeks to make provision
for a diary of proceedings in an investigation.

10. Clause 12 of the Bill seeks to delete section 117 of Act 235 as the authorization
for the conduct of prosecutions is to be dealt with under section 377 of the
Criminal Procedure Code.

11. Clause 13 of the Bill seeks to amend section 120 of Act 235 to reduce the
percentage of goods required to be examined in a proportional examination
from ten per centum to five per centum and to provide that if it is necessary
to test any goods seized under Act 235, it shall be sufficient to test only a
sample not exceeding five per centum of the goods so examined.

12. Clause 14 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 124A into Act 235
to impose a duty to keep the name and address of an informer and the substance
of the information received from an informer secret. A person who contravenes
this requirement shall be guilty of an offence and shall, on conviction, be liable
to a fine not exceeding ten thousand ringgit or to imprisonment for a term not
exceeding five years or to both.

13. Clause 15 of the Bill seeks to amend section 131 of Act 235 to provide
for the forfeiture of the amount secured under paragraph 115(l)(a) or 115(l) _(b)_
of Act 235 or of the amount realized by sale under paragraph 1 1 ( 1 ) ( c ) of Act
235 when an offence is compounded. This clause also seeks to provide that the
powers of the Director General to compound offences under subsection 131 (2A)
of Act 235 shall only be exercised by the Director General himself or any other
senior officer of customs not below the rank of Senior Assistant Director of
Customs and Excise.

14. Clauses 16 to 19 of the Bill seek to amend sections 133, 135, 136 and 138
of Act 235 to enhance the penalties for certain offences under Act 235.

15. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are minor
or consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
