## New section 39A

**6. The principal Act is amended by inserting after section 39 the**
following section:

**"Access to recorded information or computerized data**

39A. (1) Any senior officer of sales tax exercising his powers
under sections 36, 37, 38 and 39 shall be given access to any
recorded information or computerized data, whether stored in
a computer or otherwise.

(2) In addition, an officer of sales tax exercising his powers
under sections 36, 37, 38 and 39—

_(a) may inspect and check the operation of any computer_
and any associated apparatus or material which he has
reasonable cause to suspect is or has been used in
connection with that information or data; and

_(b) may require—_

(i) the person by whom or on whose behalf the officer
of sales tax has reasonable cause to suspect the
computer is or has been so used; or

(ii) the person having charge of, or is otherwise
concerned with, the operation of the computer,
apparatus or material,

to provide him with such reasonable assistance as he
may require for the purposes of this section.

(3) For the purposes of subsection (1), "access" includes
being provided with the necessary password, encryption code,
decryption code, software or hardware and any other means
required to enable comprehension of recorded information or
computerized data.".

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Sales Tax Act 1972 ("Act 64").

2. _Clause 1 of the Bill contains the short title._

3. _Clause 3 of the Bill seeks to amend subsection 23(3) of Act 64 to provide_
that a penalty or surcharge imposed under Act 64 shall not be subject to the
one year period of limitation prescribed under the Limitation Ordinance of
Sabah and the Limitation Ordinance of Sarawak respectively.

4. _Clause 4 of the Bill seeks to amend section 26 of Act 64 to provide that_
if a company, a firm, a society, an association or other body of persons, as the
case may be, is liable to pay the sales tax, penalty or surcharge, then the
directors of the company, the partners of the firm or other body of persons shall,
together with the company, firm, society, association or other body of persons,
be jointly and severally liable for the sales tax, penalty or surcharge payable.
However, in the case of a company that is being wound up, the directors of such
company shall only be so liable where the assets of the company are insufficient
to meet the amount due, after paying any sums having priority under the
Companies Act 1965 in relation to the application of the assets of the company
in such winding up over the sales tax, penalty or surcharge.

5. _Clause 5 of the Bill seeks to delete subsections 36(2A) and 36(2B) from Act_
64 as access to recorded information and computerized data is to be dealt with
under the new section 39A.

6. _Clause 6 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 39A into Act 64 to_
enable the senior officer of sales tax exercising his powers under sections 36,
37, 38 and 39 of Act 64 to gam access to recorded information or computerized
data.


-----

_Sales Tax (Amendment)_ _7_

7. _Clause 7 of the Bill seeks to amend section 42 of Act 64 to provide for_
the release of a person arrested under Act 64 from custody on his depositing
such reasonable sum of money as the proper officer may require or on his
executing a bond, with such surety or sureties as the proper officer may require,
or both.

8. _Clause 9 of the Bill seeks to delete section 46 of Act 64 as the authorization_
for the conduct of prosecutions is to be dealt with under section 377 of the
Criminal Procedure Code.

9. _Clause 10 of the Bill seeks to amend section 48 of Act 64 to reduce the_
percentage of goods required to be examined in a proportional examination
from ten per centum to five per centum and to provide that if it is necessary
to test any goods seized under Act 64, it shall be sufficient to test only a sample
not exceeding five per centum of the goods so examined.

10. _Clause 11 of the Bill seeks to introduce a new section 5l_ A into Act 64
to impose a duty to keep the name and address of an informer and the substance
of the information received from an informer secret. A person who fails to
comply with this requirement shall be guilty of an offence and shall be liable
to a fine not exceeding ten thousand ringgit or to imprisonment for a term not
exceeding five years or to both.

11. Other amendments not specifically dealt with in this Statement are minor
or consequential in nature.

_FINANCIAL_ _IMPLICATIONS_

This Bill will not involve the Government in any extra financial expenditure.
