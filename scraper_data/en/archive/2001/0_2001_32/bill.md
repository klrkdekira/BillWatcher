EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Income Tax Act 1967, the Petroleum (Income Tax)
Act 1967 and the Stamp Act 1949.

CHAPTER II

AMENDMENTS TO THE INCOME TAX ACT 1967

This Bill seeks to amend the Income Tax Act 1967 ("Act 53").

2. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 34 of Act 53. A new paragraph 34(6)(n)_
is introduced to allow as a deduction from a person's gross income expenditure
incurred by that person on the provision of practical training in Malaysia to an
individual. The practical training must be in relation to that person's business
and the individual who is given the practical training must be a resident in the
basis year for a year of assessment and should also not be an employee of that
person.

This amendment will have effect for the year of assessment 2002 and subsequent
years of assessment.

3. _Clause 5 seeks to delete paragraph 39(l)(h) of Act 53. With the deletion_
of paragraph 39(l)(h) any bonus payment made to an employee is now fully
deductible.

_Clause 5 further seeks to amend paragraph 39( 1 )(k). The proposed amendment_
provides that the limit on deduction in respect of the rentals paid on a motor
vehicle is increased from fifty thousand ringgit to one hundred thousand ringgit
if the motor vehicle has not been used for any purpose prior to the rental and
the total cost of the vehicle is not more than one hundred and fifty thousand
ringgit.

This amendment will have effect for the year of assessment 2002 and subsequent
years of assessment.


-----

_Finance_ 19

4. Clause 6 seeks to amend subsection 75( 1) of Act 53 to exclude the application
of any other written law upon a person or persons who are responsible for
performing on behalf of a company or a body of persons its responsibility for
doing all acts or things, including the payment of tax, which are required to
be done for the purposes of Act 53.

5. Clause 7 seeks to amend the rate of tax for individuals in Part I of Schedule
I to Act 53. The existing rates for resident individuals range from 0 to
29 per cent. The proposed rates range from 0 to 28 per cent. This clause further
seeks to increase the threshold of chargeable income taxed at the highest rate
of 28 per cent, from chargeable income exceeding RM 150,000 to chargeable
income exceeding RM250,000.

These amendments will have effect for the year of assessment 2002 and
subsequent years of assessment.

6. Clause 8 seeks to amend Schedule 3 to Act 53. A new paragraph 3A is
introduced to provide that where a building is purchased as an industrial building
the purchaser shall be deemed to have constructed that building and to have
incurred capital expenditure on its construction.

This clause further seeks to provide that where a building is deemed under
the new paragraph 3A to have been constructed by a purchaser, the capital
expenditure incurred by the person who constructed the building and the date
of its construction shall be disregarded for the purposes of Schedule 3 to
Act 53.

This clause further seeks to introduce new paragraphs 37F, 37c and 37H into
Schedule 3 to Act 53. The new paragraphs extend the application of provisions
relating to industrial building, to a building or part of that building which is
used solely by a person for the purpose of an hotel, airport and motor racing
circuit.

The amendments in clause 8, other than paragraphs 37c and 37H, will have
effect for the year of assessment 2002 and subsequent years of assessment.

7. Clause 9 seeks to introduce a special provision into Act 53 to provide that
where a person has been enjoying annual allowance in respect of a purchased
building and the allowance which has been calculated based on a permitted
fraction under the previous paragraph 17 of Schedule 3 to Act 53 is higher than
three-hundredth of the qualifying expenditure, that allowance for a year
of assessment shall be calculated based on the higher fraction but it shall
not exceed the amount of residual expenditure at the end of the basis period
for that year of assessment.


-----

**20** **_Bill_**

8. Clause 10 seeks to introduce a new paragraph 1B into Schedule 7 A to Act
53. The new paragraph 1B is an anti avoidance provision to prevent a company
from taking undue advantage of a claim for reinvestment allowance.

These amendments will have effect for the year of assessment 2002 and
subsequent years of assessment.

_Clause 10 further seeks to extend the period that a company is given reinvestment_
allowance from five to fifteen consecutive years of assessment beginning from
the year of assessment for the basis period in which the capital expenditure was
first incurred.

This amendment will have effect for the year of assessment 1998 and subsequent
years of assessment.

CHAPTER IV

AMENDMENTS TO THE STAMP ACT 1949

This Bill seeks to amend the Stamp Act 1949 ("Act 378").

13. _Clause 17 seeks to amend subsection 9(3) of Act 378 to empower the_
Collector of Stamp Duty to inspect any stocks of unstamped cheques, contract
notes or policies of insurance held by a banker, stockbroker or insurer and any
books kept by him in connection with the issue of the cheques, contract notes
or policies of insurance so as to be consistent with the existing power of the
Collector under subsection 9(1) of Act 378.

This amendment will have effect from 1 January 2002 and subsequent years
of assessment.

14. _Clause 18 seeks to introduce a new section 38A into Act 378 to provide_
a procedure for any person who is dissatisfied with an assessment made by the
Collector to object to the assessment by written notice within thirty days after
the date of the assessment or such further period as the Collector may allow
and to apply to the Collector to review the assessment.

This amendment will have effect from 1 January 2002 and subsequent years
of assessment.

15. _Clause 19 seeks to amend section 39 of Act 378. With this amendment_
any person who is dissatisfied with the review decision of the Collector under
section 38A of Act 378, may within twenty-one days after the person is informed
of that decision and upon payment of duty, appeal in writing against the decision
to the High Court.

This amendment will have effect from 1 January 2002 and subsequent years
of assessment.

16. _Clause 20 seeks to amend subsection 47A(2) of Act 378 to empower the_
Collector to reduce or remit any penalty or the further amount payable under
paragraph 9(l)(c) of Act 378.

This amendment will have effect from 1 January 2002 and subsequent years
of assessment.

17. Clause 21 seeks to amend the First Schedule of Act 378. Item 27(d) and
32(c) is amended in order to be consistent with the current situation as the
activities of banks, merchant banks and finance companies are regulated under
the Banking and Financial Institutions Act 1989 [Act 372] and the Islamic
Banking Act 1983 [Act 276].

This amendment will have effect from 1 January 2002 and subsequent years
of assessment.
