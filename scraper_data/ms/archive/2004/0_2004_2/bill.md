**D.R. 3/2004**

RANG UNDANG-UNDANG

_b e r n a m a_

Suatu Akta untuk meminda Akta Komunikasi dan Multimedia
1998.

**DIPERBUAT oleh Parlimen Malaysia seperti yang berikut:**

**Tajuk ringkas dan permulaan kuat kuasa**

**1. ( 1 ) Akta ini bolehlah dinamakan Akta Komunikasi dan**
Multimedia (Pindaan) 2004.

(2) Akta ini mula berkuat kuasa pada tarikh yang ditetapkan

oleh Menteri melalui pemberitahuan dalam _Warta,_ dan Menteri
boleh menetapkan tarikh yang berlainan bagi permulaan kuat kuasa
peruntukan yang berlainan dalam Akta ini.

**Pindaan seksyen 4**

**2.** Akta Komunikasi dan Multimedia 1998 [Akta 588], yang disebut
"Akta ibu" dalam Akta ini, dipinda dalam perenggan 4(2)(b) dengan
memotong perkataan "atau akan memberikan".

26A. **(1) Menteri hendaklah menamakan seorang pegawai awam**

(2) Menteri boleh menamakan apa-apa bilangan pegawai

**26B.** (1) Kecuali bagi Bahagian ini atau bagi maksud apaapa prosiding sivil atau jenayah di bawah mana-mana undangundang bertulis, atau jika dibenarkan oleh Menteri —

_(a)_ Pengerusi, anggota Tribunal Rayuan atau mana-mana

pegawai Tribunal Rayuan tidak boleh, semasa dia
berkhidmat sebagai Pengerusi, anggota atau pegawai

cara, mempunyai akses kepada apa-apa maklumat

Rayuan boleh mendedahkan maklumat atau dokumen
itu.

(2) Seseorang yang melanggar subseksyen (1) melakukan

suatu kesalahan dan apabila disabitkan, boleh didenda tidak

8 _Bill_

EXPLANATORY STATEMENT

This Bill seeks to amend the Communications and Multimedia Act 1998
("Act 588").

_2._ _Clause 1 contains the short title and provisions on the commencement of_
the proposed Act.

3. _Clause 2 seeks to amend paragraph 4(2)(b) of Act 588 to clarify that the_

application of Act 588 does not cover prospective facilities or service providers.

4. _Clause 3 seeks to amend the definition of "network facilities provider" in_

section 6 of Act 588 to be in line with section 126 of the Act.

5. _Clause 4 seeks to amend section 16 of Act 588 so that regulations made_

by the Minister can provide for additional penalty if an offence is continued
after conviction.

6. _Clause 5 seeks to introduce a new section 23A into Act 588 to facilitate_

the enforcement of decisions of the Appeal Tribunal.

7. _Clause 6 seeks to amend section 24 of Act 588 to remove the requirement_

of a determination by the Minister under Chapter 2 of Part II of the Act in
respect of Appeal Tribunal procedures.

8. _Clause 7 seeks to introduce a new section 24A into Act 588 which sets out_

the powers of the Appeal Tribunal in performing its functions under the Act.

9. _Clause 8 seeks to introduce new sections 26A,_ 26B, 26C, 26D and 26E into

Act 588. The proposed section 26A seeks to empower the Minister to designate
a public officer as the Secretary of the Appeal Tribunal. He is also empowered
to designate other public officers to assist the Secretary. The proposed section
26B seeks to impose an obligation of secrecy on the Chairman, members and
officers of the Appeal Tribunal. The proposed section 26c deems the Chairman,
members and officers of the Appeal Tribunal to be public servants and public
officers for the purposes of the Penal Code and Criminal Procedure Code,
respectively. The proposed section 26D extends the protection in the Public
Authorities Protection Act 1948 to the Appeal Tribunal, the Chairman and
members and officers of the Appeal Tribunal in respect of any action, suit,
prosecution or proceedings against them for any act, neglect or default done
or committed in carrying out their respective duties under Act 588. The proposed
section 26E seeks to provide protection for the Appeal Tribunal, the Chairman,
officers of the Appeal Tribunal and other persons authorized to act for and on
behalf of the Appeal Tribunal against any action or suit for any act or omission
done in good faith.

10. _Clauses 9 and 10 seek to amend subsections 30(9) and 34(2) of Act 588_

respectively so that the Minister's decision under those subsections will not
have to go through the process of "determination" under Chapter 2 of Part II
of the Act.
